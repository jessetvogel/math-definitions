An abelian category is a category \(\mathcal{A}\) such that
  • every \(\Hom(M, N)\) is an abelian group, and composition is bilinear,
  • there is an object \(0\) which is both terminal and initial,
  • for all \(M, N\) in \(\mathcal{A}\), the direct product \(M \times N\) and direct sum \(M \oplus N\) exist and are isomorphic,
  • all kernels and cokernels exist,
  • every monomoprhism is the kernel of some morphism, and every epimoprhism is the cokernel of some morphism.
Examples include
  • the category of abelian groups
  • the category of left modules over a ring \(R\)
  • the category of sheaves on a topological space
