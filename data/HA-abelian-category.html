An abelian category is an additive category category \(\mathcal{A}\) in which
  • all kernels and cokernels exist,
  • every monomoprhism is the kernel of some morphism, and every epimoprhism is the cokernel of some morphism.

Examples include
  • the category of abelian groups
  • the category of left modules over a ring \(R\)
  • the category of sheaves on a topological space
