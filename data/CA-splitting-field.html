Let \(k\) be a field, and let \(f \in k[x]\) be a non-constant polynomial. A splitting field \(\ell\) of \(f\) over \(k\) is a field extension such that
  • \(f\) splits in \(\ell[x]\) as a product of linear factors,
  • if \(\alpha_1, \ldots, \alpha_s\) are the zeros of \(f\) in \(\ell\), then \(\ell = k(\alpha_1, \ldots, \alpha_s)\).
A splitting field always exists, and it is unique up to unique isomorphism over \(k\).
