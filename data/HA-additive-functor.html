Let \(\mathcal{A}\) and \(\mathcal{B}\) be abelian categories. A functor \(F : \mathcal{A} \to \mathcal{B}\) is called additive if for all \(M, N\) in \(\mathcal{A}\) the map \[ \Hom_\mathcal{A}(M, N) \to \Hom_\mathcal{B}(FM, FN) \] is a group morphism. It follows that an additive functor preserves finite direct sums and sends \(0\) to \(0\).
