Let \(k\) be a field of characteristic \(p > 0\). The map \(F : k \to k : x \mapsto x^p\) is a homomorphism (use binomial theorem) called the Frobenius morphism.
Note that \(F\) is injective as it is a field morphism. So when \(k\) is finite, \(F\) will also be surjective, and hence an automorphism of \(k\).
