Let \(f, g : X \to Y\) be morphisms in a category \(\mathcal{C}\). An object \(E\) of \(\mathcal{C}\) together with morphism \(e : E \to X\) is said to be an equalizer of the pair \(f, g\) if \(fe = ge\) and for any other such \(e' : E' \to X\) there exists a unique morphism \(h : E' \to E\) such that \(e' = eh\).
