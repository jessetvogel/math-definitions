Let \(f, g : X \to Y\) be morphisms in a category \(\mathcal{C}\). An object \(Q\) of \(\mathcal{C}\) together with morphism \(q : Y \to Q\) is said to be a coequalizer of the pair \(f, g\) if \(qf = qg\) and for any other such \(q' : Y \to Q'\) there exists a unique morphism \(h : Q \to Q'\) such that \(q' = hq\).
