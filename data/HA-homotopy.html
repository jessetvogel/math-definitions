Let \(\mathcal{A}\) be an abelian category, and let \(f, g : M^\bdot \to N^\bdot\) be morphisms of complexes in \(\mathcal{A}\). A homotopy from \(f\) to \(g\) is a collection of morphisms \(k^i : M^i \to N^{i - 1}\) such that \(f - g = dk + kd\). If a homotopy exists from \(f\) to \(g\), we say that \(f\) and \(g\) are homotopic. This gives an equivalence relation on the set of morphisms from \(M^\bdot\) to \(N^\bdot\).
