A Hermitian matrix \(A\) always has real eigenvalues. Namely, if \(Av = \lambda v\), then \[ \lambda \norm{v} = v^H A v = v^H A^H v = (A v)^H v = \overline{\lambda} v^H v = \overline{\lambda} \norm{v} , \] which shows \(\lambda\) is real.
Moreover, if \(\lambda\) and \(\mu\) are distinct eigenvalues, with respective eigenvectors \(v\) and \(w\), then \(v\) and \(w\) are orthogonal. Namely, \[ \mu \langle v^H, w \rangle = \langle v^H, A w \rangle = \langle v^H A^H, w \rangle = \lambda \rangle v^H, w \rangle , \] which shows \(\langle v^H, w \rangle = 0\) as \(\lambda - \mu \ne 0\).
