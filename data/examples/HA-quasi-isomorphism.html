Quasi-isomorphisms are generally not invertible. For example, the morphism of complexes is a quasi-isomorphism, but not invertible.
Also there are complexes with isomorphic homology, but which are not quasi-isomorphic. For example, \[ 0 \to \CC[x, y] \oplus \CC[x, y] \overset{\varphi}{\longrightarrow} \CC[x, y] \to 0 \qquad \text{ and } \qquad 0 \to \CC[x, y] \overset{0}{\longrightarrow} \CC \to 0 \] with \(\varphi(f, g) = xf + yg\).
