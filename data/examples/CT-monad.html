Any adjunction \(F : \mathcal{C} \to \mathcal{D}\) and \(G : \mathcal{D} \to \mathcal{C}\) gives rise to a monad with \(T = GF\). The map \(\eta : \id_{\mathcal{C}} \to T\) is the unit of the adjunction, and the map \(\mu : T^2 \to T\) is \(G \varepsilon F\), where \(\varepsilon : FG \to \id_{\mathcal{D}}\) is the counit of the adjunction.
