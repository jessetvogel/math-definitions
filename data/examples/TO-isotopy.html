Every isotopy is a homotopy, but not conversely. Namely, let \(X = Y = [0, 1]\) and \(f, g : X \to Y\) given by \(f(x) = x\) and \(g(x) = 1 - x\). Then there is a homotopy from \(f\) to \(g\) given by \[ H(x, t) = (1 - t) x - t (1 - x) . \] However, no isotopy from \(f\) to \(g\) can exist, since for any homotopy from \(f\) to \(g\) the endpoints must cross for some \(t\).
