• Any representation of \(G = \textup{GL}_1(\CC) = \CC^*\) is a direct sum of \(1\)-dimensional representations of the form \(\rho_n : \CC^* \to \CC^*, z \mapsto z^n\) for some character \(n \in \ZZ\). Hence the representation ring of \(G\) is \(R_\CC(G) = \ZZ[t, t^{-1}]\), where \(t\) corresponds to \(\rho_1\).
  • Any representation of the cyclic group \(G = \ZZ/n\ZZ\) is a direct sum of \(1\)-dimensional representations which sends \(1 \textup{ mod } n\) to an \(n\)-th root of unity. Hence the (complex) representation ring of \(G\) is \(R_\CC(G) = \ZZ[t]/(t^n - 1)\), where \(t\) corresponds to the representation \(1 \mapsto \zeta_n\), a primitive root of unity.
  • The complex irreducible representations of a product of finite groups \(G \times H\) are given by the tensor products \(\rho \otimes \tau\), where \(\rho\) and \(\tau\) are irreducible representations of \(G\) and \(H\), respectively. Therefore, there is a natural isomorphism \(\textup{Rep}_\CC(G) \otimes \textup{Rep}_\CC(H) \simeq \textup{Rep}_\CC(G \times H)\).
