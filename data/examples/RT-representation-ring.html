• Any representation of \(G = \text{GL}_1(\CC) = \CC^*\) is a direct sum of \(1\)-dimensional representations of the form \(\rho_n : \CC^* \to \CC^*, z \mapsto z^n\) for some character \(n \in \ZZ\). Hence the representation ring of \(G\) is \(R_\CC(G) = \ZZ[t, t^{-1}]\), where \(t\) corresponds to \(\rho_1\).
  • Any representation of the cyclic group \(G = \ZZ/n\ZZ\) is a direct sum of \(1\)-dimensional representations which sends \(1 \text{ mod } n\) to an \(n\)-th root of unity. Hence the (complex) representation ring of \(G\) is \(R_\CC(G) = \ZZ[t]/(t^n - 1)\), where \(t\) corresponds to the representation \(1 \mapsto \zeta_n\), a primitive root of unity.
