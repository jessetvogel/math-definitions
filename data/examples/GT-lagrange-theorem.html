The left cosets of \(H\) form a partition of \(G\). Namely, if two cosets \(g_1 H\) and \(g_2 H\) intersect, that is, \(g_1 h_1 = g_2 h_2\) for some \(h_1, h_2 \in H\), then \(g_1 H = g_2 H\) as \(g_1 h = g_2 h_2 h_1^{-1} h \in g_2 H\) and \(g_2 h = g_1 h_1 h_2^{-1} h \in g_1 H\) for any \(h \in H\). Furthermore, all cosets \(gH\) are in bijection with \(H\) via \(H \to gH, h \mapsto g h\) with inverse \(x \mapsto g^{-1} x\). Therefore, after choosing a set \(A\) of representatives for the cosets, we obtain a bijection of sets \[ A \times H \to G, \quad (g, h) \mapsto gh , \] with \(|A| = [G : H]\), which proves the theorem.
