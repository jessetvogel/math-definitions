The following are examples of seminorms which are not a norm.
  • The constant zero map on \(V\) is a seminorm.
  • On \(V = \RR^2\), the map \((x, y) \mapsto x^2\) is a semi-norm.
  • For any linear map \(f : V \to \RR\), the map \(v \mapsto |f(v)|\) is a seminorm.
Any seminorm \(\norm{\cdot}\) is non-negative, that is, \(\norm{v} \ge 0\) for all \(v \in V\). Namely, from absolute homogenity follows that \(\norm{0} = 0\), and then \[ 0 = \norm{0} \le \norm{v} + \norm{-v} = 2 \cdot \norm{v} . \]
