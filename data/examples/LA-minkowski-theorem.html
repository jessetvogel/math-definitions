The given bound is sharp. Namely, consider \(L = \ZZ^n\) with \(\det(L) = 1\), and \(S\) the interior of \([-1, 1]^n\), whose volume is \(2^n\). Then \(S\) does not contain any non-zero lattice points.
Proof. By assumption, the set \(\tfrac{1}{2} S = \{ \tfrac{1}{2} x : x \in S \}\) has volume \(\textup{vol}(\tfrac{1}{2} S) = 2^{-n} \textup{vol}(S) > \textup{vol}(\RR^n/L)\), so the map \(\tfrac{1}{2} S \to \RR^n / L\) cannot be injective. Hence, there are distinct points \(x_1, x_2 \in S\) such that \(\tfrac{1}{2} x_1 - \tfrac{1}{2} x_2 \in L\). Since \(-x_2 \in S\) and \(S\) is convex, the linear combination \(\tfrac{1}{2} x_1 - \tfrac{1}{2} x_2\) is a non-zero lattice point in \(S\).
