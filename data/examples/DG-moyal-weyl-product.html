The center of \(\Gamma(W_\hbar)\) with respect to the Weyl product is \(C^\infty(M)\llbracket \hbar \rrbracket\). Namely, if \(a\) is in the center of \(\Gamma(W_\hbar)\) and \(b = y^k\) for some \(k\), then \[ a \circ b = a y^k - \frac{i\hbar}{2} \omega^{ik} \frac{\partial a}{\partial y^i} \qquad \text{ and } \qquad b \circ a = a y^k - \frac{i\hbar}{2} \omega^{kj} \frac{\partial a}{\partial y^j} , \] so \[ 0 = a \circ b - b \circ a = - i \hbar \omega^{ik} \frac{\partial a}{\partial y^i} . \] Varying \(k\), we find that \(\frac{\partial a}{\partial y^i} = 0\) for all \(i\), so \(a \in C^\infty(M)\llbracket \hbar \rrbracket\). The converse inclusion is straightforward.
