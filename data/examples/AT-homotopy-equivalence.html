The \(n\)-sphere \(S^n \subset \RR^{n + 1}\) is homotopic to \(\RR^{n + 1} \setminus \{ 0 \}\). Namely, consider the inclusion \(i : S^n \to \RR^{n + 1}\) and the map \[ f : \RR^{n + 1} \setminus \{ 0 \} \to S^n, \quad x \mapsto \frac{x}{\|x\|} . \] Then \(f \circ i = \id_{S^n}\), and \(i \circ f\) is homotopic to \(\id_{\RR^{n + 1}}\) since \[ H : \RR^{n + 1} \times [0, 1] \to \RR^{n + 1}, \quad (x, t) \mapsto (1 - t) x + \frac{tx}{\|x\|} \] satisfies \(H(-, 0) = i \circ f\) and \(H(-, 1) = \id_{\RR^{n + 1}}\).
