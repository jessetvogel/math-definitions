The path-ordered exponential is the unique solution to the initial value problem of finding an \(f : \RR \to A\) satisfying \[ \frac{d}{dt} f(t) = a(t) f(t) \quad \textup{ with } \quad f(0) = 1 . \]
When \(A\) is commutative or \(a\) is constant, the path-ordered exponential reduces to the ordinary exponential \[ \textup{P} \exp[a](t) = \exp \left( \int_0^t a(s) ds \right) . \]
