Let \(V\) be a finite-dimensional vector space and \(f : V \to V\) a linear map, whose characteristic polynomial splits into linear factors \[ p_f(x) = (x - \lambda_1)^{m_1} \cdots (x - \lambda_k)^{m_k} , \] with \(\lambda_i\) distinct. Then there exists a basis for \(V\) such that the matrix representing \(f\) is in Jordan normal form.
