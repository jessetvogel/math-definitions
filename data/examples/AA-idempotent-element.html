The idempotent elements of a commutative ring \(R\) are in bijection with the decompositions of \(R\) as a product \(R = R_1 \times R_2\). Indeed, every such decomposition yields the idempotent element \((1, 0)\). Conversely, for every idempotent \(e \in R\), we have that \(Re\) and \(R(1 - e)\) are subrings of \(R\), and we have an isomorphism \[ \begin{aligned} R &\isom Re \times R(1 - e) \\ r &\mapsto (re, r - re) \end{aligned} \] whose inverse is \((a, b) \mapsto a + b\). Clearly these two constructions are inverse to each other.
