In the category of topological spaces with the natural model structure, a path object of a topological space \(X\) is the mapping space \(\textup{Map}([0, 1], X)\) with the factorization \[ X \xrightarrow{\textup{const}} \textup{Map}([0, 1], X) \xrightarrow{(d_0, d_1)} X \times X , \] where \(\textup{const}(x)\) is the constant path at \(x\), and \(d_0(\gamma) = \gamma(0)\) and \(d_1(\gamma) = \gamma(1)\). Indeed \(\textup{const}\) is a weak homotopy equivalence.
