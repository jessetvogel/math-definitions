Given functors \(F, G : \mathcal{C} \to \mathcal{D}\), form the functor \[ S = \Hom_\mathcal{D}(F(-), G(-)) : \mathcal{C}^\textup{op} \times \mathcal{C} \to \textbf{Set} . \] The end of \(S\) is the set of natural transformations \(F \Rightarrow G\), \[ \int_c \Hom_\mathcal{D}(F(c), G(c)) \simeq \textup{Nat}(F, G) . \] The components of any \(\mu \in \int_c \Hom_\mathcal{D}(F(c), G(c))\) can be obtained via the projections \(\pi_c\). The wedge condition precisely reduces to the naturality condition.
Let \(T : \Delta^\textup{op} \to \textbf{Set}\) be a simplicial set and let \(\gamma : \Delta \to \textbf{Top}\) be the functor sending \([n]\) to the standard \(n\)-simplex in \(\RR^{n + 1}\). Then form the functor \[ S : \Delta^\textup{op} \times \Delta \to \textbf{Top}, \quad ([m], [n]) \mapsto T([m]) \times \gamma([n]) , \] where \(T([m])\) has the discrete topology. Now the coend \(\int^c S(c, c)\) of \(S\) is the geometric realization of \(T\).
