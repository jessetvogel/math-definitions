Consider \(M = \RR^{2n + 1}\) with coordinates \((x_1, \ldots, x_n, y, z_1, \ldots, z_n)\). The standard contact structure on \(M\) is \[ \xi_\textup{std} = \ker \alpha_\textup{std} \quad \textup{ with } \quad \alpha_\textup{std} = dy - \sum_{i = 1}^{n} z_i d x_i . \] Indeed, as \(d \alpha_\textup{std} = \sum_{i = 1}^{n} dx_i \wedge dz_i\), it follows that \[ \alpha_\textup{std} \wedge (d \alpha_\textup{std})^n = n! dy \wedge dx_1 \wedge \cdots \wedge dx_n \wedge dz_1 \wedge \cdots \wedge dz_n \ne 0 . \]
