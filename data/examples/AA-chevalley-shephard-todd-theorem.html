Let \(G = \ZZ/2\ZZ\) act on \(V = k^2\) by \((x, y) \mapsto (x, -y)\). Then \(G\) is generated by a pseudoreflection, and indeed \[ k[x, y]^G = k[x, y^2] \] a free polynomial algebra over \(k\). However, if we let \(G\) act on \(V\) by \((x, y) \mapsto (-x, -y)\), then \(G\) is not generated by pseudoreflections, and indeed \[ k[x, y]^G = k[x^2, xy, y^2] = k[u, v, w] / (w^2 - uv) \] is not a free polynomial algebra over \(k\).
