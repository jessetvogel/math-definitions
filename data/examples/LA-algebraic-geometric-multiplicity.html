Consider the matrix \[ A = \begin{pmatrix} 2 & 1 & 0 \\ 0 & 2 & 0 \\ 0 & 0 & 3 \end{pmatrix} . \] Its characteristic polynomial is \(p_A(\lambda) = (2 - \lambda)^2 (3 - \lambda)\). Hence the eigenvalues are \(\lambda = 2\) (with alg. multiplicity \(2\)) and \(\lambda = 3\) (with alg. multiplicity \(1\)). However, both have a geometric multiplicity of \(1\) as \[ E_2 = \ker(A - 2I) = \text{span}(1, 0, 0) \quad \text{ and } \quad E_3 = \ker(A - 3I) = \text{span}(0, 0, 1) . \]
