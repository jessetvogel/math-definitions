Any torsionless module \(M\) over \(R\) is torsion-free. Namely, if \(m \in M\) exists with \(rm = 0\) for some non zero-divisor \(r \in R\), then for any \(f : M \to R\) we have \(0 = f(rm) = rf(m)\), implying \(f(m) = 0\). However, the converse is not true. The \(\ZZ\)-module \(\QQ\) is clearly torsion-free, but not torsionless since the only \(\ZZ\)-module morphisms \(\QQ \to \ZZ\) is zero.
