Consider \(R = k[x, y] / (xy)\) and \(M = k\) as an \(R\)-module, where \(x\) and \(y\) act by multiplication by zero. Then \(M\) cannot be resolved by a finite complex of projective \(R\)-modules, but there does exist an infinite resolution \[ \cdots \to R^2 \xrightarrow{\begin{pmatrix} y & 0 \\ 0 & x \end{pmatrix}} R^2 \xrightarrow{\begin{pmatrix} x & 0 \\ 0 & y \end{pmatrix}} R^2 \xrightarrow{\begin{pmatrix} y & 0 \\ 0 & x \end{pmatrix}} R^2 \xrightarrow{\begin{pmatrix} x & y \end{pmatrix}} R \to M \to 0 . \] Therefore, the projective dimension of \(M\) is infinite.
