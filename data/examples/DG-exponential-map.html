When \(G\) is a Lie group, the exponential map is the map \[ \exp : \mathfrak{g} \to G \] from Lie algebra \(\mathfrak{g} = T_e G\) of \(G\) to \(G\), given by \(\exp(v) = \gamma(1)\) where \(\gamma : \RR \to G\) is the unique one-parameter subgroup of \(G\) with \(\gamma'(0) = v\).
In particular, when \(G = \text{GL}_n(\RR)\) is the general linear group, we have \(\mathfrak{g} = \text{Mat}_{n \times n}(\RR)\) and \[ \exp : \mathfrak{g} \to G, \quad A \mapsto \exp(A) = \sum_{k = 0}^{\infty} \frac{A^k}{k!} \] is the usual exponential map.
