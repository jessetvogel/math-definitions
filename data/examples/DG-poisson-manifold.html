Any symplectic manifold \((M, \omega)\) gives rise to a Poisson manifold with bracket given by \[ \{ F, G \} = \omega(X_F, X_G) \] where \(X_F\) is the Hamiltonian vector field of \(F\). Indeed the Leibniz rule is satisfied as \[ \{ FG, H \} = d(FG) X_H = (F d G + G d F) X_H = F (d G X_H) + G (d F X_H) = F \{ G, H \} + G \{ F, H \} . \] Furthermore, the Jacobi identity can be shown from \[ \{ \{ F, G \}, H \} + \{ \{ G, H \}, F \} + \{ \{ H, F \}, G \} = -d \omega(X_F, X_G, X_H) \] and that \(\omega\) is closed.
