• The compact objects in \(\textbf{Set}\) are finite sets.
  • The compact objects in \(\textbf{Grp}\) are finitely presented groups.
  • For \(X\) a topological space, the compact objects in the category of open subsets of \(X\) with respect to inclusions, are the compact subsets.
  • The compact objects in \(\textbf{Vect}_k\) are the finite-dimensional vector spaces.
    Let \(V\) be a compact vector space. Since \(V\) is isomorphic to the filtered colimit of its finite-dimensional subspaces \(W \subset V\), it follows that \[ \underset{\textup{fd } W \subset V}{\operatorname{colim}} \Hom(V, W) \simeq \Hom(V, V) . \] In particular, there exists a finite-dimensional subspace \(i : W \hookrightarrow V\) and a morphism \(f : V \to W\) such that \(i \circ f = \id_V\), which shows that \(V \simeq W\) is finite-dimensional. Conversely, for any finite-dimensional vector space \(V\), there is an adjunction \[ \Hom(V, -) \dashv \Hom(V^*, -) , \] where \(V^*\) denotes the dual of \(V\). Therefore, \(\Hom(V, - )\) preserves all colimits, so \(V\) is compact.
