Any flasque sheaf is soft, but not conversely. Namely, consider \(X = \{ a, b \}\) with topology \(\mathcal{T} = \{ \varnothing, \{ a \}, X \}\) and sheaf \(\mathcal{F}\) given by \[ \mathcal{F}(\{ a \}) = \ZZ \quad \textup{ and } \quad \mathcal{F}(X) = 0 . \] Then \(\mathcal{F}\) is not flasque as \(\mathcal{F}(X) \to \mathcal{F}(\{ a \})\) is not surjective, but \(\mathcal{F}\) is soft as \(\mathcal{F} \to \mathcal{F}(\{ b \}) = \colim_{U \supset \{ b \}} \mathcal{F}(U) = \mathcal{F}(X) = 0\) is surjective.
