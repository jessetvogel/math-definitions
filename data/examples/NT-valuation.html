For \(K\) equal to \(\CC\) or a subfield of it such as \(\RR\) or \(\QQ\), the absolute value \(\phi = |\cdot|\) defines a valuation. Its norm is \(2\).
For a prime \(\mathfrak{p}\) of a number field \(K\), there is the valuation \[ \phi_\mathfrak{p} : K \to \RR_{\ge 0}, \quad x \mapsto c^{\text{ord}_\mathfrak{p}(x)} , \] for some fixed \(c \in (0, 1)\). From \[ \text{ord}_\mathfrak{p}(x + y) \ge \min \{ \text{ord}_\mathfrak{p}(x), \text{ord}_\mathfrak{p}(y) \} \] follows that \(\norm{\phi_\mathfrak{p}} = 1\).
For an irreducible polynomial \(P\) in the polynomial ring \(F[X]\) over an arbitrary field \(F\), we have the number \(\text{ord}_P(f) \in \ZZ_{\ge 0}\) of factors \(P\) occurring in the factorization of a non-zero polynomial \(f \in F[X]\), which is well-defined as \(F[X]\) is a unique factorization domain. It yields the valuation on the field of fractions \(F(X)\), \[ \phi_P : F(X) \to \RR_{\ge 0}, \quad f \mapsto c^{\text{ord}_P(f)} , \] for some fixed \(c \in (0, 1)\). From \[ \text{ord}_P(f + g) \ge \min \{ \text{ord}_P(f), \text{ord}_P(g) \} \] follows that \(\norm{\phi_P} = 1\).
If \(K = \FF_q\) is a finite field, then every valuation \(\phi\) is trivial: every non-zero \(x \in K\) has finite order, so \(\phi(x)^n = \phi(x^n) = \phi(1) = 1\) for some \(n \ge 1\), so \(\phi(x) = 1\).
