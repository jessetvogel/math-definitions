Any ring \(R\) is Morita equivalent to the ring \(\textup{M}_n(R)\) of \(n \times n\) matrices with elements in \(R\), for any \(n > 0\). Indeed, take \[ R\textup{-Mod} \to \textup{M}_n(R)\textup{-Mod}, \quad M \to R^{n \times 1} \otimes_R M \isom M^n , \] where \(\textup{M}_n(R)\) acts on \(M^n\) by matrix multiplication on the left. Inversely, take \[ \textup{M}_n(R)\textup{-Mod} \to R\textup{-Mod}, \quad N \to R^{1 \times n} \otimes_{\textup{M}_n(R)} N . \] Indeed these are inverse to each other since \[ R^{1 \times n} \otimes_{\textup{M}_n(R)} R^{n \times 1} \isom R \quad \textup{and} \quad R^{n \times 1} \otimes_R R^{1 \times n} \isom \textup{M}_n(R) . \]
