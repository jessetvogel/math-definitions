Note that \(\ker \partial^1\) consists of all \(f : R \to M\) for which \(\partial f\), given by \((r \otimes s) \mapsto r f(s) - f(rs) + f(r) s\), is zero, that is, \(f\) is a \(k\)-derivation. Furthermore, \(\im \partial^0\) consists of all \(f\) such that \(f(r) = mr - rm\), which are known as inner derivations. Therefore, \(HH^1(R, M) = \{ \text{derivations} \} / \{ \text{inner derivations} \}\).
The Hochschild (co)homology can be expressed in terms of Tor (Ext) as \[ HH_i(R, M) \simeq \text{Tor}_i^{R^\text{e}}(R, M) \quad \text{and} \quad HH^i(R, M) \simeq \text{Ext}^i_{R^\text{e}}(R, M) , \] where \(R^\text{e}\) denotes the enveloping algebra of \(R\). This can be shown by resolving \(R\) as a (left) \(R^\text{e}\)-module using the bar resolution.
The (first order) deformations of a ring \(R\) up to trivial deformations are parametrized by \(HH^2(R)\). Namely, any deformed multiplication \(r \star s := rs + \varepsilon f(r, s)\) with \(f : R \otimes R \to R\) and \(\varepsilon^2 = 0\) is associative if and only if \((r \star s) \star t = r \star (s \star t)\) for all \(r, s, t \in R\). This translates to \[ r f(s, t) - f(rs, t) + f(r, st) - f(r, s) t = 0 , \] that is, \(\partial f = 0\). If \(f\) is a trivial deformation, then there exists some \(k[\varepsilon]\)-linear automorphism \(\phi\) of \(R[\varepsilon]\) such that \(\phi(r_1 + \varepsilon r_2) \phi(s_1 + \varepsilon s_2) = \phi((r_1 + \varepsilon r_2) \star (s_1 + \varepsilon s_2))\). Expanding gives \(f(r, s) = r \phi_1(s) - \phi(rs) + \phi(r) s = (\partial^1 \phi)(r, s)\) for all \(r, s \in R\), that is, \(f \in \im \partial\).
