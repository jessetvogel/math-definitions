Let \(i : M \to N\) and \(f : M \to Q\) be \(R\)-module morphisms, with \(i\) injective. Consider the partially ordered set of pairs \((M', f')\), with \(M \subset M' \subset N\) and \(f'\) extending \(f\), where \((M', f') \le (M'', f'')\) if \(M' \subset M''\) and \(f''\) extends \(f'\). By Zornâ€™s lemma, there exists a maximal element \((M', f')\). If \(M' = N\), we are done, and otherwise there exists some \(x \in N \setminus M'\). Consider the ideal \(I = \{ r \in R \mid rx \in M' \}\) and define \(g : I \to Q\) by \(g(r) = f'(rx)\). Then by assumption \(g\) extends to some \(h : R \to Q\). Now let \(M'' = M' + \langle x \rangle\) and define \[ f'' : M'' \to Q, \quad y + rx \mapsto f'(y) + h(r) , \] for all \(y \in M'\) and \(r \in R\). Then \(f''\) extends \(f'\), contradicting the maximality of \(M'\).
