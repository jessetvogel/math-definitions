• The partition \(\lambda = (n)\), corresponding to the trivial representation of \(S_n\), yields the Schur functor \[ \mathbb{S}_{(n)}(M) = \operatorname{Sym}^n M . \]
  • The partition \(\lambda = (1, \ldots, 1)\), corresponding to the alternating representation of \(S_n\), yields the Schur functor \[ \mathbb{S}_{(1, \ldots, 1)}(M) = \wedge^n M . \]
