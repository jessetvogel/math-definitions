• The field extension \(\FF_2[\sqrt{t}] \supset \FF_2[t]\) is normal, since any field extension of degree \(2\) is normal, but not separable. Namely, the minimal polynomial of \(\sqrt{t}\) is \(x^2 - t = (x - \sqrt{t})^2\), which is not separable.
  • The field extension \(\QQ(\sqrt[3]{2}) \supset \QQ\) is separable, since any field extension over characteristic zero is separable, but not normal. Namely, the conjugates of \(\sqrt[3]{2}\) are \(\zeta_3 \sqrt[3]{2}\) and \(\zeta_3^2 \sqrt[3]{2}\), which do not lie in \(\QQ(\sqrt[3]{2})\).
  • Its normal closure, \(\QQ(\sqrt[3]{2}, \zeta_3)\), is normal, and hence a Galois extension of \(\QQ\).
