A ring \(R\) can be considered as an additive category \(\mathcal{R}\) with one object \(\bdot\) via \(R = \text{End}_\mathcal{R}(\bdot)\): multiplication is given by composition. Let \(S\) is a multiplicatively closed subset of \(R\) containing \(1\). If \(R\) is commutative, or more generally if \(S\) is in the center of \(R\), then the localization \(S^{-1} R\) can be defined, which corresponds precisely to the localization \(\mathcal{R}\left[\frac{1}{S}\right]\).
