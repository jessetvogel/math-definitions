Let \(\phi : K \to \RR_{\ge 0}\) be a non-archimedean valuation on a field \(K\). Then the subring \[ A = \{ x \in K \mid \phi(x) \le 1 \} \] is a valuation ring, called the valuation ring of \(\phi\). Indeed, for every \(x \in K\), we have \(x \in A\) if \(\phi(x) \le 1\) and \(x^{-1} \in A\) if \(\phi(x) > 1\). In particular, the field of fractions of \(A\) is \(K\). The valuation ring \(A\) is a local ring with unit group \(A^* = \{ x \in K \mid \phi(x) = 1 \}\) and maximal ideal \(\mathfrak{m} = \{ x \in K \mid \phi(x) < 1 \}\).
