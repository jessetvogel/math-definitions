Consider \(\mathfrak{g} = \mathfrak{sl}_2(\CC)\) with the usual basis \[ X = \begin{pmatrix} 0 & 1 \\ 0 & 0 \end{pmatrix}, \quad Y = \begin{pmatrix} 0 & 0 \\ -1 & 0 \end{pmatrix} \quad \textup{ and } \quad H = \begin{pmatrix} 1 & 0 \\ 0 & -1 \end{pmatrix} . \] For any \(\lambda \in \mathfrak{h}^*\) given by \(\lambda(H) = m \in \CC\), the corresponding Verma module \(V_\lambda\) is spanned by vectors \(v_0, v_1, v_2, \ldots\) and satisfies \[ Y \cdot v_j = v_{j + 1}, \quad X \cdot v_j = j(m - (j - 1)) v_{j - 1}, \quad H \cdot v_j = (m - 2j) v_j . \] In particular, \(X \cdot v_0 = 0\) and \(H \cdot v_0 = m v_0\), so \(v_0\) is a highest weight vector of weight \(m\).
