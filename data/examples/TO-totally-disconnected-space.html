• Any discrete space is totally disconnected.
  • The rational numbers \(\QQ\) with Euclidean topology is totally disconnected. Namely, for any \(A \subset \QQ\) containing at least two distinct \(q_1, q_2 \in A\), there exists an irrational number \(r\) with \(q_1 < r < q_2\), so that \[ A = \{ q \in A \mid q < r \} \sqcup \{ q \in A \mid q > r \} , \] which shows \(A\) is disconnected.
