Any free group \(F_S\) is, by the Seifertâ€“van Kampen theorem, the fundamental group of a bouquet of circles \((X, x) = \coprod_{s \in S} (S^1, *)\). In particular, \((X, x)\) is a (pointed) connected \(1\)-dimensional CW-complex. By the theory of covering spaces, the subgroups \(H \subset \pi_1(X, x) = F_S\) are in bijection with isomorphism classes of connected covering spaces \(p : (Y, y) \to (X, x)\) with \(\pi_1(Y, y) \cong H\). Now, a covering space of a connected \(1\)-dimensional CW-complex is again a connected \(1\)-dimensional CW-complex, and hence homotopy equivalent to a bouquet of circles, whose fundamental group is free. Therefore, \(H\) is free.
