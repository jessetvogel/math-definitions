Every integral distribution is involutive. Indeed, at any point \(p \in M\) there exists an integral submanifold \(i : N \to M\), so that \(X, Y \in \Gamma(\mathcal{D})\) can be viewed as vector fields on \(N\). Hence, \([X, Y]\) is also a vector field on \(N\).
Conversely, it is a theorem by Frobenius that every involutive distribution is integrable.
