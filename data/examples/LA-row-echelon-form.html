Consider the matrices \[ A = \begin{pmatrix} 1 & 0 & 0 & 0 \\ 0 & 2 & 0 & 0 \\ 0 & 1 & 0 & 0 \end{pmatrix}, \quad B = \begin{pmatrix} 0 & 3 & 0 & 7 \\ 0 & 0 & 5 & 8 \\ 0 & 0 & 0 & 0 \end{pmatrix}, \quad C = \begin{pmatrix} 0 & 1 & 0 & 3 \\ 0 & 0 & 1 & 4 \\ 0 & 0 & 0 & 0 \end{pmatrix} . \] The matrices \(B\) and \(C\) are in row echelon form, and only \(C\) is in reduced row echelon form. Matrix \(A\) is not in row echelon form.
