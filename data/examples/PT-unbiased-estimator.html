Let \(X_1, \ldots, X_n\) be a random sample from a uniform distribution on the interval \([0, \theta]\) with parameter \(\theta \in \RR\) unknown. The statistic \(Y = \max \{ X_1, \ldots, X_n \}\) has CDF \[ F_Y(y; \theta) = \PP(Y \le y) = \prod_{i = 1}^{n} \PP(X_i \le y) = (y / \theta)^n \] and its derivative is the PDF of \(Y\), \[ f_Y(y; \theta) = n y^{n - 1} / \theta^n . \] Hence, the expected value of \(Y\) is \[ \EE(Y) = \int_0^\theta y f_Y(y; \theta) dy = \int_0^\theta n y^n / \theta^n dy = \frac{n}{n + 1} \theta . \] In particular, \(\frac{n + 1}{n} Y\) is an unbiased estimator for \(\theta\).
