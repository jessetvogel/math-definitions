One way to construct toric varieties is using fans. Let \(N \simeq \ZZ^n\) be a finitely generated free abelian group, and let \(\Delta\) be a fan in \(N_\RR = N \otimes_\ZZ \RR\). For every cone \(\sigma \in \Delta\), consider the commutative monoid \(S_\sigma = \sigma^\vee \cap M\), where \(\sigma^\vee\) is denotes the dual cone, and \(M = \Hom_\ZZ(N, \ZZ)\). Furthermore, let \(k\) be a commutative ring, and write \(U_\sigma = \Spec(k[S_\sigma])\).
Now for every face \(\tau \subset \sigma\), there exists some \(u \in \sigma^\vee\) such that \(\tau = \sigma \cap u^\perp\), and hence \(S_\tau = S_\sigma + \ZZ_{\ge 0} \cdot (-u)\). So, the natural map \(k[S_\sigma] \to k[S_\tau]\) is a localization given by inverting \(u\), which induces a principal open subset \(\Spec(k[S_\tau]) \to \Spec(k[S_\sigma])\).
One constructs a toric variety \(X(\Delta)\) from \(\Delta\) by taking all \(U_\sigma\), and gluing \(U_{\sigma_1}\) and \(U_{\sigma_2}\) along \(U_\tau\) whenever \(\tau = \sigma_1 \cap \sigma_2\) is a face of both cones \(\sigma_1, \sigma_2 \in \Delta\).
As an example, take \(N = \ZZ^2\) and \(\Delta\) the fan as described by the picture below. One computes \[ U_{\sigma_1} = \Spec(k[x, y]), \quad U_{\sigma_2} = \Spec(k[x^{-1}, yx^{-1}]), \quad U_{\sigma_3} = \Spec(k[y^{-1}, xy^{-1}]) , \] and gluing gives \(X(\Delta) \simeq \PP_k^2\).
Projective space \(\PP^n\) is a toric variety, since the torus \[ T = \{ (1 : t_1 : t_2 : \cdots : t_n) : t_i \ne 0 \} \subset \PP^n \] is open and dense, and the action of \(T\) on itself extends to \(\PP^n\) via \[ T \times \PP^n \to \PP^n, \quad \left( (t_1, \ldots, t_n), (x_0 : x_1 : \cdots x_n) \right) \mapsto (x_0 : t_1 x_1 : \cdots t_n x_n) . \]
