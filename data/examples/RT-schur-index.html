Let \(k = \QQ\) and take \(G\) to be the quaternion group \(Q_8 = \langle \overline{e}, i, j, k \mid \overline{e}^2 = 1, i^2 = j^2 = k^2 = ijk = \overline{e} \rangle\). Consider the complex representation \(V\) of \(Q_8\) given by \[ \overline{e} \mapsto \begin{pmatrix} -1 & 0 \\ 0 & -1 \end{pmatrix}, \quad i \mapsto \begin{pmatrix} i & 0 \\ 0 & -i \end{pmatrix}, \quad j \mapsto \begin{pmatrix} 0 & -1 \\ 1 & 0 \end{pmatrix}, \quad k \mapsto \begin{pmatrix} 0 & -i \\ -i & 0 \end{pmatrix} . \] Computing the traces of all matrices, one sees that \(k(\chi) = \QQ\). Now, since \(V\) cannot be realized over \(\QQ\), but it can be realized over \(\QQ(i)\), the Schur index of \(V\) is \(m_\QQ(V) = 2\).
