Let \(K\) be a number field, and \(\phi : K \to \RR_{\ge 0}\) a valuation corresponding to a prime \(\mathfrak{p}\) of the ring of integers \(\mathcal{O}_K\) of \(K\). Then the completion \(K_\phi\) of \(K\) with respect to \(\phi\) is a local field.
Any local field \(K\) is complete with respect to the metric induced by \(\phi\), and either
  • \(K\) is archimedean, and topologically isomorphic to \(\RR\) or \(\CC\),
  • \(K\) is non-archimedean, its valuation is discrete and its residue field is finite.
