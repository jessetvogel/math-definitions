• The category Set is balanced, as injective and surjective imply bijective.
  • Any abelian category is balanced. Since every monomorphism \(f : A \to B\) is the kernel of some map \(\pi : B \to C\), it is also the equalizer of \(\pi\) and \(0\). If moreover \(f\) is an epimorphism, then \(\pi = 0\), implying \(f\) is an isomorphism.
  • The category Ring is not balanced. Namely, the inclusion \(\ZZ \to \QQ\) is a monomorphism since it is injective, and an epimorphism since a morphism \(\QQ \to R\) is determined by the image of \(\ZZ\). However, it is clearly not an isomorphism.
  • The category Top is not balanced. Namely, the map \(\RR \to \RR\) from the discrete topology to the usual topology is a monomorphism and an epimorphism, but not an isomorphism.
