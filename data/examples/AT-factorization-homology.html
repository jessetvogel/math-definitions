• Let \(A\) be an abelian group and \(M\) a smooth manifold, then \(\int_M A \isom H_\bdot(M; A)\), the singular homology of \(A\) with coefficients in \(A\).
  • Let \(A\) be an associative algebra, then \(\int_{S^1} A \isom HH_\bdot(A)\), the Hochschild homology of \(A\). Indeed, from \(S^1 = D^1 \sqcup_{D^1 \times S^0} D^1\) follows that \[ \int_{S^1} A \isom \int_{D^1} A \mathbin{\mathop{\overset{\textup{L}}{\otimes}}\limits_{\int_{D^1 \times S^0} A}} \int_{D^1} A \isom A \mathbin{\mathop{\overset{\textup{L}}{\otimes}}\limits_{A \otimes A^\textup{op}}} A \isom \textup{Tor}^{A^\textup{e}}_*(A, A) \isom HH_\bdot(A) . \]
