For any pointed spaces \(X, Y\), there is a natural bijection \[ [\Sigma X, Y] \simeq [X, \Omega Y], \] where \([A, B]\) denotes the set of homotopy classes of maps \(A \to B\), and \(\Sigma X\) is the suspension of \(X\). In particular, this yields \[ \pi_k(\Omega X) = [ S^k, \Omega X ] \simeq [ \Sigma S^k, X ] \simeq [ S^{k + 1}, X ] = \pi_{k + 1}(X) , \] which is in fact an isomorphism of groups for \(k \ge 1\).
