Take \(X = \RR^n\) with \(d(x, y) = \norm{x - y}\). This is a metric space since (i) \(\norm{x - y} = 0\) if and only if \(x = y\), (ii) \(d(x, y) = \norm{x - y} = \norm{y - x} = d(x, y)\) and (iii) \(\norm{x - z} \le \norm{x - y} + \norm{y - z}\) is the usual triangle inequality.
Non-negativity of the metric follows directly from the axioms as \[ d(x, y) = \frac{1}{2} (d(x, y) + d(y, x)) \ge \frac{1}{2} d(x, x) = 0 \] for all \(x, y \in X\).
Any metric space is naturally a topological space, where a basis of the topology is given by the set of open balls \[ B(x, r) = \{ y \in X : d(x, y) < r \} \] for \(x \in X\) and \(r > 0\).
