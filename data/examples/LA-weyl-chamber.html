Below is depicted the root system \(A_2\). The dotted lines are the hyperplanes which are orthogonal to the roots. The filled area is one of the Weyl chambers, corresponding to the base \(\Delta = \{ (0, 1), (\frac{1}{2} \sqrt{3}, -\frac{1}{2}) \}\). Note that the Weyl group, in this case \(W = S_3\), acts on the set of Weyl chambers (or equivalently, on the set of bases for \(\Phi\)). It is a theorem that this action is always free and transitive.
