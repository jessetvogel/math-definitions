Since conjugation \(x \mapsto gxg^{-1}\), for any \(g \in G\), defines an automorphism of \(G\), it follows that characteristic subgroups are normal. However, the converse does not hold. Namely, if we consider the additive group \(\QQ\) with normal subgroup \(\ZZ \subset \QQ\), then \(\ZZ\) is not preserved under the automorphism \(\QQ \to \QQ\), \(q \mapsto q/2\).
