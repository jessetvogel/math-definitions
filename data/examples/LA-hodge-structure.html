• For any \(H_R\), taking \(H^{k, k} = H_\CC\) and \(H^{p, q} = 0\) when \((p, q) \ne (k, k)\) gives the trivial Hodge structure of weight \(2k\).
  • Take \(H_\ZZ = 2 \pi i \ZZ \subset \CC\) and \(H_\CC = H^{-1, -1}\). This is a Hodge structure of weight \(-2\). In fact, is the unique \(1\)-dimensional Hodge structure of weight \(-2\) up to isomorphism, and it is called the Tate Hodge structure, often denoted by \(\ZZ(1)\).
