Consider \(\mathfrak{g} = \RR^3\) with bracket operation defined by the cross product \([x, y] = x \times y\).
For any Lie group \(G\), the tangent space at the identity \(\mathfrak{g} = T_e G\) has a Lie algebra structure, called the Lie algebra of \(G\). Tangent vectors \(v \in T_e G\) correspond one-to-one to left invariant vector fields on \(G\), i.e. vector fields \(X\) satisfying \(X_g = ((L_g)_* X)_e\) for all \(g \in G\), where \(L_g : G \to G\) denotes left multiplication by \(g\). The Lie bracket on \(\mathfrak{g}\) is then given by the Lie bracket of vector fields. Note that the Lie bracket of left-invariant vector fields is indeed also left-invariant.
  • \(\mathfrak{sl}_n(k) = \{ X \in \textup{Mat}_{n \times n}(k) : \tr X = 0 \}\) is the Lie algebra associated to the special linear group.
  • \(\mathfrak{so}_n = \{ X \in \textup{Mat}_{n \times n}(\RR) : X + X^T = 0 \}\) is the Lie algebra associated to the (special) orthogonal group.
  • \(\mathfrak{u}_n = \{ A \in \textup{Mat}_{n \times n}(\CC) : X + X^H = 0 \}\) is the Lie algebra associated to the special unitary group.
  • \(\mathfrak{su}_n = \{ A \in \textup{Mat}_{n \times n}(\CC) : X + X^H = 0 \textup{ and } \tr X = 0 \}\) is the Lie algebra associated to the special unitary group.
  • \(\mathfrak{sp}_{2n}(k) = \{ A \in \textup{Mat}_{2n \times 2n}(k) : \Omega X + X^T \Omega = 0 \}\) is the Lie algebra associated to the symplectic group.
For any ring \(A\) over a field \(k\) (in particular matrix rings), the commutator bracket \([x, y] = xy - yx\) makes \(A\) into a Lie algebra.
