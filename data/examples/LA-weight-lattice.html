The weight lattice \(P\) is preserved under the Weyl group. Indeed, for any \(\mu \in P\) and \(\alpha \in \Phi\), the reflection \(s_\alpha(\mu) = \mu - 2 \frac{\langle \mu, \alpha \rangle}{\langle \alpha, \alpha \rangle} \alpha\) satisfies \[ 2 \frac{\langle s_\alpha(\mu), \beta \rangle}{\langle \beta, \beta \rangle} = 2 \frac{\langle \mu, \beta \rangle}{\langle \beta, \beta \rangle} - \left( 2 \frac{\langle \mu, \alpha \rangle}{\langle \alpha, \alpha \rangle} \right) \left( 2 \frac{\langle \alpha, \beta \rangle}{\langle \beta, \beta \rangle} \right) \in \ZZ , \] for all \(\beta \in \Phi\), so \(s_\alpha(\mu)\) is integral as well.
