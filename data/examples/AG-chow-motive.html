For any morphism \(f : X \to Y\) between smooth projective schemes over \(k\), the graph \(\Gamma_f = \{ (x, y) \in X \times_k Y \mid y = f(x) \}\) defines a correspondence \(\Gamma_f \in \textup{CH}_{\dim X}(X \times_k Y)\). For morphisms \(f : X \to Y\) and \(g : Y \to Z\), one can check that \(\Gamma_g \circ \Gamma_f =\Gamma_{g \circ f}\). In particular, there is a functor \[ \textbf{SmProj}_k \to \textup{Chow}^\textup{eff}(k) , \] which sends a smooth projective scheme \(X\) over \(k\) to \((X, \Gamma_{\id_X})\), and a morphism \(f : X \to Y\) to \(\Gamma_f\).
