Let \(A\) be a diagonalizable matrix. Note that we can write \[ AC = DC , \] with \(C\) invertible and \(D\) diagonal. From this expression it is clear that the columns of \(C\) must be eigenvectors of \(A\), and that \(D\) contains the corresponding eigenvalues. The matrix \(C\) being invertible means that \(A\) has \(n\) linearly independent eigenvectors.
