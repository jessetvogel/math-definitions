Let \(G = \textup{SL}_2\) with \(B \subset G\) any Borel subgroup invariant under \(F\). The Weyl group \(W\) is isomorphic to \(\ZZ/2\ZZ = \{ 1, \sigma \}\), and the Deligne–Lustzig varieties are given by \[ X(1) = \mathcal{O}(1) \cap \Gamma_F = G^F / B^F = \PP^1(\FF_q) \] and \[ X(\sigma) = \mathcal{O}(\sigma) \cap \Gamma_F = \PP^1 \setminus \PP^1(\FF_q) . \] Note that the latter is isomorphic to \(\{ x^q y - x y^q = 1 \}\).
The Deligne–Lustzig varieties \(X(w)\) are non-empty for all \(w \in W\). Namely, by Lang’s theorem there exists some \(g \in G\) such that \(g^{-1} F(g)\) represents \(w\), so that \((g B, F(g B)) \in X(w)\).
