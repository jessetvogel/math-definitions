If \(\mathcal{A}\) has enough injectives, then \(F\) is effaceable if and only if \(F(I) = 0\) for all injective objects \(I\) of \(\mathcal{A}\).
\((\Leftarrow)\) As \(\mathcal{A}\) has enough injectives, for any \(A\) of \(\mathcal{A}\), there exists a monomorphism \(m : A \to I\) with \(I\) injective. Since \(F(I) = 0\), also \(F(m) = 0\). \((\Rightarrow)\) Let \(I\) be an injective object of \(\mathcal{A}\). Since \(F\) is effaceable, there exists a monomorphism \(m : I \to J\), and by injectivity of \(I\), there exists a map \(h : J \to I\) such that \(hm = \id_I\). Now \(F(\id_I) = F(h) \circ F(m) = F(h) \circ 0 = 0\), so \(F(I) = 0\).
