The kernel of a morphism \(f : G \to H\) is always a normal subgroup. Indeed, for any \(x \in \ker f\) and \(g \in G\) we have \[ f(gxg^{-1}) = f(g) f(x) f(g)^{-1} = f(g) f(g)^{-1} = 1 , \] so \(gxg^{-1} \in \ker f\), and thus \(g (\ker f) g^{-1} \subset \ker f\). The other inclusion is shown completely similarly.
Conversely, any normal subgroup \(N \subset G\) is the kernel of the quotient map \(\pi : G \to G / N\).
