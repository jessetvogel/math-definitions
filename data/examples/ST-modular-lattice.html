Let \(R\) be a ring and \(M\) an \(R\)-module. Then the lattice of submodules of \(M\) with respect to inclusions, sums and intersections, is a modular lattice. That is, for any submodules \(A, B, C \subset M\) with \(A \subset C\), we have \[ A + (B \cap C) = (A + B) \cap C . \] Indeed, for any \(x \in A + (B \cap C)\), we can write \(x = a + y\) with \(a \in A\) and \(y \in B \cap C\). Since \(A \subset C\), we have \(a \in A \cap C\), so \(x \in (A + B) \cap C\). Conversely, for any \(x \in (A + B) \cap C\), we can write \(x = a + b\) with \(a \in A\) and \(b \in B\) and \(a + b \in C\). Since \(a \in A \subset C\), also \(b = (a + b) - a \in C\), so \(x \in A + (B \cap C)\).
