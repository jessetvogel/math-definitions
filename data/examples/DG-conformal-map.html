Let \(M = \RR^2 \setminus \{ (0, 0) \}\) and \(M' = \RR^2\), both equipped with the standard Euclidean metric, and consider the map \[ f : M \to M', \quad (x, y) \mapsto (u, v) = \left(\frac{x}{x^2 + y^2}, \frac{y}{x^2 + y^2} \right) . \] Then the pullback of \(g' = du^2 + dv^2\) along \(f\) is given by \[ \begin{aligned} f^* g' &= f^* (du^2 + dv^2) \\ &= \left(d \left(\frac{x}{x^2 + y^2} \right)\right)^2 + \left(d \left( \frac{y}{x^2 + y^2} \right)\right)^2 \\ &= \frac{1}{(x^2 + y^2)^2} (dx^2 + dy^2) , \end{aligned} \] so \(f\) is conformal with conformal factor \(\alpha = (x^2 + y^2)^{-2}\).
