For \(n = 1\), the generalized Jacobi identity states that the map \(d = l_1 : \mathfrak{g} \to \mathfrak{g}\), of degree \(-1\), satisfies \[ d^2 = 0 , \] making \(\mathfrak{g}\) into a chain complex. For \(n = 2\), the condition states that \[ d[v_1, v_2] = [dv_1, v_2] + (-1)^{\deg(v_1)} [v_1, dv_2] , \] for homogeneous \(v_1, v_2 \in \mathfrak{g}\), making \(d\) a derivation for the bracket \([-, -]\).
If \(l_n = 0\) for all \(n \ge 2\), then the generalized Jacobi identity for \(n = 3\) translates to the graded Jacobi identity \[ [v_1, [v_2, v_3] = [[v_1, v_2], v_3] + (-1)^{\deg(v_1) \deg(v_2)} [v_2, [v_1, v_3]] , \] for all homogeneous \(v_1, v_2, v_3 \in \mathfrak{g}\). In particular, such an \(L_\infty\)-algebra is equivalent to a differential graded Lie algebra.
