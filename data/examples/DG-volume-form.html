When \(M\) is an oriented Riemannian manifold with metric \(g\), there is a natural volume form on \(M\). In local coordinates \(x_1, \ldots, x_n\) it is given by \[ \textup{vol}_g = \sqrt{|\det g|} \; dx_1 \wedge dx_2 \wedge \cdots \wedge dx_n , \] which is indeed independent of the choice of local coordinates.
