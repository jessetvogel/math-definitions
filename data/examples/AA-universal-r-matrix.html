Sweedler’s Hopf algebra is the Hopf algebra \(A\) generated by two elements \(x\) and \(y\) and relations \[ x^2 = 1, \quad y^2 = 0, \quad xy + yx = 0 , \] whose Hopf algebra structure given by \[ \Delta(x) = x \otimes x, \quad \Delta(y) = 1 \otimes y + y \otimes x, \quad S(x) = x, \quad S(y) = xy, \quad \varepsilon(x) = 1, \quad \varepsilon(y) = 0 . \] For any \(q \in k\), the matrix \[ R_q = \frac{1}{2} (1 \otimes 1 + 1 \otimes x + x \otimes 1 - x \otimes x) + \frac{q}{2} (y \otimes y + y \otimes xy + xy \otimes xy - xy \otimes y) \] is a universal \(R\)-matrix for \(A\). Note that \(R_q^{-1} = \tau_{A, A}(R_q)\).
Any universal \(R\)-matrix \(R\) of \(A\) satisfies the quantum Yang–Baxter equation, as \[ \begin{aligned} R_{12} R_{13} R_{23} &= R_{12} (\Delta \otimes \id) R \\ &= ((\tau_{A, A} \circ \Delta \otimes \id) R) R_{12} \\ &= ((\tau_{A, A} \otimes \id)(\Delta \otimes \id) R) R_{12} \\ &= ((\tau_{A, A} \otimes \id) R_{13} R_{23}) R_{12} \\ &= R_{23} R_{13} R_{12} . \end{aligned} \]
Let \(A\) be a Hopf algebra over a field \(k\), and consider the monoidal category of (left) \(A\)-modules. The tensor product of \(A\)-modules \(V\) and \(W\) is given by \(V \otimes_k W\), where \(a \cdot (v \otimes w) = \Delta(a) \cdot (v, w)\), and the unit is the one-dimensional vector space \(\textbf{1} = k\) with \(a \cdot \lambda = \varepsilon(a) \lambda\). This being a monoidal category follows from \(A\) being a bialgebra.
Now a universal \(R\)-matrix \(R\) precisely corresponds to a braiding \[ c_{V, W}^R(v \otimes w) = \tau_{V, W}(R (v \otimes w)) . \] Conversely, from a braiding \(c\) one can obtain a universal \(R\)-matrix as \[ R = \tau_{A, A}(c_{A, A}(1 \otimes 1)) . \]
