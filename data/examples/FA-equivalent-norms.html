On a finite-dimensional vector space \(V = \RR^n\) or \(\CC^n\), all norms are equivalent. Namely, since the unit sphere is compact, both norms \(\norm{\cdot}_1\) and \(\norm{\cdot}_2\) attain minima \(m_1, m_2\) and maxima \(M_1, M_2\) on the sphere. Now it follows that \[ \frac{m_2}{M_1} \norm{x}_1 \le \norm{x}_2 \le \frac{M_2}{m_1} \norm{x}_1 \] for all \(x \in V\) by linearity.
