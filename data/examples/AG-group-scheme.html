• For any group \(G\), the scheme \(G_S = \bigsqcup_{g \in G} S\), with multiplication, inversion and the unit given by those of \(G\), is a group scheme, called the constant group scheme.
  • The scheme \(\GG_m = \Spec \ZZ[t^{\pm 1}]\) with multiplication \(t \mapsto t \otimes t\), inversion \(t \mapsto t^{-1}\) and unit \(t \mapsto 1\), is a group scheme over \(S = \Spec \ZZ\), called the multiplicative group scheme.
  • The scheme \(\GG_m = \Spec \ZZ[x]\) with multiplication \(x \mapsto x \otimes 1 + 1 \otimes x\), inversion \(x \mapsto -x\) and unit \(x \mapsto 0\), is a group scheme over \(S = \Spec \ZZ\), called the additive group scheme.
  • The scheme \(\operatorname{GL}_n = \Spec \ZZ[x_{ij} : 1 \le i, j \le n]\) with matrix multiplication is a group scheme, called the general linear group of rank \(n\).
