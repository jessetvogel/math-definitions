Every convergent sequence is Cauchy. Namely, if \(x = \lim_{n \to \infty} x_n\), then given an \(\varepsilon > 0\), there exists an \(N \ge 0\) such that \(d(x, x_n) < \varepsilon / 2\) for all \(n \ge N\). In particular for \(m, n \ge N\) we have \[ d(x_m, x_n) \le d(x_m, x) + d(x, x_n) < \varepsilon/2 + \varepsilon/2 = \varepsilon . \]
