Let \(R = k[x, y]\) and \(M = k[x, y] / (x^2, xy)\). The prime ideals associated to \(M\) are \[ \mathfrak{p}_1 = (x) \quad \textup{ and } \quad \mathfrak{p}_2 = (x, y) , \] annihilating \(y\) and \(x\), respectively. Since \(\mathfrak{p}_1 \subset \mathfrak{p}_2\), the prime ideal \(\mathfrak{p}_1\) is isolated, while \(\mathfrak{p}_2\) is embedded.
Geometrically, this corresponds to the closed subscheme defined by \(\mathfrak{p}_2\) being embedded in the closed subscheme defined by \(\mathfrak{p}_1\).
