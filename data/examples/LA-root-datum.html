Let \(G\) be a reductive algebraic group over an algebraically closed field \(k\), with a split maximal torus \(T\). Its root datum is the quadruple \((X^*, \Phi, X_*, \Phi^\vee)\), where
  • \(X^* = \Hom(T, \GG_m)\) is that character lattice,
  • \(X_* = \Hom(\GG_m, T)\) is the lattice of \(1\)-parameter subgroups,
  • the perfect pairing \(\langle \alpha, \lambda \rangle\) is given by taking the degree of the composition \(\alpha \circ \lambda : \GG_m \to \GG_m\),
  • the roots \(\Phi\) are all non-zero \(\alpha \in X^*\) for which \[ \mathfrak{g}_\alpha = \{ x \in \mathfrak{g} \mid \textup{Ad}(t) x = \alpha(t) x \textup{ for all } t \in T \} \] is non-zero, where \(\mathfrak{g}\) denotes the Lie algebra of \(G\),
  • for each \(\alpha \in \Phi\), it can be shown there exists a unique \(\alpha^\vee \in X_*\) satisfying some sufficient conditions. This determines \(\Phi^\vee\), and the bijection \(\Phi \to \Phi^\vee\).

It is a theorem that for every root datum, there exists up to isomorphism a unique reductive algebraic group \(G\) with that root datum.
Since the axioms are symmetric, for any root datum \((X^*, \Phi, X_*, \Phi^\vee)\), the quadruple \((X_*, \Phi^\vee, X^*, \Phi)\) is also a root datum, called the dual root datum.
