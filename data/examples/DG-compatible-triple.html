Let \(M = \CC^n\) with the standard Hermitian metric \(h(-, -)\). Decomposing in real and imaginary parts, we have \[ h(u, v) = g(u, v) + i \omega(u, v) \] for some bilinear forms \(g\) and \(\omega\). Since \(h(v, u) = \overline{h(u, v)}\), it follows that \(g\) is symmetric and \(\omega\) is skew-symmetric. Moreover, \(g\) and \(\omega\) are non-degenerate since \(h\) is. In particular, \(g\) is a Riemannian metric, and \(\omega\) a symplectic form. Furthermore, multiplication by \(i\) defines an almost complex structure \(J\). Note that \[ g(u, Jv) + i \omega(u, Jv) = h(u, iv) = i h(u, v) = i g(u, v) - \omega(u, v) , \] from which follows that \[ g(u, v) = \omega(u, Jv) , \] that is, \((\omega, J, g)\) is a compatible triple.
