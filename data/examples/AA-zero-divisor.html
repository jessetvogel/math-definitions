The zero-divisors of the ring \(\ZZ / 6 \ZZ\) are \(\{ \overline{2}, \overline{3}, \overline{4} \}\), since \(\overline{2} \cdot \overline{3} = \overline{3} \cdot \overline{4} = 0\).
Let \(R\) be the ring of endomorphisms of \(V = k^\infty\), for some field \(k\). Let \(f : V \to V\) be given by \(f(e_i) = e_{i + 1}\) with \(\{ e_i : i \in \NN \}\) denotes the standard basis of \(V\). Then \(f\) is a right zero-divisor of \(R\) since \[ g \circ f = 0 \quad \textup{ for }g(e_i) = \left\{ \begin{array}{cl} e_0 & \textup{ if } i = 0 , \\ 0 & \textup{ otherwise.} \end{array} \right. \] However, \(f\) is not a left zero-divisor of \(R\) as it is easy to verify that \(f \circ h = 0\) implies \(h = 0\).
