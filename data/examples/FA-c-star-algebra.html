The algebra \(\text{Mat}_n(\CC)\) of \(n \times n\) matrices over \(\CC\) becomes a C*-algebra if we use operator norm \[ \norm{A} = \sup \{ \norm{A v} : v \in \CC^n \textup{ with } \norm{v} = 1 \} . \]
Let \(X\) be a topological space which is compact and Haudorff. The algebra of complex-valued functions \[ C(X) = \{ f : X \to \CC \textup{ with $f$ continuous} \} \] with norm \[ \norm{f} = \sup_{x \in X} |f(x)| \] and involution \(f^*(x) = \overline{f(x)}\), is a C*-algebra.
