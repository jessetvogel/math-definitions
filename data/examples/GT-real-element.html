• Every element \(\sigma \in S_n\) of the symmetric group is strongly real. Indeed, decomposing \(\sigma\) in terms of cycles, \[ \sigma = (a_{1, 1} \; \ldots \; a_{1, m_1}) (a_{2, 1} \; \ldots \; a_{2, m_2}) \cdots (a_{k, 1} \; \ldots \; a_{k, m_k}) , \] it follows that \(\tau \sigma \tau^{-1} = \sigma^{-1}\) and \(\tau^2 = 1\) for \(\tau(a_{i, j}) = a_{i, m_i - j + 1}\).
  • Every element in the quaternion group \(Q_8 = \langle -1, i, j, k \mid (-1)^2 = 1 \textup{ and } i^2 = j^2 = k^2 = ijk = -1 \rangle\) is real, but not all elements are strongly real. For example, \(j\) is real since \(i j i^{-1} = -j = j^{-1}\), but \(j\) is not strongly real since the only element in \(Q_8\) of order \(2\) is \(-1\), but \((-1) j (-1)^{-1} = j \ne j^{-1}\).
