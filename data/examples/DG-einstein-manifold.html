In general relativity, Einstein’s equation with a cosmological constant \(\Lambda\) is given by \[ \textup{Ric} - \frac{1}{2} g R + g \Lambda = \kappa T , \] where \(R\) is the scalar curvature, \(T\) the stress-energy tensor, and \(\kappa = \frac{8 \pi G}{c^4}\) the gravitational constant. In particular, in a vacuum we obtain \(T = 0\) and \[ \textup{Ric} = \frac{2 \Lambda}{\dim(M) - 2} g . \] Hence, the vacuum solutions of Einstein’s equation are Einstein manifolds.
