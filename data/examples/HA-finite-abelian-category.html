A theorem by Deligne states that for every finite abelian category \(\mathcal{A}\) over \(k\), there is a finite-dimensional \(k\)-algebra \(A\) and a \(k\)-linear equivalence of categories \[ \mathcal{A} \simeq A\textup{-}\textbf{Mod}_\textup{fd} , \] between \(\mathcal{A}\) and the category of \(A\)-modules which are finite-dimensional as vector spaces over \(k\). Such \(A\) is uniquely determined up to Morita equivalence.
