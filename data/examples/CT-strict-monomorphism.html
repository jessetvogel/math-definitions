Any strict monomorphism \(f : X \to Y\) is a monomorphism. Suppose that \(\alpha, \beta : Z \to X\) are morphisms with \(f \alpha = f \beta\). Then pick \(g = f \alpha = f \beta\) and note that \(xf = yf\) implies \(xg = yg\) for any \(x, y : Y \to W\). Hence there is a unique morphism \(h : Z \to X\) with \(g = fh\). Since \(\alpha\) and \(\beta\) both satisfy this property, it follows that \(\alpha = \beta\).
