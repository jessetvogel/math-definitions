Suppose for a contradiction that \(f : A \to P(A)\) is a surjective function, and let \(S = \{ x \in A \mid x \not\in f(x) \}\). Since \(f\) is surjective, there exists some \(x \in A\) with \(f(x) = S\). But now, by definition of \(S\), \[ x \in S \iff x \not\in f(x) = S , \] which is a contradiction.
