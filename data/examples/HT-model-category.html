The category \(\textbf{Top}\) of topological spaces can be given the structure of a model category by defining a map \(f : X \to Y\) to be a
  • weak equivalence if \(f\) is a weak homotopy equivalence,
  • cofibrations if \(f\) is a retract of a map \(X \to Y'\) in which \(Y'\) is obtained from \(X\) by attaching cells,
  • fibration if \(f\) is a Serre fibration.
In this model category, all objects are fibrant and CW-complexes are cofibrant.

Let \(R\) be a commutative ring, and let \(\textbf{Ch}_R = \textbf{Ch}_{\ge 0}(\textbf{Mod}_R)\) be the category of chain complexes of \(R\)-modules, in non-negative degree. Take
  • weak equivalences = quasi-isomorphisms,
  • fibrations = degreewise surjective maps,
  • cofibrations = degreewise injective maps with projective cokernel.
All objects are fibrant, and the cofibrant objects are the complexes which has in each degree a projective \(R\)-module.
In any model category, the fibrations are precisely the maps satisfying the right lifting property (RLP) w.r.t. acyclic cofibrations.
