Let \(\mathcal{S} \subset \mathcal{T}\) be a right admissible subcategory. We will show that the quotient \(\mathcal{T}/\mathcal{S}\) is equivalent to the orthogonal complement \(S^\perp\). Since \(\mathcal{S}\) is right admissible, any object \(X\) of \(\mathcal{T}\) fits in an exact triangle \[ \pi(X) \to X \to B_X \to \pi(X)[1] , \] where \(\pi : \mathcal{T} \to \mathcal{S}\) denotes a right adjoint to the inclusion \(i : \mathcal{S} \to \mathcal{T}\), and \(B_X\) lies in \(\mathcal{S}^\perp\).
