• A Haar measure on a discrete group \(G\) is the counting measure \(\mu(S) = |S|\).
  • A Haar measure on the circle group \(S^1\) is the measure \(\mu(S) = \frac{1}{2 \pi} m(\pi^{-1}(S))\), where \(f : [0, 2 \pi] \to S^1\) is the map \(f(t) = (\cos t, \sin t)\), and \(m\) is the Lebesgue measure on \([0, 2 \pi]\).
  • A Haar measure on the multiplicative group \((\RR_{> 0}, \cdot)\) is given by \(\mu(S) = \int_S \frac{dt}{t}\). For example, \(\mu([a, b]) = \log(b/a)\), and \(\mu([ca, cb]) = \log(cb/(ca)) = \log(b/a)\).
  • A Haar measure on the general linear group \(\textup{GL}_n(\RR)\) is given by \(\mu(S) = \int_S \frac{1}{|\det(X)|^n} dX\), where \(dX\) denotes the Lebesgue measure on \(\RR^{n^2}\).
