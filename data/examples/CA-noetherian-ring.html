The following are all noetherian rings.
  • Any field: their only ideal is \((0)\).
  • Any PID: every ideal is generated by a single element.
  • If \(R\) is noetherian, then so is \(R[x]\): this is Hilbert’s basis theorem.
The ring \(k[x_1, x_2, x_3, \ldots]\) is not noetherian. Namely, the sequence of ideals \[ (x_1) \subset (x_1, x_2) \subset (x_1, x_2, x_3) \subset \ldots \] is ascending, but does not stabilize.
