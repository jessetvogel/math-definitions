• For any scheme \(X\), taking \(\mathcal{M} = \mathcal{O}_X^*\) gives the trivial log structure on \(X\). It is the initial object in the category of log structures over \(X\).
  • For any scheme \(X\), taking \(\mathcal{M} = \mathcal{O}_X\) gives the final object in the category of log structures over \(X\).
  • For a scheme \(X\) and divisor \(D \subset X\), the divisorial log structure is given by \[ \mathcal{M}(U) = \{ f \in \mathcal{O}_X(U) : f \text{ is invertible outside $D$} \} \] for any open subset \(U \subset X\).
Let \(X = \Spec k[x, y]\). The map \(\NN^2 \to k[x, y] : (a, b) \mapsto x^a y^b\) induces a sheaf of monoids \[ \mathcal{M}' = \NN^2 \oplus \mathcal{O}_X^* \to \mathcal{O}_X , \quad (a, b, f) \mapsto x^a y^b f , \] which is not quite a log structure. The associated log structure is \[ \mathcal{M} = \mathcal{M}' \oplus_{\alpha^{-1} \mathcal{O}_X^*} \mathcal{O}_X^* . \] More generally, given a commutative ring \(R\) and commutative monoid \(P\), there exists a canonical log structure on the scheme \(X = \Spec(R[P])\). Namely, the natural map \(P \to R[P]\) induces a morphism of sheaves \(\alpha : \underline{P} \to \mathcal{O}_X\), where \(\underline{P}\) denotes the constant sheaf with values in \(P\). The associated log structure is \[ \mathcal{P} = \underline{P} \oplus_{\alpha^{-1} \mathcal{O}_X^*} \mathcal{O}_X^* . \]
