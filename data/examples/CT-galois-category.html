Let \(X\) be a connected topological space and \(x \in X\) a basepoint. The category \(\textbf{FCov}_X\) of finite coverings \(Y \to X\) together with the functor \(F : \textbf{FCov}_X \to \textbf{FSet}\) which maps \(Y \to X\) to the fiber \(Y_x\) is a Galois category.
Let \(k\) be a field, and \(\mathcal{C}\) the opposite of the category \(\textbf{SAlg}_k\) of free separable \(k\)-algebras, with \(F(A) = \textup{Alg}_k(A, k_s)\), where \(k_s\) is a separable closure of \(k\). Then \(\mathcal{C}\) with \(F\) is a Galois category.
Let \(\pi\) be a profinite group. The category \(\pi\textup{-}\textbf{set}\) of finite sets with a continuous \(\pi\)-action, and \(F : \pi\textup{-}\textbf{set} \to \textbf{Set}\) the forgetful functor, is a Galois category.
It is a theorem that any essentially small Galois category is equivalent to \(\pi\textup{-}\textbf{set}\) for the uniquely determined profinite group \(\pi = \textup{Aut}(F)\).
