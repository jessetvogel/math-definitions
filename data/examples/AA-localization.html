If \(R\) is a domain, then \(S = R - \{ 0 \}\) is a multiplicative set, and \(S^{-1} R\) is the field of fractions of \(R\).
For any commutative ring \(R\) and \(f \in R\), the set \[ S = \{ 1, f, f^2, \ldots \} \] is a multiplicative set, and the localization is \[ R_f := S^{-1} R = \left\{ \frac{a}{f^n} : a \in R, \; n \ge 0 \right\} . \]
For any commutative ring \(R\) and prime ideal \(\mathfrak{p}\), the set \[ S = \{ f \in R : f \not\in \mathfrak{p} \} \] is a multiplicative set, and the localization \[ R_\mathfrak{p} := S^{-1} R = \left\{ \frac{f}{g} : f, g \in R, \; g \not\in \mathfrak{p} \right\} \] is a local ring with maximal ideal \(\mathfrak{p} R_\mathfrak{p}\).
