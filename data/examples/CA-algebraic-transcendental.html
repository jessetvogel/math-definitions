The sum and product of algebraic elements is again algebraic. Namely, let \(\alpha, \beta \in \ell\) be algebraic elements. Then \([k(\alpha, \beta) : k(\alpha)]\) is the degree of the minimal polynomial of \(\beta\) over \(k(\alpha)\), which can be at most \([k(\beta) : k]\) since this minimal polynomial is a factor of the minimal polynomial of \(\beta\) over \(k\). It follows that \[ [k(\alpha, \beta) : k] = [k(\alpha, \beta) : k(\alpha)] \cdot [k(\alpha) : k] \le [k(\beta) : k] \cdot [k(\alpha) : k] \] is finite, and thus \(\alpha + \beta, \alpha \beta \in k(\alpha + \beta)\) must be algebraic over \(k\).
