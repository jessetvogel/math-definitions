An outer measure \(\mu^*\) on \(\Omega\) can be used to define a \(\sigma\)-algebra on \(\Omega\). Let \(\mathcal{A}^*\) be the set of subsets \(A \subset \Omega\) such that \[ \mu^*(Z) = \mu^*(Z \cap A) + \mu^*(Z \cap (\Omega \setminus A)) , \] for all \(Z \subset \Omega\). Then \(\mathcal{A}^*\) is a \(\sigma\)-algebra on \(\Omega\), and the restriction of \(\mu^*\) to \(\mathcal{A}^*\) is a complete measure.
