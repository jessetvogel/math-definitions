• The Pontryagin dual of \(\ZZ\) is \(S^1\), and vice versa.
  • The Pontryagin dual of \(\RR\) is \(\RR\) itself.
  • The Pontryagin dual of \(\ZZ/n\ZZ\) is \(\ZZ/n\ZZ\) itself.
The Pontryagin duality theorem states there is a canonical isomorphism between locally compact abelian topological groups \[ G \xrightarrow{\sim} \widehat{\widehat{G}}, \quad g \mapsto (\chi \mapsto \chi(g)) . \]
