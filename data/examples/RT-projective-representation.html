The universal (double) cover of the special orthogonal group \(\textup{SO}(3)\) is the special unitary group \(\textup{SU}(2)\). Hence, any representation \(\rho : \textup{SU}(2) \to \textup{GL}(V)\) descends to a projective representation \(\pi : \textup{SO}(3) \to \textup{PGL}(V)\), which can be lifted to an honest representation if and only if the dimension of \(V\) is odd. For example, the standard representation \(\textup{SU}(2) \to \textup{GL}(\CC^2)\) induces a projective representation \[ \textup{SO}(3) \to \textup{PGL}(\CC^2) \] which cannot be lifted to an honest representation.
