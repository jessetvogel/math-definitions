Let \(V = \textup{Map}(\RR, \RR)\) be the space of continuous functions from \(\RR\) to \(\RR\), and let \[ \begin{aligned} U_+ &= \{ f : \RR \to \RR \;|\; f(-x) = f(x) \textup{ for all } x \in \RR \}, \\ U_- &= \{ f : \RR \to \RR \;|\; f(-x) = -f(x) \textup{ for all } x \in \RR \} \end{aligned} \] be the subspaces of even and odd functions, respectively. Then \(U_+ \cap U_- = \{ 0 \}\) since for any \(f \in U_+ \cap U_-\) we have \(f(x) = f(-x) = -f(x)\) for all \(x \in \RR\). Furthermore, \(V = U_+ + U_-\) since for any \(f : \RR \to \RR\) we have \[ f(x) = \underbrace{\frac{f(x) + f(-x)}{2}}_{\in U_+} + \underbrace{\frac{f(x) - f(-x)}{2}}_{\in U_-} . \]
