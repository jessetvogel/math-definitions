Consider the following polyhedral cones in \(\RR^2\), given by \[ \sigma_1 = \RR_{\ge 0} \{ (1, 0), (0, 1) \}, \quad \sigma_2 = \RR_{\ge 0} \{ (\sqrt{2}, 1), (-1, \pi) \}, \quad \sigma_3 = \RR_{\ge 0} \{ (-1, 0), (1, 0) \} . \] Cones \(\sigma_1\) and \(\sigma_3\) are rational, while \(\sigma_2\) is not. Cones \(\sigma_1\) and \(\sigma_2\) are strongly convex, while \(\sigma_3\) is not. All cones have dimension \(2\). The faces of \(\sigma_1\) are \(\{ \{ 0 \}, \RR_{\ge 0} (1, 0), \RR_{\ge 0} (0, 1), \sigma_1 \}\).
