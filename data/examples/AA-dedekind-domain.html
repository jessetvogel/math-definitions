Dedekind domains can be characterized in a number of ways. The following are all equivalent.
  1. \(R\) is a Dedekind domain.
  2. \(R\) is noetherian, and for every prime ideal \(\mathfrak{p} \subset R\), the localization \(R_\mathfrak{p}\) is a discrete valuation ring.
  3. Every fractional ideal of \(R\) is invertible.
  4. Every non-zero ideal \(I \subsetneq R\) factors into a product of prime ideals.
  • Any field is a Dedekind domain.
  • The ring of integers \(\mathcal{O}_K\) of a number field \(K\) is a Dedekind domain.
  • The ring \(\ZZ[\sqrt{5}]\) is not a Dedekind domain as it is not integrally closed: the element \(\alpha = \frac{1 + \sqrt{5}}{2}\) satisfies \(\alpha^2 - \alpha - 1 = 0\).
