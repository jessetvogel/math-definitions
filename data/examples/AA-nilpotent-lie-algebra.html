• The Lie algebra given by \[ \mathfrak{g} = \left\{ \begin{pmatrix} 0 & x & y \\ 0 & 0 & z \\ 0 & 0 & 0 \end{pmatrix} \right\} , \] and the commutator bracket, is nilpotent. Namely, \([\mathfrak{g}, \mathfrak{g}]\) is the span of \(\begin{pmatrix} 0 & 0 & 1 \\ 0 & 0 & 0 \\ 0 & 0 & 0 \end{pmatrix}\), and \([\mathfrak{g}, [\mathfrak{g}, \mathfrak{g}]] = \{ 0 \}\).
  • The Lie algebra \(\mathfrak{sl}_2(\CC)\) is not nilpotent since \([\mathfrak{sl}_2(\CC), \mathfrak{sl}_2(\CC)] = \mathfrak{sl}_2(\CC)\).
