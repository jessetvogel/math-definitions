If the character \(\chi\) of \(\rho\) only takes real values, then the dual representation \(\rho^*\) is isomorphic to \(\rho\) itself. Namely, denote by \(\chi^*\) the character of \(\rho^*\), then \[ \langle \chi, \chi^* \rangle = \frac{1}{|G|} \sum_{g \in G} \chi(g) \chi(g^{-1}) = \frac{1}{|G|} \sum_{g \in G} \chi(g) \chi(g) = 1 , \] so the result follows from the first orthogonality theorem.
