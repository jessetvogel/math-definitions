Limits (and colimits) can be expressed in terms of Kan extensions. The limit of a functor \(F : \mathcal{C} \to \mathcal{D}\) is the same as \(\textup{Ran}_K F\), where \(K : \mathcal{C} \to \textbf{1}\) is the (unique) functor from \(\mathcal{C}\) to \(\textbf{1}\), the category with one object and one morphism. Similarly, the colimit \(\textup{colim } F\) is the same as \(\textup{Lan}_K F\).
Conversely, whereas the limit functor \(\lim : \mathcal{D}^\mathcal{C} \to \mathcal{D}\) (resp. colimit functor) is right adjoint (resp. left adjoint) to the diagonal functor \(\Delta : \mathcal{D} \to \mathcal{D}^\mathcal{C}\), the right Kan extension \(\textup{Ran}_K : \mathcal{D}^\mathcal{C} \to \mathcal{D}^\mathcal{E}\) (resp. left Kan extension) is right adjoint (resp. left adjoint) to the precomposition functor \((-) \circ K : \mathcal{D}^\mathcal{E} \to \mathcal{D}^\mathcal{C}\).
