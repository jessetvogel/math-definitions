The categories Set, Group, Ring, Top are all concrete, where the forgetful functor \(U\) is the functor that maps an object to its underlying set.
However, the category hTop of topological spaces where morphisms are homotopy classes of continuous maps, is not concrete. The obvious forgetful functor \(U : \textbf{hTop} \to \textbf{Set}\) is not faithful, since homotopic maps need not be equal. Moreover, it is proven that hTop cannot be made into a concrete category.
