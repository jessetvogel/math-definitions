The complex \[ \cdots \to 0 \to \ZZ \xrightarrow{\id} \ZZ \to 0 \to \cdots \] is contractible, which follows from taking \(k^0 : \ZZ \to \ZZ\) to be the identity.
Note that any contractible complex has zero homology, since homotopy equivalences preserve homology. However, not every complex with zero homology is contractible. Namely, the complex \[ \cdots \to 0 \to \ZZ \xrightarrow{\cdot 2} \ZZ \to \ZZ / 2\ZZ \to 0 \to \cdots \] has zero homology, but is not contractible since the sequence does not split.
