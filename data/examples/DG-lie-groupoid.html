• A Lie groupoid \(G \rightrightarrows *\) with one object is the same as a Lie group.
  • Given a Lie group \(G\) acting on a smooth manifold \(M\), the action groupoid is the Lie groupoid \(G \times M \rightrightarrows M\), where \(\operatorname{dom}(g, x) = x\) and \(\operatorname{cod}(g, x) = g \cdot x\).
  • The banal groupoid associated to a submersion \(X_0 \to Y\), is the Lie groupoid \(X_1 \rightrightarrows X_0\) with \(X_1 = X_0 \times_Y X_0\). The source and target maps define an equivalence relation \(X_1 \xrightarrow{(s, t)} X_0 \times X_0\).
