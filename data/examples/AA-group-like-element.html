If \(A = \mathcal{O}_G(G)\) for some algebraic group \(G\), then a group-like element \(a \in A\) corresponds to a group morphism \[ \chi : G \to \GG_m = \Spec(k[t, t^{-1}]), \quad t \mapsto a , \] known as a character, and vice-versa, any such character corresponds to a group-like element \(a = \chi^\#(t) \in A\).
For example, for \(G = \textup{GL}_n(k)\) the group of invertible matrices, the determinant \(\det \in \mathcal{O}_G(G)\) is a group-like element, since \(\det(AB) = \det(A) \det(B)\) for all \(A, B \in \textup{GL}_n(k)\).
