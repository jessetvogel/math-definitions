Any ring \(R\) is Morita equivalent to the ring \(\text{M}_n(R)\) of \(n \times n\) matrices with elements in \(R\), for any \(n > 0\). Indeed, take \[ R\text{-Mod} \to \text{M}_n(R)\text{-Mod}, \quad M \to R^{n \times 1} \otimes_R M \simeq M^n , \] where \(\text{M}_n(R)\) acts on \(M^n\) by matrix multiplication on the left. Inversely, take \[ \text{M}_n(R)\text{-Mod} \to R\text{-Mod}, \quad N \to R^{1 \times n} \otimes_{\text{M}_n(R)} N . \] Indeed these are inverse to each other since \[ R^{1 \times n} \otimes_{\text{M}_n(R)} R^{n \times 1} \simeq R \quad \text{and} \quad R^{n \times 1} \otimes_R R^{1 \times n} \simeq \text{M}_n(R) . \]
