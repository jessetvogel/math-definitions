Let \(f : M_1 \to M_2\) be a diffeomorphism. Then the graph \(\Gamma_f \subset M_1 \times M_2\) of \(f\) is a Lagrangian correspondence if and only if \(f\) is a symplectomorphism. Namely, the restriction of \(\pi_1^* \omega_1 - \pi_2^* \omega_2\) to \(\Gamma_f \cong M_1\) is precisely \(\omega_1 - f^* \omega_2\), so \(\Gamma_f\) is Lagrangian if and only if \(f^* \omega_2 = \omega_1\), that is, \(f\) is symplectic.
