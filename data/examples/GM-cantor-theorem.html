The cardinality of \(A\) is at most the cardinality of \(\mathcal{P}(A)\) since \(x \mapsto \{ x \}\) is an injection \(A \to \mathcal{P}(A)\). Conversely, suppose that there exists a surjective function \(f : A \to \mathcal{P}(A)\), and let \(B = \{ x \in A \;|\; x \not\in f(x) \}\). Then by surjectivity of \(f\) there exists some \(y \in A\) such that \(f(y) = B\). But then \(y \in B\) if and only if \(y \not\in f(y) = B\), which is a contradiction. Therefore, the cardinality of \(A\) is strictly smaller than that of \(\mathcal{P}(A)\).
