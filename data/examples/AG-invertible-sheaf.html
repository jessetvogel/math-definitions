Let \(D\) be a Cartier divisor on a scheme \(X\), represented by a collection \(\{ (U_i, f_i) \}\) of open subsets \(U_i\) and \(f_i \in K_X / \mathcal{O}_X^* (U_i)\), where \(K_X\) denotes the sheaf of rational functions. One defines the invertible sheaf \(\mathcal{O}_X(D)\) as the subsheaf of \(K_X\) generated as an \(\mathcal{O}_X\)-submodule by \(1/f_i\) on \(U_i\). Indeed this gives an invertible sheaf, and moreover this construction gives a canonical homomorphism of groups \[ \mathcal{O}_X(-) : \{ \textup{Cartier divisors} \} / \textup{linear equivalence} \to \textup{Pic}(X), \quad D \mapsto \mathcal{O}_X(D) . \] This map is injective on any scheme \(X\), and when \(X\) is integral it is also surjective.
