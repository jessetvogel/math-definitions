Any étale morphism is formally étale, but the converse does not hold. Namely, the morphism \(f : \Spec k \to \Spec k[x_1, x_2, \ldots] / (x_1^2, x_2^2, \cdots)\) is easily checked to be formally étale. However, since \(f\) is not locally of finite presentation, it cannot be étale.
A morphism is étale if and only if it is formally étale and locally of finite presentation.
