A topological space \(X\) is Hausdorff if for any two distinct points \(x, y \in X\) there exist disjoint open sets \(U, V \subset X\) such that \(x \in U\) and \(y \in V\).
Equivalently, this is the case if the diagonal \(\Delta = \{ (x, x) \in X \times X : x \in X \}\) is closed in \(X \times X\).
