Let \(\mathcal{A}\) be an abelian category. The homotopy category \(\textbf{K}(\mathcal{A})\) is defined to be the category whose objects are complexes in \(\mathcal{A}\), and morphisms are chain maps modulo homotopy. The derived category \(\textbf{D}(\mathcal{A})\) is obtained from \(\textbf{K}(\mathcal{A})\) by `pretending' any quasi-isomorphism is an isomorphism. Concretely, a morphism from \(A^\bdot\) to \(B^\bdot\) is given by a roof where \(f\) is a quasi-isomorphism, and we think of this roof as \(g \circ f^{-1}\) (even though \(f^{-1}\) might not exist). Technically, one localizes the category \(\textbf{K}(\mathcal{A})\) w.r.t. quasi-isomorphisms.
