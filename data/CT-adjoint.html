Let \(F : \mathcal{C} \to \mathcal{D}\) and \(G : \mathcal{D} \to \mathcal{C}\) be a pair of functors. We say that \(F\) is left adjoint to \(G\), or \(G\) is right adjoint to \(F\), denoted \(F \dashv G\), if there is a natural bijection \[ \Hom_{\mathcal{D}}(F(C), D) \xrightarrow{\sim} \Hom_{\mathcal{D}}(C, G(D)) \] for each object \(C\) in \(\mathcal{C}\) and \(D\) in \(\mathcal{D}\). Naturality means that for all \(f : C \to C'\) in \(\mathcal{C}\) and \(g : D' \to D\) in \(\mathcal{D}\), the following square commutes. Two maps \(f : F(C) \to D\) and \(g : C \to G(D)\) which correspond to each other are called transposes.
