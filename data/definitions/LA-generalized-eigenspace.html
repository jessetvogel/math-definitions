Let \(A : V \to V\) be a linear map, and \(\lambda\) an eigenvalue of \(A\). The generalized eigenspace corresponding to \(\lambda\) is the subspace \[ V_\lambda(A) = \{ v \in V \mid (A - \lambda I)^n v = 0 \textup{ for some } n \ge 1 \} , \] whose elements are called generalized eigenvector of \(A\) corresponding to \(\lambda\).
