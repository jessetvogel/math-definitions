A functor \(F : \mathcal{C} \to \mathcal{D}\) is said to be an equivalence of categories if there exists a functor \(G : \mathcal{D} \to \mathcal{C}\) and natural isomorphisms \(\mu : \id_\mathcal{C} \Rightarrow GF\) and \(\nu : \id_\mathcal{D} \Rightarrow FG\). In this case \(F\) and \(G\) are called pseudo-inverses of each other.
