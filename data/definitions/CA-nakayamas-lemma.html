Nakayama’s lemma states that if \(M\) is a finitely generated module over \(R\), and \(I \subset R\) an ideal such that \(IM = M\), then there exists an \(r \in R\) with \(r \equiv 1 \; (\text{mod } I)\) such that \(rM = 0\).
The following corollary is also known as Nakayama’s lemma: if \(M\) is a finitely generated module over \(R\), and \(I \subset R\) an ideal contained in the Jacobson radical \(\mathfrak{J}_R\) of \(R\) such that \(IM = M\), then \(M = 0\).
(This follows from the above by observing that \(r \equiv 1 \; (\text{mod } \mathfrak{J}_R)\) must be a unit, so \(M = r^{-1} r M = 0\).)
