Let \(X\) be a variety over a field \(k\). The group of algebraic cycles on \(X\) is the free abelian group on closed integral subvarieties of \(X\), \[ Z_*(X) = \bigoplus_{Y \subset X} \ZZ \cdot [Y] . \] Elements of this group are formal linear combinations \[ \sum_i n_i [Y_i] \] called algebraic cycles on \(X\).
An algebraic cycle is effective if \(n_i \ge 0\) for all \(i\).
An algebraic \(k\)-cycle is a formal linear combination of closed integral subvarieties of \(X\) of dimension \(k\).
