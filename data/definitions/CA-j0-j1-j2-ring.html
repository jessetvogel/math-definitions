Let \(R\) be a noetherian commutative ring. The regular locus \(\textup{Reg}(X)\) of \(X = \Spec R\) is the set of prime ideals \(\mathfrak{p}\) of \(R\) such that the localization \(R_\mathfrak{p}\) is regular.
The ring \(R\) is J-0 if \(\textup{Reg}(X)\) contains a nonempty open.
The ring \(R\) is J-1 if \(\textup{Reg}(X)\) is open.
The ring \(R\) is J-2 if \(\textup{Reg}(X)\) if any finitely generated \(R\)-algebra is J-1.
