Let \(\mathcal{D}\) be a \(k\)-linear triangulated category. An object \(E \in \mathcal{D}\) is called exceptional if \[ \Hom_\mathcal{D}(E, E[\ell]) = \left\{\begin{array}{cl} k & \text{ if } \ell = 0, \\ 0 & \text{ otherwise.} \end{array}\right. \] An exceptional sequence is a sequence \(E_1, \ldots, E_n\) of exceptional objects such that \[ \Hom_\mathcal{D}(E_i, E_j[\ell]) = 0 \qquad \textup{for $i > j$ and all $\ell$}. \] Such an exceptional sequence is strong if moreover \[ \Hom_\mathcal{D}(E_i, E_j[\ell]) = 0 \qquad \textup{for all $i, j$ and $\ell \ne 0$}. \] If \(\mathcal{D}\) is a triangulated category, such an exceptional sequence is full if the objects \(E_1, \ldots, E_n\) generate \(\mathcal{D}\).
