Let \(C : \textbf{Ab}_\Delta \to \textup{Ch}_{\ge 0}(\textbf{Ab})\) be the Moore complex functor. For \(A, B \in \textbf{Ab}_\Delta\), the Eilenberg–Zilber map is the natural transformation on chain complexes \[ \begin{aligned} \nabla_{A, B} : C(A) \otimes C(B) &\to C(A \otimes B) \\ a \otimes b &\mapsto \sum_{(\mu, \nu) \in \textup{Sh}(p, q)} \textup{sign}(\mu, \nu) (s_\nu(a) \otimes s_\mu(b)) , \end{aligned} \] for all \(a \in A_p\) and \(b \in B_q\), where the sum is taken over all \((p, q)\)-shuffles \((\mu, \nu) = (\mu_1, \ldots, \mu_p, \nu_1, \ldots, \nu_q)\), and \(s_\mu\) and \(s_\nu\) are given by compositions of the degeneracy maps \[ s_\mu = s_{\mu_p - 1} \circ \cdots \circ s_{\mu_1 - 1} \quad \textup{ and } \quad s_\nu = s_{\nu_q - 1} \circ \cdots \circ s_{\nu_1 - 1} . \] The Eilenberg–Zilber map descends to the normalized Moore complex \[ \nabla_{A, B} : N(A) \otimes N(B) \to N(A \otimes B) . \]
The Eilenberg–Zilber theorem states that for \(A, B \in \textbf{Ab}_\Delta\), the composition \(\Delta_{A, B} \circ \nabla_{A, B}\) equals the identity on \(N(A) \otimes N(B)\), and the composition \(\nabla_{A, B} \circ \Delta_{A, B}\) is homotopic to the identity on \(N(A \otimes B)\), where \(\Delta_{A, B}\) denotes the Alexander–Whitney map. In particular, the chain complexes \(N(A \otimes B)\) and \(N(A) \otimes N(B)\) are homotopy equivalent.
