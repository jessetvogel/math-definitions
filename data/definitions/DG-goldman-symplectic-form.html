Let \(\Sigma\) be a closed surface, and let \(G\) be a Lie group with Lie algebra \(\mathfrak{g}\). Let \(X = \Hom(\pi_1(\Sigma), G) / G\) be the \(G\)-character variety of \(\Sigma\) and \(G\). At any point \(\phi \in X\), the tangent space of \(X\) at \(\phi\) can be identified with the group cohomology group \(H^1(\pi_1(\Sigma), \mathfrak{g}_{\textup{Ad}(\phi)})\), where \(\pi_1(\Sigma)\) acts on \(\mathfrak{g}\) through \(\phi\) composed with the adjoint representation. The Goldman symplectic structure on \(X\) is the symplectic structure on \(X\) given at \(\phi\) by \[ \omega_\phi : H^1(\pi_1(\Sigma), \mathfrak{g}_{\textup{Ad}(\phi)}) \otimes H^1(\pi_1(\Sigma), \mathfrak{g}_{\textup{Ad}(\phi)}) \to H^2(\pi_1(\Sigma), \RR) \cong \RR , \] which is the composition of the cup product with a trace form on \(\mathfrak{g}_{\textup{Ad}(\phi)}\).
