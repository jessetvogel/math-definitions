A morphism \(f : X \to Y\) of schemes is syntomic at \(x \in X\) if there exists an affine open neighborhood \(U = \Spec A \subset X\) of \(x\) and an affine open \(V = \Spec B\) with \(f(U) \subset V\) such that the induced ring morphism \(B \to A\) is syntomic, i.e. of finite presentation, makes \(A\) into a flat \(B\)-module, and all the fiber rings \(A \otimes_B k(\mathfrak{p})\) are local complete intersections.
The morphism \(f\) is syntomic if it is syntomic at all points \(x \in X\).
