Let \(M\) be a smooth manifold and \(N \subset M\) a smooth submanifold, and let \(T_{N/M}\) be the normal bundle to \(N\) in \(M\). The tubular neighborhood theorem states that there exists an open neighborhood \(U \subset M\) of \(N\), an open neighborhood \(V \subset T_{N/M}\) of the zero section, and a diffeomorphism \(\phi : U \to V\) extending this zero section.
Such a neighborhood \(U\) is called a tubular neighborhood of \(N\) in \(M\).
