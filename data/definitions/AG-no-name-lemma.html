Let \(k\) be an algebraically closed field, and \(G\) a linear algebraic group over \(k\). Let \(X\) be a variety over \(k\), with a \(G\)-action which is generically free, i.e. there exists an open dense \(U \subset X\) such that the stabilizer of any point \(x \in U\) is trivial. Let \(\pi : V \to X\) be a vector bundle of rank \(r\), with a \(G\)-action on \(V\) such that \(\pi\) is \(G\)-equivariant and the action of any \(g \in G\) restricts to a linear map \(\pi^{-1}(x) \to \pi^{-1}(g \cdot x)\) for all \(x \in X\). Then the no-name lemma states that there exists a \(G\)-equivariant birational map \(\phi : V \dashrightarrow X \times \AA^r_k\), where \(G\) acts trivially on \(\AA^r_k\), such that commutes.
