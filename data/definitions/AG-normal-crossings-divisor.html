Let \(X\) be a scheme over a base scheme \(S\). A normal crossings divisor of \(X\) is a closed subscheme \(D\) of \(X\) such that for all \(x \in X\), there exists an integer \(n \ge 0\), a scheme \(U/S\), an étale morphism \(f : U \to X\) with \(x \in f(U)\), and an étale morphism \(g : U \to \AA^n_S\), such that \(f^* D = g^* Z\), where \(Z = V(x_1 x_2 \cdots x_n) \subset \AA^n_S\) is the union of the coordinate hyperplanes.
