Let \(F : \mathcal{C} \to \mathcal{D}\) and \(G : \mathcal{D} \to \mathcal{C}\) be adjoint functors with corresponding monad \(T = GF\). The functor \(G\) factors through the Eilenbergâ€“Moore category \(\mathcal{C}^T\) \[ \mathcal{D} \xrightarrow{\tilde{G}} \mathcal{C}^T \xrightarrow{\textup{forget}} \mathcal{C} , \] with \(\tilde{G}(D) = (G(D), (G \varepsilon) (D))\), where \(\varepsilon : FG \Rightarrow \id_\mathcal{D}\) denotes the counit of the adjunction. The adjunction is monadic if \(\tilde{G}\) is an equivalence of categories.
More generally, a functor \(G : \mathcal{D} \to \mathcal{C}\) is monadic if it has a left adjoint \(F : \mathcal{C} \to \mathcal{D}\) forming a monadic adjunction.
