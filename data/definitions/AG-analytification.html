Let \(X\) be a non-singular variety over \(\CC\). The set of complex points \(X(\CC)\) naturally has a topology, such that for any affine open \(U \subset X\) the topology on \(U(\CC) \subset \CC^n\) is given by the subspace topology of the Euclidean topology on \(\CC^n\). These charts give \(X(\CC)\) the structure of a complex manifold \(X^\textup{an}\), called the analytification of \(X\). Moreover, a morphism of non-singular complex varieties \(f : X \to Y\) induces a holomorphic map \(f^\textup{an} : X^\textup{an} \to Y^\textup{an}\), and this construction yields the analytification functor \[ (-)^\textup{an} : \textbf{SmVar}_\CC \to \textbf{Mfld}_\CC . \]
