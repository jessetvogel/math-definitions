Let \((\Omega, \mathcal{A}, \PP)\) be a probability space and \(X : \Omega \to (E, \mathcal{E})\) a random variable. A probability density function (PDF) for \(X\), with respect to a reference measure \(\mu\) on \((E, \mathcal{E})\), is a measurable function \(f : E \to \RR\) such that \[ \PP(X \in A) = \int_A f d \mu \] for all \(A \in \mathcal{A}\).
