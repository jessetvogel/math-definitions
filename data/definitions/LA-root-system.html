Let \(V\) be a finite-dimensional real or complex vector space with an inner product \((\cdot, \cdot)\). A root system \(\Phi\) in \(V\) is a finite set of non-zero vectors in \(V\), called roots, such that
  • (span) \(\Phi\) spans \(V\),
  • (multiples) if \(\alpha \in \Phi\), then \(n \alpha \in \Phi \iff n = \pm 1\),
  • (reflection) \(\beta - \frac{2 (\alpha, \beta)}{(\alpha, \alpha)} \alpha \in \Phi\) for all \(\alpha, \beta \in \Phi\),
  • (integrality) \(\frac{2 (\alpha, \beta)}{\alpha, \alpha}\) is an integer for all \(\alpha, \beta \in \Phi\).
