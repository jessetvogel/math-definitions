Let \(n \ge 1\) be a fixed number, and suppose \(X\) is a pointed topological space with \(\pi_i(X) = 0\) for \(i < n\). The Hurewicz theorem states that the reduced homology \(\tilde{H}_i(X) = 0\) for \(i < n\) and the Hurewicz homomorphism induces an isomorphism \[ H_n(x) \isom \left\{ \begin{array}{ll} \pi_1(X) / [\pi_1(X), \pi_1(X)] & \text{ if } n = 1 , \\ \pi_n(X) & \text{ if } n > 1 . \end{array} \right. \]
