Let \(f : X \to Y\) be a morphism of schemes over a scheme \(S\), and assume \(f\) is of finite type and quasi-separated. The valuative criterion states that \(f\) is proper if and only if for every diagram where \(R\) is a valuation ring and \(K\) its field of fractions, there exists a unique morphism \(\Spec R \to X\) making the diagram commute. If \(Y\) is locally noetherian, it suffices to check the criterion for discrete valuation rings \(R\).
More precisely, the existence of the dashed arrow corresponds to \(f\) being universally closed, and the uniqueness corresponds to \(f\) being separated.
