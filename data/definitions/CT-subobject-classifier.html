Let \(\mathcal{C}\) be a category with finite limits. A subobject classifier is a monomorphism \(t : 1 \to \Omega\), where \(1\) is a terminal object, with the property that for any monomorphism \(m : A \to B\), there is a unique arrow \(\phi : B \to \Omega\) such that there is a pullback diagram In particular, arrows \(\phi : B \to \Omega\) classify subobjects of \(B\), that is \(\textup{Sub}(-) \simeq \Hom_\mathcal{C}(-, \Omega)\).
