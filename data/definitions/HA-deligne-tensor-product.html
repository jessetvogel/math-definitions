Let \(\mathcal{A}\) and \(\mathcal{B}\) be finite abelian categories over a field \(k\). The Deligne tensor product of \(\mathcal{A}\) and \(\mathcal{B}\) is a finite abelian category \(\mathcal{A} \boxtimes \mathcal{B}\) together with a bifunctor \(\boxtimes : \mathcal{A} \times \mathcal{B} \to \mathcal{A} \boxtimes \mathcal{B}\) that is right exact in both variables, which is universal in the sense that for every bifunctor \(F : \mathcal{A} \times \mathcal{B} \to \mathcal{C}\) which is right exact in both variables, there exists a unique right exact functor \(G : \mathcal{A} \boxtimes \mathcal{B} \to \mathcal{C}\) and a unique natural isomorphism that \(F \isom G \circ \boxtimes\).
