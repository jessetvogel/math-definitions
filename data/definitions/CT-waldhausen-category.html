A Waldhausen category is a category \(\mathcal{C}\) together with two distinguished classes of morphisms, weak equivalences and cofibrations, such that
  • \(\mathcal{C}\) has a zero object \(0\),
  • all isomorphisms in \(\mathcal{C}\) are both weak equivalences and cofibrations,
  • weak equivalences and cofibrations are both closed under composition,
  • the morphism \(0 \to X\) is a cofibration for all objects \(X\) of \(\mathcal{C}\),
  • for any diagram \(Z \leftarrow X \xrightarrow{f} Y\) with \(f\) a cofibration, the pushout exists, and the induced morphism \(Z \to Z \coprod_X Y\) is a cofibration,
  • for any diagram where the vertical morphisms are weak equivalences and \(f\) and \(f'\) are cofibrations, the induced map \(Z \coprod_X Y \to Z' \coprod_{X'} Y'\) is a weak equivalence.
