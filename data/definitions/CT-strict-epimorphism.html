Let \(\mathcal{C}\) be a category. A morphism \(f : X \to Y\) in \(\mathcal{C}\) is a strict epimorphism if for all \(g : X \to Z\) with the property that \(fx = fy\) implies \(gx = gy\) for all \(x, y : W \to X\), there exists a unique \(h : Y \to Z\) such that \(g = hf\). In other words, \(f\) is a split epimorphism if it is the colimit of all parallel morphisms \(x, y : X \to Y\) that it coequalizes.
