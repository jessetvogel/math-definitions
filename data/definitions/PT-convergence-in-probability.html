Let \((\Omega, \mathcal{A}, \PP)\) be a probability space. A sequence of random variables \(X_n : \Omega \to \RR\) is said to converge in probability to a random variable \(X : \Omega \to \RR\), denoted \(X_n \xrightarrow{\PP} X\), if \[ \lim_{n \to \infty} \PP(|X_n - X| > \varepsilon) = 0 . \] for any \(\varepsilon > 0\).
