Let \(X\) be a topologically pseudomanifold of dimension \(m\), that is, a paracompact space with a filtration by closed subspaces \[ X = X_m \supset X_{m - 1} \supset \cdots \supset X_0 \supset X_{-1} = \varnothing \] with \(X_{m - 1} = X_{m - 2}\), such that for all \(x \in X_k \setminus X_{k - 1}\), there exists a neighborhood \(N_x\) of \(x\) in \(X\), a compact topologically pseudomanifold \(L = L_{m - k - 1} \supset \cdots \supset L_0\) of dimension \(m - k - 1\), and a homeomorphism \(\phi : N_x \to \RR^k \times C(L)\) (with \(C(L)\) the open cone of \(L\)) such that (i) \(\phi\) takes \(N_x \cap X_{k + i + 1}\) homeomorphically to \(\RR^k \times C(L_i)\) for \(0 \le i \le m - k - 1\), and (ii) \(\phi\) takes \(N_x \cap X_k\) homeomorphically to \(\RR^k \times \{ \textup{vertex} \}\).
A perversity is a function \(p : \ZZ_{\ge 2} \to \ZZ\) such that \(p(2) = 0\) and \(p(k + 1) - p(k) \in \{ 0, 1 \}\) for all \(k \ge 2\).
Given a perversity \(p\), a singular \(i\)-simplex \(\sigma : \Delta^i \to X\) is \(p\)-allowable if \(\sigma^{-1}(X_{m - k} \setminus X_{m - k - 1})\) is contained in the \((i - k + p(k))\)-skeleton of \(\Delta^i\) for all \(k \ge 2\). A singular \(i\)-chain \(\xi = \sum_j a_j \sigma_j\) is \(p\)-allowable if all singular \(i\)-simplices (with non-zero coefficient) appearing in \(\xi\) or \(\partial \xi\) are \(p\)-allowable.
The intersection chain complex of \(X\) (with perversity \(p\) and coefficients in \(A\)) is the subcomplex \(I^p C_\bdot(X; A) \subset C_\bdot(X; A)\) of \(p\)-allowable singular simplices. The intersection homology of \(X\) are the homology groups of this complex, \[ I^p H_i(X; A) = H_i(I^p C_\bdot(X; A)) . \]
