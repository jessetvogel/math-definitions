Let \(X\) be a topological space, \(\mathcal{U} = \{ U_i \}_{i \in I}\) an open covering of \(X\), and \(\mathcal{F}\) a sheaf on \(X\). Put a well-ordering on \(I\) and for convenience write \(U_{i_0 \cdots i_p}\) for \(U_{i_0} \cap \cdots \cap U_{i_p}\).
The Čech complex of \(\mathcal{F}\) with respect to \(\mathcal{U}\) is the complex \[ C^p(\mathcal{U}, \mathcal{F}) = \prod_{i_0 < \cdots < i_p} \mathcal{F}(U_{i_0 \cdots i_p}) \qquad p \ge 0 , \] with differential \[ d^p : C^p(\mathcal{U}, \mathcal{F}) \to C^{p + 1}(\mathcal{U}, \mathcal{F}) \] \[ (d^p \alpha)_{i_0 , \ldots , i_{p + 1}} = \sum_{k = 0}^{p + 1} (-1)^k \alpha_{i_0 , \ldots , \hat{i}_k, \ldots, i_{p + 1}} | _{U_{i_0 \cdots i_{p + 1}}} . \] For all \(p \ge 0\) the \(p\)-th Čech cohomology group of \(\mathcal{F}\) with respect to \(\mathcal{U}\) is the cohomology group \[ \hat{H}^p(\mathcal{U}, \mathcal{F}) = H^p(C^\bdot(\mathcal{U}, \mathcal{F})) . \]
