A likelihood ratio test is any hypothesis test that has a rejection region of the form \(\{ x \mid \lambda(x) \le c \}\) for some constant \(0 \le c \le 1\), where \(\lambda(x)\) denotes the likelihood ratio test statistic \[ \lambda(x) = \frac{\sup_{\theta \in \Theta_0} L(\theta; x)}{\sup_{\theta \in \Theta} L(\theta; x)} \] and where \(L(\theta; x)\) denotes the likelihood function.
