A homotopy equivalence between two topological spaces \(X\) and \(Y\) is a pair of continuous maps \(f : X \to Y\) and \(g : Y \to X\) such that \(g \circ f\) is homotopic to \(\id_X\) and \(f \circ g\) is homotopic to \(\id_Y\). If such a pair exists, then \(X\) and \(Y\) are said to be homotopy equivalent.
