Let \(R\) be a domain with field of fractions \(K\). Let \(\mathcal{I}(R)\) be the set of invertible ideals of \(R\), which forms a group under multiplication. The set of principal fractional ideals forms a subgroup \(\mathcal{P}(R) \subset \mathcal{I}(R)\), isomorphic to \(K^* / R^*\). The Picard group of \(R\) is the quotient \[ \text{Pic}(R) = \mathcal{I}(R) / \mathcal{P}(R) . \] It fits in the exact sequence \[ 0 \to R^* \to K^* \to \mathcal{I}(R) \to \text{Pic}(R) \to 0 . \]
