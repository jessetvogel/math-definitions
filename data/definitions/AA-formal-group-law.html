Let \(R\) be a commutative ring. A (1-dimensional) formal group law on \(R\) is a power series \(F(x, y) \in R\llbracket x, y \rrbracket\) such that
  • (linear terms) \(F(x, 0) = x\) and \(F(0, y) = y\),
  • (associativity) \(F(x, F(y, z)) = F(F(x, y), z)\).
Such a formal group law is called commutative if \(F(x, y) = F(y, x)\).
