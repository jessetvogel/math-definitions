Let \(S\) be a scheme. A stack \(\mathfrak{X}\) over \(\mathfrak{S} = \textbf{Sch}_S\) is an Deligne–Mumford stack
  • the diagonal \(\Delta_{\mathfrak{X}/S} : \mathfrak{X} \to \mathfrak{X} \times_S \mathfrak{X}\) is representable by algebraic spaces,
  • there exists an étale and surjective morphism \(X \to \mathfrak{X}\) for some scheme \(X\).
The morphism \(X \to \mathfrak{X}\) is called a presentation for \(\mathfrak{X}\).
