Let \((\mathcal{C}, \otimes, \textbf{1})\) be a symmetric monoidal \(\infty\)-category, and \(n \ge 0\) an integer. An \(E_n\)-algebra \(\mathcal{E}\) of \(\mathcal{C}\) consists of
  • an object \(\mathcal{E}(U) \in \mathcal{C}\) for each open set \(U \subset \RR^n\) homeomorphic to a disk,
  • for every embedding \(U_1 \sqcup \cdots \sqcup U_m \subset V\) of disjoint disks into another disk, a multiplication map \[ \mu : \bigotimes_{i = 1}^{m} \mathcal{E}(U_i) \to \mathcal{E}(V) . \]
It is required that the multiplication maps \(\mu\) are compatible with composition, and that \(\mu\) is an equivalence for \(m = 1\).
