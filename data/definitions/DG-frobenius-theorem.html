Let \(M\) be a smooth manifold. Frobenius theorem states there is a one-to-one correspondence between foliations on \(M\) and involutive distributions \(\mathcal{D} \subset TM\). In particular, a foliation \(\mathcal{F}\) corresponds to the involutive distribution \(\mathcal{D} = T \mathcal{F}\), where \(T_x \mathcal{F} = T_x L\) for any \(x \in M\) with \(L\) the leaf through \(x\).
