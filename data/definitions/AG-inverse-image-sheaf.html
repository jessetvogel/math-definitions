Let \(f : X \to Y\) be a map of topological spaces, and let \(\mathcal{G}\) be a sheaf on \(Y\). The inverse image sheaf \(f^ {-1}\mathcal{G}\) on \(X\) is defined as the sheaf associated to the presheaf given by \(U \mapsto \varinjlim_{V \supset f(U)} \mathcal{G}(V)\) for any open set \(U \subset X\).
This construction yields the inverse image functor \[ f^{-1} : \textup{Sh}(Y) \to \textup{Sh}(X) . \] It is the left adjoint of the direct image functor \(f_* : \textup{Sh}(X) \to \textup{Sh}(Y)\).
When \(f : X \to Y\) is a morphism of schemes, and \(\mathcal{G}\) an \(\mathcal{O}_Y\)-module, the inverse image sheaf \(f^* \mathcal{G}\) is the \(\mathcal{O}_X\)-module defined by \[ f^* \mathcal{G} = f^{-1} \mathcal{G} \otimes_{f^{-1} \mathcal{O}_Y} \mathcal{O}_X . \] Again we have an adjunction \(f^* \dashv f_*\).
