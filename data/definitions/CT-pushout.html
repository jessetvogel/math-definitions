Let \(f : X \to Y\) and \(g : X \to Z\) be morphisms in a category \(\mathcal{C}\). An object \(W\) of \(\mathcal{C}\) together with morphisms \(i_Y: Y \to W\) and \(i_Z : Z \to W\) is said to be a pushout of the pair \(f, g\) if \(i_Y \circ f = i_Z \circ g\) and for any other such \(W'\) with maps \(i'_Y\) and \(i'_Z\) there exists a unique morphism \(h : W \to W'\) such that \(i'_Y = h \circ i_Y\) and \(i'_Z = h \circ i_Z\).
