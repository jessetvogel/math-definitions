Let \(F\) be a free module of finite rank \(r\) over a commutative ring \(R\). Then, given an \(R\)-linear map \(s : F \to R\) the Koszul complex associated to \(s\) is the chain complex of \(R\)-modules \[ K_\bdot(s) : \qquad 0 \to \wedge^r F \xrightarrow{d_r} \wedge^{r - 1} F \xrightarrow{d_{r - 1}} \cdots \xrightarrow{d_2} F \xrightarrow{d_1} R \to 0 \] with the differentials given by \[ d_k(e_1 \wedge e_2 \ldots \wedge e_k) = \sum_{i = 1}^{k} (-1)^{i + 1} s(e_i) e_1 \wedge \cdots \wedge \hat{e_i} \wedge \cdots \wedge e_k , \] where the hat indicates the term is missing. Note that \(d_1 = s\).
