Let \(M\) be a smooth manifold and \(p\) a point of \(M\). A derivation at \(p\), or a tangent vector at \(p\), is a linear map \(v : C^\infty(M) \to \RR\) satisfying \[ v(fg) = f(p) v(g) + g(p) v(f) \] for all \(f, g \in C^\infty(M)\). The tangent space to \(M\) at \(p\) is the set of all derivations at \(p\), denoted \(T_p M\). It is a real vector space of the same dimension as the dimension of \(M\).
