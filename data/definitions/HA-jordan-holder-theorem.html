Let \(\mathcal{A}\) be an abelian category. The Jordan–Hölder theorem states that if an object \(A\) in \(\mathcal{A}\) has two composition series \[ 0 = A_0 \subsetneq A_1 \subsetneq \cdots \subsetneq A_n = A \qquad \text{and} \qquad 0 = B_0 \subsetneq B_1 \subsetneq \cdots \subsetneq B_m = A , \] then \(m = n\) and the composition factors \(A_i/A_{i - 1}\) are isomorphic to \(B_i/B_{i - 1}\) up to permutation.
