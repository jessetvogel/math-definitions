Let \(G\) be a group, \(H \subset G\) a subgroup and \(k\) any field. Frobenius reciprocity states there is an adjunction \[ \textup{Ind}_H^G \dashv \textup{Res}_H^G \] between the induced representation functor \(\textup{Ind}_H^G : \textbf{Rep}_k(H) \to \textbf{Rep}_k(G)\) and the forgetful functor \(\textup{Res}_H^G : \textbf{Rep}_k(G) \to \textbf{Rep}_k(H)\) between categories of \(k\)-representations of \(H\) and \(G\). It can be seen a particular case of the adjunction between restriction and extension of scalars.
