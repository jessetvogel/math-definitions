Let \(M\) be a \((2n + 1)\)-dimensional manifold. A contact structure on \(M\) is a distribution \(\xi \subset TM\) of codimension one, such that any (locally defined) \(1\)-form \(\alpha\) with \(\xi = \ker \alpha\) satisfies \[ \alpha \wedge \underbrace{d \alpha \wedge \cdots \wedge d \alpha}_{n \textup{ times}} \ne 0 . \] Such a \(1\)-form \(\alpha\) is called a contact form, and the pair \((M, \xi)\) is called a contact manifold.
