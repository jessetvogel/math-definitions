A deformation retraction of a topological space \(X\) onto a subspace \(A\) is a continuous map \[ F : X \times [0, 1] \to X \] such that \[ F(x, 0) = x, \quad F(x, 1) \in A \quad \text{ and } \quad F(a, 1) = a \] for all \(x \in X\) and \(a \in A\). It is a strong deformation retraction if moreover \[ F(a, t) = a \qquad \text{ for all } t \in [0, 1] . \]
