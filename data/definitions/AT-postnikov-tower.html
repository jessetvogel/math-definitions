Let \(X\) be a path-connected pointed space. A Postnikov tower of \(X\) is a commutative diagram of pointed spaces such that for every \(n \ge 1\),
  • the map \(X \to P_n X\) induces isomorphisms \(\pi_k X \to \pi_k P_n X\) for all \(k \le n\),
  • \(\pi_k P_n X = 0\) for all \(k > n\).
It is a theorem that a Postnikov tower of \(X\) exists, and it is unique up to weak homotopy equivalence.
