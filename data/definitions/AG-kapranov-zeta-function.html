Let \(X\) be a quasi-projective variety over a field \(k\). The Kapranov zeta function of \(X\) is given by \[ Z(X, t) = \sum_{n \ge 0} [\textup{Sym}^n X] t^n \in 1 + t \cdot \textup{K}(\textbf{Var}/k) \llbracket t \rrbracket , \] where \(\textup{Sym}^n X\) denotes the symmetric product of \(X\), that is, the GIT quotient \(X^n \sslash S_n\), and \(\textup{K}(\textbf{Var}/k)\) denotes the Grothendieck ring of varieties.
For any ring morphism \(\mu : \textup{K}(\textbf{Var}/k) \to R\), the motivic zeta function of \(X\) is given by \[ Z(X, t) = \sum_{n \ge 0} \mu([\textup{Sym}^n X]) t^n \in 1 + t \cdot R \llbracket t \rrbracket . \]
