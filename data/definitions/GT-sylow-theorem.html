Let \(G\) be a finite group with \(|G| = p^n \cdot m\) for a prime \(p\) and \(m \nmid p\) and \(n \ge 1\). The Sylow theorems state that
  • \(G\) contains a Sylow \(p\)-group.
  • If \(H\) and \(H'\) are Sylow \(p\)-groups of \(G\), then \(H' = gHg^{-1}\) for some \(g \in G\).
  • The number of distinct Sylow \(p\)-groups of \(G\) is \(1 \mod p\).
  • The number of distinct Sylow \(p\)-groups of \(G\) divides \(m\).
