Let \(G_0\) be a connected reductive algebraic group over \(\FF_q\) and let \(G = G_0 \times_{\FF_q} \overline{\FF}_q\). Let \(B \subset G\) be a Borel subgroup and let \(W\) be the Weyl group of \(G\) corresponding to a maximal torus \(T\) of \(B\).
The Bruhat decomposition provides an isomorphism \[ \mathcal{O} : W \xrightarrow{\sim} \{ G\textup{-orbits in } G/B \times G/B \}, \quad w \mapsto \{ (g_1 B, g_2 B) \mid g_1^{-1} g_2 \in B w B \} . \] For any \(w \in W\), the Deligneâ€“Lustzig variety \(X(w)\) is the intersection of \(\mathcal{O}(w)\) with the graph \(\Gamma_F\) of the Frobenius morphism \(F : G/B \to G/B\) induced by the Frobenius morphism \(F : \overline{\FF}_q \to \overline{\FF}_q, x \mapsto x^q\). That is, \[ X(w) = \mathcal{O}(w) \cap \Gamma_F = \{ g B \in G/B \mid B g^{-1} F(g B) = B w B \} . \]
