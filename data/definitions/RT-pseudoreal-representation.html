Let \(G\) be a group and \(\rho : G \to \textup{GL}(V)\) a complex representation. The representation \(\rho\) is called
  • real if \(\rho = \tau \otimes_\RR \CC\) for some real representation \(\tau : G \to \textup{GL}(U)\),
  • pseudoreal if it is not real, but \(\rho\) is isomorphic to its complex conjugate \(\overline{\rho} : G \to \textup{GL}(\overline{V})\),
  • complex if it is neither real nor pseudoreal.
