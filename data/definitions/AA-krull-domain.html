Let \(R\) be a domain and denote by \(P\) the set of prime ideals \(\mathfrak{p}\) of \(R\) of height \(1\). Then \(R\) is a Krull domain if
  1. the localization \(R_\mathfrak{p}\) is a discrete valuation ring for all \(\mathfrak{p} \in P\),
  2. \(R = \bigcap_{\mathfrak{p} \in P} R_\mathfrak{p}\), where the intersection is taken in the field of fractions of \(R\),
  3. every non-zero \(r \in R\) is contained in finitely many \(\mathfrak{p} \in P\).
