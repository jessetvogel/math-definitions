A Boolean algebra is a lattice \((P, \le)\) such that
  • (complements) for every \(x \in P\) there exists an element \(\neg x \in P\) such that \(x \vee \neg x = 1\) and \(x \wedge \neg x = 0\),
  • (distributivity) \(x \wedge (y \vee z) = (x \wedge y) \vee (x \wedge z)\) for all \(x, y, z \in P\).
A Boolean algebra \((P, \le)\) is complete if every subset \(A \subset P\) has a least upper bound and a greatest lower bound.
