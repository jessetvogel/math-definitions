Let \(f : M \to N\) be a smooth map between smooth manifolds. A critical point of \(f\) is a point \(p \in M\) such that the derivative \((df)_x : T_x M \to T_{f(x)} N\) has rank less than \(\dim N\).
A critical value of \(f\) is a point \(q \in N\) such that \(q = f(p)\) for some critical point \(p\) of \(f\).
A regular value of \(f\) is a point \(q \in N\) which is not a critical value.
