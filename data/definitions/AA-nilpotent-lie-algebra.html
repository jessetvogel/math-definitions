A Lie algebra \(\mathfrak{g}\) is called nilpotent if its lower central series \[ \mathfrak{g} \supset [\mathfrak{g}, \mathfrak{g}] \supset [\mathfrak{g}, [\mathfrak{g}, \mathfrak{g}]] \supset [\mathfrak{g}, [\mathfrak{g}, [\mathfrak{g}, \mathfrak{g}]]] \supset \cdots \] terminates in the zero algebra.
