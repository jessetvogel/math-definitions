Let \(A\) be a commutative ring, and \(B\) an \(A\)-algebra. The integral closure of \(A\) in \(B\) is the subring of \(B\) of elements which are integral over \(A\).
If the integral closure is equal to \(A\), then \(A\) is said to be integrally closed in \(B\). If the integral closure is equal to \(B\), the ring \(B\) is said to be integral over \(A\).
