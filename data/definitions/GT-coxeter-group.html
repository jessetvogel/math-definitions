A Coxeter group is a group with a presentation \[ W = \langle r_1, r_2, \ldots, r_n \;|\; (r_i r_j)^{m_{ij}} = 1 \rangle , \] where \(m_{ii} = 1\) and \(m_{ij} \in \{ 2, 3, \ldots \} \cup \{ \infty \}\) for \(i \ne j\), where \(m_{ij} = \infty\) means no relation of the form \((r_i r_j)^m = 1\) is imposed.
A Coxeter system is a pair \((W, S)\), where \(W\) is a Coxeter group, and \(S = \{ r_1, r_2, \ldots, r_n \}\) a set of generators.
The Coxeter matrix if a Coxeter system is the \(n \times n\) symmetric matrix with entries \(m_{ij}\).
