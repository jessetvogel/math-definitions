Let \(f : X \to Y\) be a morphism of schemes. Covering \(X\) and \(Y\) by affine opens \(V = \Spec A \subset Y\) and \(U = \Spec B \subset X\) such that \(f(U) \subset V\), consider the sheaf \(\widetilde{\Omega_{B/A}}\) on \(U\), the sheaf associated to the module \(\Omega_{B/A}\). Gluing these sheaves together gives \(\Omega_{X/Y}\), the relative sheaf of differentials.
Alternatively, consider the diagonal morphism \(\Delta_{X/Y} : X \to X \times_Y X\), and view \(X \xrightarrow{\sim{}} \Delta(X)\) as a closed subscheme of an open subset \(W\) of \(X \times_Y X\), with corresponding sheaf of ideals \(\mathcal{I}\). Then \[ \Omega_{X/Y} = \Delta_{X/Y}^*(\mathcal{I}/\mathcal{I}^2) . \]
