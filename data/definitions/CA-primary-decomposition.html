Let \(R\) be a commutative ring. A primary decomposition of an ideal \(I \subset R\) is an expression of \(I\) as a finite intersection of primary ideals, \[ I = \bigcap_{i = 1}^{n} \mathfrak{q}_i . \]
The primary decomposition is said to be minimal if (i) the \(\mathfrak{p}_i = r(\mathfrak{q}_i)\) are all distinct, and (ii) no \(\mathfrak{q}_i\) contains the intersection of the other primary ideals.
