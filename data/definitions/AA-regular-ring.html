A regular local ring is a commutative noetherian local ring such that the minimal number of generators of its maximal ideal is equal to its Krull dimension. Equivalently, a local ring \(R\) with maximal ideal \(\mathfrak{m}\) and residue field \(k = R / \mathfrak{m}\) is regular iff \(\dim_k(\mathfrak{m} / \mathfrak{m}^2) = \dim R\).
A regular ring is a commutative noetherian ring, such that the localization at every prime ideal is a regular local ring.
