Let \(M\) be a topological manifold. A chart on \(M\) is a pair \((U, \varphi)\) where \(U\) is an open subset of \(M\) and \(\varphi : U \to \tilde{U}\) is a homeomorphism from \(U\) to an open subset \(\tilde{U} \subset \RR^n\). An atlas for \(M\) is a collection of charts \(\{ (U_\alpha, \varphi_\alpha) \}\) such that the \(U_\alpha\) cover \(M\).
An atlas is smooth if any two charts \((U, \varphi)\) and \((V, \psi)\) are smoothly compatible, that is, either \(U \cap V = \varnothing\) or the transition map \[ \psi \circ \varphi^{-1} : \varphi(U \cap V) \to \psi(U \cap V) \] is smooth: all its continuous partial derivatives exist.
A smooth atlas is maximal if it is not contained in any strictly larger smooth atlas.
