The suspension of a topological space \(X\) is the quotient space \[ S X = X \times [0, 1] / \sim{} , \] where the equivalence relation is generated by \((x_1, 0) \sim{} (x_2, 0)\) and \((x_1, 1) \sim{} (x_2, 1)\).
If \(X\) has a basepoint \(x \in X\), the reduced suspension of \(X\) is quotient space \[ \Sigma X = (X \times [0, 1]) / (X \times \{ 0, 1 \} \cup \{ x \} \times [0, 1]) , \] which is the same as the smash product \(X \wedge S^1\).
