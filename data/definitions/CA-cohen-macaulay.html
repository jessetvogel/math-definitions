A finitely generated module \(M\) over a commutative noetherian local ring \(R\) is Cohen–Macaulay if its depth \(\text{depth}_{\mathfrak{m}}(M)\) equals its dimension \(\dim_R(M) := \dim(R/\text{Ann}_R(M))\).
More generally, for a commutative noetherian ring \(R\), a finitely generated \(R\)-module \(M\) is Cohen–Macaulay if the localization \(M_\mathfrak{m}\) is Cohen–Macaulay over \(R_\mathfrak{m}\) for each maximal ideal \(\mathfrak{m}\) of \(R\).
A commutative noetherian ring \(R\) is Cohen–Macaulay if it is so as a module over itself.
