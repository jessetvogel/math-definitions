Let \(S\) be a variety over a field \(k\). The Grothendieck ring of varieties over \(S\), denoted \(\textup{K}(\textbf{Var}/S)\), is defined as the quotient of the free abelian group on the isomorphism classes of varieties over \(S\), by relations of the form \[ [X] = [Z] + [U] , \] where \(Z \subset X\) is a closed subvariety and \(U = X \backslash Z\) its open complement. Multiplication is defined by \[ [X] \cdot [Y] = [(X \times_S Y)_\textup{red}] \] and extended linearly.
