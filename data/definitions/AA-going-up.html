Let \(A\) be a commutative ring, and \(B\) an \(A\)-algebra. The ring extension \(A \subset B\) satisfies the going-up property if whenever \[ \mathfrak{p}_1 \subset \mathfrak{p}_2 \subset \cdots \subset \mathfrak{p}_n \quad \textup{and} \quad \mathfrak{q}_1 \subset \mathfrak{q}_2 \subset \cdots \subset \mathfrak{q}_m \] are chains of prime ideals of \(A\) and \(B\), respectively, with \(m < n\) such that \(\mathfrak{q}_i\) lies over \(\mathfrak{p}_i\) (that is, \(\mathfrak{q}_i \cap A = \mathfrak{p}_i\)) for each \(1 \le i \le m\), the latter chain can be extended to a chain \(\mathfrak{q}_1 \subset \cdots \subset \mathfrak{q}_m\) so that \(\mathfrak{q}_i\) lies over \(\mathfrak{p}_i\) for all \(1 \le i \le n\).
The going-up theorem states that any integral extension \(A \subset B\) satisfies the going-up property.
