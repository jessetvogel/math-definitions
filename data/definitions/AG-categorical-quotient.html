Let \(G\) be a group scheme acting on a scheme \(X\) via \(\sigma : G \times X \to X\). A categorical quotient for the action is a morphism \(\pi : X \to Y\) such that is a coequalizer diagram. That is, the diagram commutes and for every \(f : X \to Z\) with \(f \circ \sigma = f \circ \pi_X\) there exists a unique \(g : Y \to Z\) such that \(f = g \circ \pi\). Note that a categorical quotient (if it exists) is unique up to unique isomorphism.
