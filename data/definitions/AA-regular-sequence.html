Let \(R\) be a commutative ring, and \(M\) an \(R\)-module. An \(M\)-regular sequence is a sequence \[ r_1, r_2, \ldots, r_d \in R \] such that \(r_i\) is not a zero-divisor on \(M/(r_1, \ldots, r_{i - 1})\). That is, if \(r_i m = 0\) for some \(m \in M / (r_1, \ldots, r_{i - 1})\), then \(m = 0\).
When \(M = R\), such a sequence is simply called a regular sequence.
