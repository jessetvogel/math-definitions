Let \(A\) be a Hopf algebra with comultiplication \(\Delta\). An invertible element \(R \in A \otimes A\) is called a universal \(R\)-matrix for \(A\) if it satisfies \[ (\Delta \otimes \id) R = R_{13} R_{23}, \quad (\id \otimes \Delta) R = R_{13} R_{12}, \quad \tau \circ \Delta (a) = R \Delta(a) R^{-1} \textup{ for all } a \in A , \] where \(\tau : A \otimes A \to A \otimes A\) is the flip map \(\tau(a \otimes b) = b \otimes a\), and furthermore \(R = \sum_i a_i \otimes b_i\), \(R_{12} = \sum_i a_i \otimes b_i \otimes 1\), \(R_{13} = \sum_i a_i \otimes 1 \otimes b_i\) and \(R_{23} = \sum_i 1 \otimes a_i \otimes b_i\).
