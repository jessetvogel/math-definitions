Let \(k\) be a commutative ring, and \(\lambda\) a partition of a non-negative integer \(n\). Define a Young tabloid of shape \(\lambda\) to be an equivalence class of Young tableaux on a Young diagram of shape \(\lambda\), where two Young tableaux are equivalent if one is obtained from the other by permuting the entries of each row. The symmetric group \(S_n\) acts naturally on the set \(\mathcal{T}\) of Young tabloids of shape \(\lambda\). The Specht module of \(\lambda\) is the \(k\)-submodule of \(\bigoplus_{[T] \in \mathcal{T}} k\) generated by the elements \[ E_T = \sum_{\sigma \in Q_T} \operatorname{sign}(\sigma) [\sigma(T)] , \] as \(T\) runs over all Young tableaux of shape \(\lambda\), where \(Q_T\) denotes the subgroup of \(S_n\) of permutations that preserve the columns of \(T\), and \([T]\) denotes the Young tabloid corresponding to \(T\).
