A Frobenius manifold is a tuple \((M, g, \mu, \eta)\) consisting of a complex manifold \(M\), a (complex) symmetric nondegenerate bilinear form \(g \in \Gamma(TM^{\otimes 2})\), a tensor \(\mu \in \Gamma({TM^*}^{\otimes 2} \otimes TM)\), and a vector field \(\eta \in \Gamma(TM)\), such that:
  • For every \(p \in M\), the tangent space \(T_p M\) is a Frobenius algebra with unit \(\eta_p\), multiplication given by \(v \cdot w = \mu_p(v, w)\) for all \(u, v \in T_p M\), and bilinear form \(g_p\).
  • The vector field \(\eta\) is flat for the Levi-Civita connection \(\nabla\), that is, \(\nabla \eta = 0\).
  • If \(c(X, Y, Z) = g(X \cdot Y, Z)\), then the \(4\)-tensor \((\nabla_W c)(X, Y, Z)\) is completely symmetric.
An Euler field on a Frobenius manifold is a vector field \(\mathcal{E}\) on \(M\) such that
  • \(\mathcal{E} \cdot g(\alpha, \beta) - g([\mathcal{E}, X], Y) - g(X, [\mathcal{E}, Y]) = D g(X, Y)\) for some constant \(D\),
  • \([\mathcal{E}, X \cdot Y] - [\mathcal{E}, X] \cdot Y - X \cdot [\mathcal{E}, Y] = d_1 X \cdot Y\) for some constant \(d_1\),
  • \(\nabla (\nabla \mathcal{E}) = 0\).
