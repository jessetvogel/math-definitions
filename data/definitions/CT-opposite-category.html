The opposite category \(\mathcal{C}^\textup{op}\) of a category \(\mathcal{C}\) is formed by reversing the morphisms. That is, the objects of \(\mathcal{C}^\textup{op}\) are the objects of \(\mathcal{C}\), and there is a morphism \(f^\textup{op} : Y \to X\) in \(\mathcal{C}^\textup{op}\) for each morphism \(f : X \to Y\) in \(\mathcal{C}\).
