Let \(X_1, \ldots, X_n\) be a random sample from a population \(F_\theta\) depending on some parameter \(\theta\) in a parameter space \(\Theta\). A hypothesis testing problem for \(\theta\) is a partition of \(\Theta\) into a subset \(\Theta_0 \subseteq \Theta\) and its complement \(\Theta \setminus \Theta_0\). The null hypothesis, denoted \(H_0\), states that \(\theta \in \Theta_0\), and the alternative hypothesis, denoted \(H_1\), states that \(\theta \in \Theta \setminus \Theta_0\).
A hypothesis test is a rule that specifies:
  1. for which sample values of \(X = (X_1, \ldots, X_n)\) the decision is made to accept \(H_0\) as true,
  2. for which sample values of \(X\) the decision is made to reject \(H_0\) and accept \(H_1\) as true.
The first subset of sample values is called the acceptance region and the second subset of sample values is called the rejection region.
