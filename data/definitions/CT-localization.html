Let \(\mathcal{C}\) be a category, and \(W\) a collection of morphisms of \(\mathcal{C}\). A localization of \(\mathcal{C}\) by \(W\) is a category \(\mathcal{C}\left[\frac{1}{W}\right]\) with a functor \(Q : \mathcal{C} \to \mathcal{C}\left[\frac{1}{W}\right]\) such that
  • \(Q(f)\) is an isomorphism for all \(f \in W\),
  • for any category \(\mathcal{D}\) and functor \(F : \mathcal{C} \to \mathcal{D}\) such that \(F(f)\) is an isomorphism for all \(f \in W\), there exists a unique functor \(G : \mathcal{C}\left[\frac{1}{W}\right] \to \mathcal{D}\) such that \(F \isom G \circ Q\).
