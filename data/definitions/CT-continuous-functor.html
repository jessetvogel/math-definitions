A functor \(F : \mathcal{C} \to \mathcal{D}\) is continuous if it preserves all small limits in \(\mathcal{C}\), that is, if for every functor \(G : \mathcal{I} \to \mathcal{C}\) with \(\mathcal{I}\) a small category, there is an isomorphism \[ F(\lim G) \simeq \lim(F \circ G) . \] Dually, a functor \(F : \mathcal{C} \to \mathcal{D}\) is cocontinuous if it preserves all small colimits in \(\mathcal{C}\).
