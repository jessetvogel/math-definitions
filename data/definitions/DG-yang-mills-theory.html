Let \((M, g)\) be a pseudo-Riemannian manifold, \(G\) a Lie group with Lie algebra \(\mathfrak{g}\), and \(\pi : E \to M\) a vector bundle on \(M\) with structure group \(G\).
A connection \(\nabla\) on \(E\) is a \(G\)-connection if locally \(\nabla = d + A\) with \(A \in \Omega^1(M; \mathfrak{g})\), considering \(\mathfrak{g} \subset \operatorname{End}(E)\). A \(G\)-connection is a Yang–Mills field if its curvature \(F_\nabla \in \Omega^2(M; \mathfrak{g})\) satisfies the Yang–Mills equations \[ \nabla F_\nabla = 0 \quad \textup{ and } \quad \star \nabla \star F_\nabla = J , \] where \(J \in \Omega^1(M; \mathfrak{g})\) is a Yang–Mills current, and \(\star\) denotes the Hodge star operator. The first equation, also known as Bianchi’s identity, is always satisfied.
