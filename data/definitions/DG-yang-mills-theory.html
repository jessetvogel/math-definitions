Let \((M, g)\) be a pseudo-Riemannian manifold, \(G\) a Lie group with Lie algebra \(\mathfrak{g}\), and \(\pi : P \to M\) a principal \(G\)-bundle on \(M\).
A connection \(\nabla\) on \(P\) is a Yang–Mills field if the curvature \(F_\nabla \in \Omega^2(P \times_G \mathfrak{g})\), a \(2\)-form with values in the adjoint bundle, satisfies the Yang–Mills equations \[ \nabla F_\nabla = 0 \quad \textup{ and } \quad \star \nabla \star F_\nabla = J , \] where \(J \in \Omega^1(P \times_G \mathfrak{g})\) is a current, and \(\star\) denotes the Hodge star operator. Note that the first equation is the Bianchi identity, which is always satisfied.
