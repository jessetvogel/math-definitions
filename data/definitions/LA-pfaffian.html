Let \(A\) be a \(2n \times 2n\) skew-symmetric matrix. The Pfaffian of \(A\) is given by \[ \operatorname{pf} A = \frac{1}{2^n n!} \sum_{\sigma \in S_{2n}} \textup{sign}(\sigma) \prod_{i = 1}^{n} a_{\sigma(2i - 1), \sigma(2i)} . \] It satisfies \[ (\operatorname{pf} A)^2 = \det A . \] The Pfaffian of an \(n \times n\) skew symmetric matrix \(A\) with \(n\) odd is defined to be zero, as the determinant of such a matrix is zero: \[ \det A = \det A^T = \det (-A) = (-1)^n \det A = -\det A . \]
