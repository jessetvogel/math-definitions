Let \(n\) be a positive integer. Write \(a_\alpha = \det(x_i^{\alpha_j})_{i, j = 1}^{n}\) for any sequence \(\alpha = (\alpha_1, \ldots, \alpha_n)\). For any partition \(\lambda = (\lambda_1, \ldots, \lambda_n)\) of length at most \(n\), the Schur polynomial of \(\lambda\) is the polynomial \[ s_\lambda = a_{\lambda + \delta} / a_\delta \in \ZZ[x_1, \ldots, x_n] , \] where \(\delta = (n - 1, n - 2, \ldots, 1, 0)\).
In passing from \(n\) to \(n + 1\), it can be seen that \(s_\lambda(x_1, x_2, \ldots, x_n, 0) = s_\lambda(x_1, x_2, \ldots, x_n)\). Hence, there is a uniquely defined element \[ s_\lambda \in \Lambda := \bigoplus_{r \ge 0} \Lambda^r , \quad \textup{ where } \Lambda^r = \varprojlim_n \ZZ[x_1, \ldots, x_n]_{(r)}^{S_n} , \] called the Schur function of \(\lambda\).
