Let \(G\) be a complex reductive algebraic group acting linearly on a smooth complex projective variety \(X \subset \PP^n_\CC\). As \(G\) is complex reductive, \(G\) is equal to the complexification of its maximal compact subgroup \(K\). The restriction of the Fubini–Study form on \(\PP^n_\CC\) to \(X\) gives a symplectic form \(\omega\) on \(X\). After possibly rechoosing coordinates on \(\PP^n_\CC\), \(K\) acts unitarily on \(\PP^n_\CC\), inducing a symplectic action of \(K\) on \(X\) admitting a moment map \(\mu : X \to \mathfrak{k}^*\). The Kempf–Ness theorem states that \(\mu^{-1}(0) \subset X^\textup{ss}\) inducing a homeomorphism between the Marsden–Weinstein quotient and the projective GIT quotient \[ \mu^{-1}(0) / K \cong X \sslash G . \]
