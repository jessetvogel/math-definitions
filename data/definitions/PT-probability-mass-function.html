Let \((\Omega, \mathcal{A}, \PP)\) be a probability space and \(X : \Omega \to (E, \mathcal{E})\) a random variable which is discrete, that is, such that \(E\) is countable and \(\mathcal{E}\) contains all singletons. The probability mass function (PMF of \(X\) is the function \(f_X : E \to \RR\) given by \[ f_X(x) = \PP(X^{-1}(\{ x \})) \quad \textup{ for all } x \in E . \]
