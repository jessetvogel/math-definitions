Let \(\mathcal{C}\) be a category and \(F : \mathcal{C}^\textup{op} \to \textbf{Set}\) a functor. The Yoneda lemma states that for any object \(X\) of \(\mathcal{C}\) there is a bijection \[ \Hom(y_X, F) \xrightarrow{\sim} F(X) , \] where \(y_{(-)}\) denotes the Yoneda embedding, and that this bijection is natural in \(X\) and \(F\). That is, for any morphism \(f : X \to Y\) in \(\mathcal{C}\) and natural transformation \(\mu : F \Rightarrow G\), the diagram commutes.
