Let \((\mathcal{C}, \otimes, \textbf{1})\) be a symmetric monoidal category. An internal hom in \(\mathcal{C}\) is a functor \[ [-, -] : \mathcal{C}^\textup{op} \times \mathcal{C} \to \mathcal{C} \] such that for every object \(X\) of \(\mathcal{C}\) there is an adjunction \[ (-) \otimes X \dashv [X, -] . \] If such an internal hom exists, \(\mathcal{C}\) is called a closed monoidal category.
