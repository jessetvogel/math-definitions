Let \(X\) be a topological space and \(C = \{ X_i : i \in I \}\) a family of subsets \(X_i \subset X\) with the subspace topology. Then \(X\) is coherent with \(C\) if \(X\) has the finest topology such that the inclusions \(\iota_i : X_i \to X\) are continuous.
There are two equivalent definitions:
  • A subset \(A \subset X\) is closed if and only if \(A \cap X_i\) is closed for all \(i\).
  • A subset \(A \subset X\) is open if and only if \(A \cap X_i\) is open for all \(i\).
