A Lie algebra is is a vector space \(\mathfrak{g}\) over a field \(k\), together with an operation \(\mathfrak{g} \times \mathfrak{g} \to \mathfrak{g}, (x, y) \mapsto [x, y]\) called the Lie bracket, satisfying
  • (bilinearity) \([ax + by, z] = a[x, z] + b[y, z]\) and \([z, ax + by] = a[z, x] + b[z, y]\) for all \(x, y, z \in \mathfrak{g}\) and \(a, b \in k\),
  • (alternativity) \([x, x] = 0\) for all \(x \in \mathfrak{g}\),
  • (Jacobi identity) \([x, [y, z]] + [z, [x, y]] + [y, [z, x]] = 0\) for all \(x, y, z \in \mathfrak{g}\).
