Let \(M\) be a smooth manifold. The de Rham complex of \(M\) is the cochain complex \[ 0 \to \Omega^0(M) \xrightarrow{d} \Omega^1(M) \xrightarrow{d} \Omega^2(M) \xrightarrow{d} \cdots \] where \(\Omega^k(M)\) denotes the space of differential \(k\)-forms, and \(d\) the exterior derivative.
The de Rham cohomology groups \(H_\textup{dR}^k(M)\) are the cohomology groups of \(\Omega^\bdot(M)\).
