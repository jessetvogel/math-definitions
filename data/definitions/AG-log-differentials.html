Let \(f : (X, \mathcal{M}) \to (Y, \mathcal{N})\) be a morphism of log schemes. The \(\mathcal{O}_X\)-module \(\omega^1_{X/Y}\) of log differentials is the quotient of \[ \Omega_{X/Y}^1 \oplus (\mathcal{O}_X \otimes_\ZZ \mathcal{M}^\text{gp}) \] by the following relations of local sections:
  • \((d \alpha(a), 0) = (0, \alpha(a) \otimes a)\) for all \(a \in M\),
  • \((0, 1 \otimes a) = 0\) for all \(a \in \im(f^{-1} \mathcal{N} \to \mathcal{M})\).

Think of \(\omega^1_{X/Y}\) as an extension of the usual sheaf of differentials \(\Omega_{X/Y}^1\), where elements of the form \(d \log(a) = \frac{da}{a}\) (represented by \((0, 1 \otimes a)\)) are added for all \(a \in \mathcal{M}^\text{gp}\), up to the image of \(f^{-1} \mathcal{N}\). In fact, we have a morphism \[ d \log : \mathcal{M} \to \omega_{X/Y}^1, \quad f \mapsto d \log(f) . \]
