Let \(X\) be a Kan complex. For \(x \in X_0\) and \(n \ge 0\), the \(n\)th simplicial homotopy group of \(X\) at \(x\), denoted \(\pi_n(X, x)\), is the set of equivalence classes of maps \(\alpha : \Delta^n \to X\) sending the boundary \(\partial \Delta^n\) to \(x\), where two maps \(\alpha, \beta : \Delta^n\) are equivalent if there exists a simplicial homotopy \(\eta : \Delta^n \times \Delta^1 \to X\) which fixes the boundary: For \(n \ge 1\), the sets \(\pi_n(X, x)\) come equipped with the following group structure. For any \(f, g : \Delta^n \to X\), consider the map \(\Lambda^{n + 1}_n \to X\), where the \(i\)th face of \(\Lambda^{n + 1}_n\) is mapped to \[ v_i = \left\{ \begin{array}{cl} s_0 \circ \cdots \circ s_0 (x) & \textup{ for } 0 \le i \le n - 2, \\ f & \textup{ for } i = n - 1, \\ g & \textup{ for } i = n + 1. \end{array} \right. \] Since \(X\) is a Kan complex, this map extends to some \(\theta : \Delta^{n + 1} \to X\), and the group structure on \(\pi_n(X, x)\) is given by \[ [f] \cdot [g] = [d_n(\theta)] . \] The simplicial homotopy groups of any simplicial set are given by the simplicial homotopy groups of its Kan fibrant replacement.
