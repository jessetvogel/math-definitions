Let \(p : E \to B\) be a fiber bundle with fiber \(F\), and let \(G\) be a topological group acting continuously and faithfully on \(F\). Then \(G\) is called a structure group of the bundle if there exists a trivialization \(\{ (U_i, \varphi_{ij} : E|_{U_i} \xrightarrow{\sim} U_i \times F) \}\) such that the transition functions \(\varphi_i \circ \varphi_j^{-1}\) take values in \(G\).
In physics, the group \(G\) is called the gauge group of the bundle.
