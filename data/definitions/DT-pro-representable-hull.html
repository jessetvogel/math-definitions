Let \(F : \textbf{Art}_k \to \textbf{Set}\) be a deformation functor. A pro-representable hull for \(F\) is a pair \((R, \xi)\) with \(R\) a complete local \(k\)-algebra and \(\xi \in \hat{F}(R) = \varprojlim F(R / \mathfrak{m}_R^n)\) such that
  • the associated map \(h_R \to F\) is surjective, i.e. \(\Hom(R, A) \to F(A)\) is surjective for all \(A\),
  • every surjection \(B \to A\) in \(\textbf{Art}_k\) induces a surjection \(\Hom(R, B) \to \Hom(R, A) \times_{F(A)} F(B)\),
  • the map \(h_R(k[\varepsilon]/(\varepsilon^2)) \to F(k[\varepsilon]/(\varepsilon^2))\) of tangent spaces is bijective.
The pair \((R, \xi)\) is also called a miniversal family. If the pair \((R, \xi)\) only satisfies the first two conditions, it is called a versal family.
