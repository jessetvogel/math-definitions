Let \(G\) be a reductive algebraic group, acting on an affine variety \(X\), over an algebraically closed field \(k\) of characteristic zero. Let \(x \in X(k)\) be a smooth point, with stabilizer \(G_x\), whose orbit \(O_x\) is closed. Then Luna’s slice theorem states that there exists a smooth affine subvariety \(Y \subset X\) containing \(x\), invariant under \(G_x\), such that
  1. the tangent space at \(x\) decomposes as \(\mathcal{T}_{X, x} = \mathcal{T}_{Y, x} \oplus \mathcal{T}_{O_x, x}\),
  2. the \(G\)-equivariant map \[ \psi : G \times_{G_x} Y \to X, \quad (g, y) \mapsto g \cdot y , \] is étale, and its image \(U\) is open in \(X\),
  3. the map \[ Y/G_x \simeq (G \times_{G_x} Y) / G \xrightarrow{\psi / G} U / G \] is étale at \([x]\),
  4. the maps \(\psi\) and \(G \times_{G_x} Y \to (G \times_{G_x} Y) / G \to Y / G_x\) induce an isomorphism \[ G \times_{G_x} Y \simeq U \times_{U/G} (Y/G_x) . \]
The subvariety \(Y\) is called an étale slice at \(x\).
