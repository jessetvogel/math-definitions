A differential graded (dg) category \(\mathcal{A}\) over a commutative ring \(k\), is a category enriched over chain complexes of \(k\)-modules. Concretely, morphisms between objects \(A\) and \(B\) of \(\mathcal{A}\) form a chain complex, \[ \Hom_\mathcal{A}(A, B) = \bigoplus_{n \in \ZZ} \Hom^n_\mathcal{A}(A, B) , \] with differential \(d : \Hom^n_\mathcal{A}(A, B) \to \Hom^{n + 1}_\mathcal{A}(A, B)\) satisfying \(d^2 = 0\). Furthermore, composition of morphisms \[ \Hom_\mathcal{A}(A, B) \otimes_k \Hom_\mathcal{A}(B, C) \to \Hom_\mathcal{A}(A, C) \] must be a chain map.
