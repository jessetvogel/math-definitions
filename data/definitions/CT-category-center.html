The center of a monoidal category \((\mathcal{C}, \otimes, \textbf{1})\) is the category \(Z(\mathcal{C})\) defined as follows.
  • Its objects are pairs \((X, \mu)\) with \(X\) an object of \(\mathcal{C}\) and \(\mu : X \otimes (-) \Rightarrow (-) \otimes X\) a natural isomorphism, such that \(\mu_{Y \otimes Z} = (\id_Y \otimes \mu_Z) \circ (\mu_Y \otimes \id_Z)\) for all objects \(Y\) and \(Z\) of \(\mathcal{C}\).
  • A morphism from \((X, \mu)\) to \((Y, \nu)\) is a morphism \(f : X \to Y\) in \(\mathcal{C}\) such that \(\nu_Z \circ (f \otimes \id_Z) = (\id_Z \otimes f) \circ \mu_Z\) for all objects \(Z\) of \(\mathcal{C}\).
