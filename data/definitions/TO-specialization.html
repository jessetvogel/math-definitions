Let \(X\) be a topological space. If \(x, y \in X\) then we say \(y\) is a specialization of \(x\), (sometimes that \(x\) is a generalization of \(y\)) if \(y \in \overline{\{ x \}}\), i.e. \(y\) lies in the closure of \(x\). This is denoted as \(x \leadsto y\).
A subset \(T \subset X\) is said to be stable under specialization if for all \(x \in T\) and every specialization \(x \leadsto y\) we have \(y \in T\).
Similarly, a subset \(T \subset X\) is said to be stable under generalization if for all \(y \in T\) and every generalization \(x \leadsto y\) we have \(x \in T\).
