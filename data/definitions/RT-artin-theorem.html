Let \(G\) be a finite group, \(X\) a family of subgroups of \(G\), and \(k\) a field of characteristic zero. Artinâ€™s theorem states that the following are equivalent:
  1. \(G = \bigcup_{H \in X} \bigcup_{g \in G} g H g^{-1}\),
  2. the map \(\textup{Ind} : \bigoplus_{H \in X} R_k(H) \to R_k(G)\) on representation rings induced by the induced representations \(\textup{Ind}_H^G : R_k(H) \to R_k(G)\) has a finite cokernel,
  3. for each character \(\chi\) of \(G\), there exist \(f_H \in R_k(H)\) and \(d \ge 1\) such that \[ d \cdot \chi = \sum_{H \in X} \textup{Ind}_H^G(f_H) . \]
In particular, the set \(X\) of all cyclic subgroups of \(G\) satisfies \((i)\).
