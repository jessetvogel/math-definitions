Let \(\mathcal{A}\) and \(\mathcal{B}\) be abelian categories, assume that \(\mathcal{A}\) has enough projectives, and let \(F : \mathcal{A} \to \mathcal{B}\) be a right exact functor. Take \(M\) to be an object of \(\mathcal{A}\) and pick a projective resolution \(P^\bdot \to M[0]\). Then we define \[ L_i F M = H^i(F(P^\bdot)) . \] This is well-defined (independent of the chosen resolution) and yields additive functors \(L_i F : \mathcal{A} \to \mathcal{B}\), called the left derived functors of \(F\). Note that \(L_0 F \isom F\).
For each short exact sequence \(0 \to A \to B \to C \to 0\) in \(\mathcal{A}\) there is an associated long exact sequence \[ \cdots \to L_2 FC \to L_1 FA \to L_1 FB \to L_1 FC \to FA \to FB \to FC \to 0 \]
