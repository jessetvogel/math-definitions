Let \(X\) be a noetherian scheme, and \(Y \subset X\) a closed subscheme, defined by a sheaf of ideals \(\mathcal{I}\). The formal completion of \(X\) along \(Y\), denoted \((\hat{X}, \mathcal{O}_{\hat{X}})\), is the locally ringed space whose topological space \(\hat{X}\) is the topological space of \(Y\), and \(\mathcal{O}_{\hat{X}}\) is the inverse limit \(\varprojlim_n \mathcal{O}_X / \mathcal{I}^n\).
When \(X = \Spec R\) is an affine scheme and \(I \subset R\) an ideal, the formal completion is also known as the formal spectrum of \(R\) along \(I\), denoted \(\text{Spf}(R)\).
