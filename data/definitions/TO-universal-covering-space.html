A universal covering space of a topological space \(X\) is a covering space \(\pi : \tilde{X} \to X\) which is simply connected. It has the universal property that for any other connected covering \(p : Y \to X\) there exists a unique covering map \(f : \tilde{X} \to Y\) such that \(\pi = p \circ f\). A universal cover can explicitly be constructed as follows. Let \(x \in X\) be a basepoint, and let \(\tilde{X}_x\) be the set of homotopy classes of paths \(\gamma : [0, 1] \to X\) starting at \(x\), with \(\pi : \tilde{X}_x \to X\) given by \(\gamma \mapsto \gamma(1)\). There is a suitable topology on \(\tilde{X}_x\) such that \(\pi : \tilde{X}_x \to X\) is continuous and a universal covering space of \(X\).
