Let \(G\) be a finite group and \(\rho : G \to \textup{GL}(V)\) an irreducible representation with character \(\chi\). The Frobenius–Schur indicator of \(\rho\) is \[ \frac{1}{|G|} \sum_{g \in G} \chi(g^2) . \] The Frobenius–Schur indicator is equal to \(1\), \(0\) or \(-1\), corresponding to \(\rho\) being real, complex or pseudoreal, respectively.
