An inner product on a vector space \(V\) over \(k = \RR\) or \(\CC\) is a map \(\langle \cdot, \cdot \rangle : V \times V \to k\) satisfying
  • (linearity) \(\langle \alpha x + \beta y, z \rangle = \alpha \langle x, z \rangle + \beta \langle y, z \rangle\) for all scalars \(\alpha\) and \(x, y, z \in V\),
  • (conjugate symmetry) \(\langle x, y \rangle = \overline{\langle y, x \rangle}\) for all \(x, y \in V\),
  • (positive definiteness) \(\langle x, x \rangle > 0\) for all \(x \ne 0\) in \(V\).
A vector space together with an inner product is called an inner product space.
