Let \(X\) be a topological space and \(G\) a topological group. A principal \(G\)-bundle on \(X\) is a fiber bundle \(\pi : P \to X\) with a free and transitive action of \(G\) on \(P\), preserving the fibers of \(P\), such that for any \(x \in X\) and \(y \in P_x\) the map \(G \to P_x, g \mapsto g \cdot y\) is a homeomorphism.
