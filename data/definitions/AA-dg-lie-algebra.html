A differential graded Lie algebra (dgla) over a field \(k\) is a chain complex \((\mathfrak{g}_\bdot, d)\) of \(k\)-modules with a bilinear map \([\cdot, \cdot] : \mathfrak{g}_p \otimes \mathfrak{g}_q \to \mathfrak{g}_{p + q}\) satisfying
  • (graded derivation) \(d[x, y] = [dx, y] + (-1)^p [x, dy]\) for all \(x \in \mathfrak{g}_p\) and \(y \in \mathfrak{g}_q\),
  • (graded skew-symmetry) \([x, y] = -(-1)^{pq} [y, x]\) for all \(x \in \mathfrak{g}_p\) and \(y \in \mathfrak{g}_q\),
  • (graded Jacobi identity) \([x, [y, z]] = [[x, y], z] + (-1)^{pq} [y, [x, z]]\) for all \(x \in \mathfrak{g}_p\), \(y \in \mathfrak{g}_q\), and \(z \in \mathfrak{g}_r\).
