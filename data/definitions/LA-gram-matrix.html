Let \((V, \langle \cdot, \cdot \rangle)\) be an inner product space. The Gram matrix of a list of vectors \(v_1, \ldots, v_n \in V\) is the Hermitian matrix \[ G_{ij} = \begin{pmatrix} \langle v_1, v_1 \rangle & \langle v_1, v_2 \rangle & \cdots & \langle v_1, v_n \rangle \\ \langle v_2, v_1 \rangle & \langle v_2, v_2 \rangle & \cdots & \langle v_2, v_n \rangle \\ \vdots & \vdots & \ddots & \vdots \\ \langle v_n, v_1 \rangle & \langle v_n, v_2 \rangle & \cdots & \langle v_n, v_n \rangle \end{pmatrix} . \]
