In a category \(\mathcal{C}\), a span from an object \(X\) to an object \(Y\) is a diagram of the form \[ X \longleftarrow W \longrightarrow Y . \] If the category \(\mathcal{C}\) has pullbacks, we can compose spans and form the category of spans \(\textup{Span}(\mathcal{C})\). In fact this is naturally a \(2\)-category, where a \(2\)-morphism from \(X \leftarrow W \rightarrow Y\) to \(X \leftarrow V \rightarrow Y\) is given by a morphism \(W \to V\) such that commutes.
Dually, a cospan from an object \(X\) to an object \(Y\) is a diagram of the form \(X \rightarrow W \leftarrow Y\), and similarly if \(\mathcal{C}\) has pushouts, one can form the \(2\)-category of cospans \(\textup{Span}^\textup{op}(\mathcal{C})\).
