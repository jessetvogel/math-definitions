Let \(\mathcal{C}\) be a category. An endomorphism \(e : X \to X\) in \(\mathcal{C}\) is idempotent if \(e \circ e = e\).
An endomorphism \(e : X \to X\) in \(\mathcal{C}\) is split idempotent if there exist morphisms \(f : X \to Y\) and \(g : Y \to X\) such that \(f \circ g = \id_Y\) and \(g \circ f = e\).
