The \(\chi^2\)-distribution with \(n \in \ZZ_{\ge 0}\) degrees of freedom is the probability distribution of a sum of squares of \(n\) mutually independent standard normal distributions.
Explicitly, it is the probability distribution on \([0, \infty)\) with PDF \[ f(x) = \frac{1}{2^{n / 2} \Gamma(n / 2)} x^{n / 2 - 1} \exp(-x / 2) \] for \(x \in [0, \infty)\), where \(\Gamma\) denotes the gamma function.
