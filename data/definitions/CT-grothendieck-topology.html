Let \(\mathcal{C}\) be a category. A Grothendieck topology on \(\mathcal{C}\) consists of a family \(\text{Cov}(C)\) of sieves on \(C\) for every object \(C\) of \(\mathcal{C}\), called covering sieves, such that
  • the maximal sieve \(\text{mac}(C) = \{ \text{all } f : C' \to C \}\) is in \(\text{Cov}(C)\),
  • if \(R \in \text{Cov}(C)\) then for every \(f : C' \to C\), \(f^*(R) \in \text{Cov}(C')\),
  • if \(R\) is any sieve on \(C\) and \(S\) is a covering sieve on \(C\), such that for every \(f : C' \to C\) from \(S\) we have \(f^*(R) \in \text{Cov}(C')\), then \(R \in \text{Cov}(C)\).
