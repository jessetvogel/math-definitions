Let \(X\) and \(Y\) be schemes over a field \(k\). A Chow correspondence from \(X\) to \(Y\) is an element from the Chow group \(\textup{CH}_{\dim X}(X \times_k Y)\).
The category of Chow correspondences over \(k\) is the category \(\textup{Corr}(k)\) whose objects are smooth projective schemes over \(k\), and whose morphisms are given by Chow correspondences. Composition is given by \[ \beta \circ \alpha = \pi_{XZ*}(\pi_{XY}^*(\alpha) \cdot \pi_{YZ}^*(\beta)) \] for all \(\alpha \in \textup{CH}_{\dim X}(X \times_k Y)\) and \(\beta \in \textup{CH}_{\dim Y}(Y \times_k Z)\), and for any \(X\) the identity \(\id_X\) is given by the diagonal \(\Delta_X(X) \in \textup{CH}_{\dim X}(X, X)\).
