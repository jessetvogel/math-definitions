Let \(F_S\) be a free group on a set \(S\). For every \(x \in S\), the Fox derivative with respect to \(x\) is the unique function \[ \frac{\partial}{\partial x} : F_S \to \ZZ[F_S] \] satisfying \[ \frac{\partial}{\partial x}(y) = \left\{ \begin{array}{cl} 1 & \textup{ if } y = x \\ 0 & \text{ if } y \ne x \end{array} \right. \quad \textup{ and } \quad \frac{\partial}{\partial x}(g h) = \frac{\partial}{\partial x}(g) + g \frac{\partial}{\partial x}(h) \] for all \(y \in S\) and \(g, h \in G\).
