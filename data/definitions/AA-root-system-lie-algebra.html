Let \(\mathfrak{g}\) be a complex semisimple Lie algebra, and \(\mathfrak{h}\) a Cartan subalgebra. A root of \(\mathfrak{g}\) (relative to \(\mathfrak{h}\)) is an element \(\alpha \in \mathfrak{h}^*\) such that \[ \mathfrak{g}_\alpha = \{ x \in \mathfrak{g} : [h, x] = \alpha(h) x \textup{ for all } h \in \mathfrak{h} \} \] is non-empty. The root system of \(\mathfrak{g}\) (relative to \(\mathfrak{h}\)) is the set \(\Phi\) of all roots. In particular, \(\Phi\) is a root system in \(\mathfrak{h}^*\). The inner product on \(\mathfrak{h}^*\) is given by \[ \langle \alpha, \beta \rangle = \kappa_\mathfrak{g}(t_\alpha, t_\beta) \] for all \(\alpha, \beta \in \Phi\), where \(\kappa_\mathfrak{g}(t_\alpha, -)|_\mathfrak{h} = \alpha\) and \(\kappa_\mathfrak{g}\) denotes the Killing form.
