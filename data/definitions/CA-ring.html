A ring is an abelian group \(R\) with an operation called multiplication and an element \(1 \in R\) satisfying
  • (associativity) \(a(bc) = (ab)c\),
  • (distributivity) \(a(b + c) = ab + ac\) and \((a + b)c = ac + bc\),
  • (unit) \(1 \cdot a = a \cdot 1 = a\),
for all \(a, b, c \in R\).
A ring \(R\) is called commutative if moreover \(ab = ba\) for all \(a, b \in R\).
