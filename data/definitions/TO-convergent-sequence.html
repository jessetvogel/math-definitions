Let \(X\) be a topological-space. A sequence of points \((x_n)_{n \in \NN}\) in \(X\) is convergent if there exists a point \(x \in X\) such that for any open neighborhood \(U\) of \(x\), there exists some \(N \in \NN\) such that \(x_n \in U\) for every \(n \ge N\). The point \(x\) is called a limit point of the sequence.
