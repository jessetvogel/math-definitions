Let \(V\) be a vector space over a field \(k\), and let \(\omega : V \times V \to k\) be a skew-symmetric bilinear form on \(V\). The Heisenberg Lie algebra corresponding to \((V, \omega)\) is the Lie algebra \(V \oplus k\) whose Lie bracket is given by \[ [ (v, \alpha), (w, \beta) ] = (0, \omega(v, w)) . \]
