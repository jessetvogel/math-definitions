Let \(k\) be a field and let \(S = k[x_1, \ldots, x_n] / I\) for some ideal \(I\). A Schreyer resolution of an \(S\)-module \(M\) is an exact sequence \[ \Phi : \cdots \to F_i \xrightarrow{\varphi_i} F_{i - 1} \xrightarrow{\varphi_{i - 1}} \cdots \xrightarrow{\varphi_2} F_1 \xrightarrow{\varphi_1} F_0 \xrightarrow{\varphi_0} M \to 0 \] such that
  • every \(F_i = S^{\oplus n_i}\) is a free \(S\)-module with basis \(e_1, \ldots, e_{n_i}\),
  • \(\{ \varphi_i(e_j) : 1 \le j \le n_i \}\) forms a minimal Gr√∂bner basis for the image of \(\varphi_i\), for all \(i \ge 1\).

A Schreyer frame of \(M\) is a sequence of \(S\)-module morphisms \[ \Xi : \cdots \to F_i \xrightarrow{\xi_i} F_{i - 1} \xrightarrow{\xi_{i - 1}} \cdots \xrightarrow{\xi_2} F_1 \xrightarrow{\xi_1} F_0 \xrightarrow{\xi_0} M \to 0 \] such that
  • every \(F_i = S^{\oplus n_i}\) is a free \(S\)-module with basis \(e_1, \ldots, e_{n_i}\),
  • the sequence is exact at \(M\),
  • \(\{ \xi_i(e_j) : 1 \le j \le n_i \}\) is a minimal set of generators for the initial ideal \((\operatorname{lm}(f) : f \in \ker \xi_{i - 1})\) for all \(i \ge 1\).
In particular, if \(\Phi\) is a Schreyer resolution for \(M\), then defining \(\xi_0 = \varphi_0\) and \(\xi_i(e_j) = \operatorname{lm}(\varphi_i(e_j))\) for \(i > 0\) gives a Schreyer frame for \(M\).
