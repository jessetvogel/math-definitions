An \(\infty\)-category is a simplicial set \(\mathcal{C}\) such that every map of simplicial sets \(\Lambda^n_i \to \mathcal{C}\) with \(0 < i < n\) can be extended to a map \(\Delta^n \to \mathcal{C}\). One thinks of the vertices \(\mathcal{C}_0\) as the objects, the edges \(\mathcal{C}_1\) as the \(1\)-morphisms (with \(\id_x = s_0(x)\) and \(d_0(f) = \textup{cod}(f)\) and \(d_1(f) = \textup{dom}(f)\)), the triangles \(\mathcal{C}_2\) as a \(2\)-morphism from \(g \circ f\) to \(h\), etc.
A functor between \(\infty\)-categories is a map of simplicial sets.
An \((\infty, r)\)-category is an \(\infty\)-category for which all \(k\)-morphisms with \(k > r\) are invertible.
