A Euclidean ring is a domain \(R\) for which there exists a function \[ g : R^\times \to \ZZ_{\ge 0} \] such that for all \(a, b \in R\) with \(b \ne 0\), there exists \(q, r \in R\) with \(a = qb + r\) and either \(r = 0\) or \(g(r) < g(b)\).
That is, a ring in which one can perform division with remainder. The function \(g\) is used to say that the ‘remainder’ \(r\) is ‘smaller’ than the element \(b\) one divides by.
In particular, one can find the gcd of elements by means of the Euclidean algorithm.
