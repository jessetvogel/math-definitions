Let \(X\) be a compact oriented \(n\)-dimensional smooth manifold. Poincar√© duality states that there is a canonical isomorphism \[ H^k(X; \ZZ) \xrightarrow{\sim} H_{n - k}(X; \ZZ) \] which sends \(\omega\) to the cap product \([X] \frown \omega\), where \([X] \in H_n(X; \ZZ)\) denotes the fundamental class of \(X\). That is, \(\omega\) is sent to \[ (\eta \mapsto (\omega \smile \eta)([X])) \in \Hom(H^{n - k}(X; \ZZ), \ZZ) = H_{n - k}(X; \ZZ) . \]
