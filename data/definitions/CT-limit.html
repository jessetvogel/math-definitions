Given a functor \(F : \mathcal{C} \to \mathcal{D}\), a cone for \(F\) is an object \(D\) of \(\mathcal{D}\) together with a family of morphisms \(\mu_C : D \to F(C)\) for all objects \(C\) in \(\mathcal{C}\), such that for all \(f : C \to C'\) in \(\mathcal{C}\), commutes in \(\mathcal{D}\). A map of cones \((D, \mu) \to (D', \mu')\) is a map \(g : D \to D'\) such that \(\mu'_C g = \mu_C\) for all \(C\) in \(\mathcal{C}\). A limiting cone or a limit for \(F\) is a terminal object in the category of cones for \(F\).
Dually, one can define a cocone for \(F\) and colimiting cocones to define a colimit for \(F\).
