Let \(R\) be a ring. A subgroup under addition \(I \subset R\) is called a
  • left ideal if \(ra \in I\) for all \(r \in R\) and \(a \in I\),
  • right ideal if \(ar \in I\) for all \(r \in R\) and \(a \in I\),
  • two-sided ideal if \(I\) is both a left and right ideal.
When \(R\) is commutative, these notions coincide, and \(I\) is simply called an ideal.
