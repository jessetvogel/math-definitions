Let \((T, \mu, \nu)\) be a monad on a category \(\mathcal{C}\). The Kleisli category of \(T\) is the category \(\mathcal{C}_T\) whose objects are the objects of \(\mathcal{C}\), and a morphism from \(X\) to \(Y\) in \(\mathcal{C}_T\) is given by a morphism \(X \to TY\) in \(\mathcal{C}\). Composition is given by \[ (Y \xrightarrow{g} TZ) \circ_T (X \xrightarrow{f} TY) = \mu_Z \circ Tg \circ f : X \to TY \to T^2 Z \to TZ . \] The identity morphisms are given by \(\id_X = \eta_X\).
