Let \(F : \textbf{Art}_k \to \textbf{Set}\) be a deformation functor. The following conditions are known as Schlessinger’s criteria:
  • H1: The map \(F(A' \times_A A'') \to F(A') \times_{F(A)} F(A'')\) is surjective for every small extension \(A'' \to A\).
  • H2: The map of H1 is bijective for \(A'' = k[\varepsilon] / (\varepsilon^2)\) and \(A = k\).
  • H3: The tangent space \(t_F = F(k[\varepsilon]/(\varepsilon^2))\) is a finite-dimensional \(k\)-vector space.
  • H4: For every small extension \(p : A'' \to A\) and every \(\eta \in F(A)\) for which \(p^{-1}(\eta)\) is non-empty, the group action of \(t_F\) on \(p^{-1}(\eta)\) is bijective.
Schlessinger’s theorem states that \(F\) has a pro-representable hull if and only if it satisfies H1-H3. Furthermore, \(F\) is pro-representable (i.e. representable by a complete local \(k\)-algebra) if and only if it satisfies H1-H4.
