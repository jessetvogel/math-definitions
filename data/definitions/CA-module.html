Let \(R\) be a commutative ring. An \(R\)-module is an abelian group \(M\) with an action of \(R\), that is a map \[ R \times M \to M, \qquad (a, m) \mapsto a \cdot m, \] satisfying
  • \(a \cdot (m + m') = a \cdot m + a \cdot m'\),
  • \((a + b) \cdot m = a \cdot m + b \cdot m\),
  • \(a \cdot (bm) = (ab) \cdot m\),
  • \(1 \cdot m = m\),
for all \(a, b \in R\) and \(m, m' \in M\).
