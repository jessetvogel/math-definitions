Let \(X_1, \ldots, X_n\) be a random sample from a population \(F_\theta\) depending on some parameter \(\theta\). If \(f(x; \theta)\) is a PDF of the sample \((X_1, \ldots, X_n)\), and given an outcome \(x = (x_1, \ldots, x_n)\), then the function (of \(\theta\)) \[ L(\theta; x) = f(x; \theta) \] is called the likelihood function.
