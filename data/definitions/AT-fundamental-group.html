Let \(X\) be a topological space, and take a point \(x_0 \in X\). The fundamental group of \(X\) w.r.t. the basepoint \(x_0\) is given by \[ \pi_1(X, x_0) = \{ \text{continuous } f : [0, 1] \to X : f(0) = x_0 = f(1) \} / \sim{} , \] where \(f \sim{} g\) if the paths are homotopic.
The group multiplication is given by concatenating paths, and inversion is given by inverting paths. In general, the fundamental group is not abelian.
