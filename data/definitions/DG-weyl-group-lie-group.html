Let \(G\) be a connected compact Lie group, and \(T \subset G\) a maximal torus. The Weyl group of \(G\) (relative to \(T\)) is the quotient group \[ W = N(T) / Z(T) , \] where \(N(T) = \{ g \in G \mid gTg^{-1} = T \}\) is the normalizer of \(T\) in \(G\), and \(Z(T) = \{ g \in G \mid gtg^{-1} = t \textup{ for all } t \in T \}\) is the centralizer of \(T\) in \(G\). In fact, \(Z(T) = T\), so that the Weyl group can be expressed as \(W = N(T) / T\).
