A variety \(X\) over a field \(k\) is non-singular at a point \(x \in X\) if the local ring \(\mathcal{O}_{X, x}\) is regular. The variety \(X\) is non-singular if it is non-singular at all points \(x \in X\).
Equivalently, \(X\) is non-singular if and only if the structure morphism \(X \to \Spec k\) is smooth.
