Let \(p : E \to B\) be a continuous map, and let \(A \subset X\) be a pair of topological spaces. Then \(p\) is said to have the homotopy lifting property (HLP) for \((X, A)\) if for any continuous map \(f : X \to E\), homotopy \(F : A \times [0, 1] \to E\) with \(F|_{A \times \{ 0 \}} = f|_A\), and homotopy \(G : X \times [0, 1] \to B\) with \(G|_{X \times \{ 0 \}} = p \circ f\) and \(G|_{A \times [0, 1]} = p \circ F\), there exists a homotopy \(H : X \times [0, 1] \to E\) such that \(H|_{A \times [0, 1]} = F\), \(H|_{X \times \{ 0 \}} = f\) and \(p \circ H = G\).
