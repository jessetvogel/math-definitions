Let \(R\) be a commutative ring and let \(M\) be a module over \(R\). The (first) syzygy module \(\operatorname{Syz}(f_1, \ldots, f_n)\) of generators \(f_1, \ldots, f_n \in M\) is the \(R\)-module consisting of all relations between these elements, that is, it is the kernel of \[ \bigoplus_{i = 1}^{n} R \to M, \qquad (r_1, \ldots, r_k) \mapsto r_1 f_1 + \cdots r_n f_n . \] Inductively, one can define the \(i\)-th syzygy module for any \(i \ge 2\) after choosing generators for the \((i - 1)\)-th syzygy module.
