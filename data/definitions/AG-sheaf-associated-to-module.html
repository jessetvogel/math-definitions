Let \(R\) be a ring and let \(M\) be an \(R\)-module. The sheaf associated to \(M\) on \(X = \Spec R\), denoted \(\tilde{M}\), is defined by the gluing data: to each distinguished open \(X_f = \{ f \ne 0 \}\) is assigned the localized \(R_f\)-module \(M_f\). For each \(X_f \subset X_g\) there is the natural map \(M_g \to M_f\). In particular, \(\tilde{R} = \mathcal{O}_X\).
For the projective case, let \(S\) be a graded ring and \(M\) a graded \(S\)-module. The sheaf associated to \(M\) on \(X = \Proj S\), denoted \(\tilde{M}\), is defined by the gluing data: for each homogeneous \(f \in S\), we have \(\tilde{M}|_{\{ f \ne 0\}} \isom (M_f)_0\).
