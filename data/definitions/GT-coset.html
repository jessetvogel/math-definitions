Let \(G\) be a group and \(H \subset G\) a subgroup. A left coset of \(H\) is a set of the form \(gH = \{ gh : h \in H \}\) for some \(g \in G\). A right coset of \(H\) is a set of the form \(Hg = \{ hg : h \in H \}\) for some \(g \in G\).
In particular, when \(H\) is normal, left and right cosets coincide since \(gH = Hg\) for all \(g \in G\).
