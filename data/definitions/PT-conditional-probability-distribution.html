Let \((\Omega, \mathcal{A}, \PP)\) be a probability space. Given events \(A, B \in \mathcal{A}\) with \(\PP(B) > 0\), the conditional probability of \(A\) given \(B\) is defined as \[ \PP(A \mid B) = \frac{\PP(A \cap B)}{\PP(B)} . \] Given a random variable \(X : \Omega \to (E, \mathcal{E})\), the conditional probability distribution of \(X\) given \(B\) is the probability measure \(\mu_{X \mid B}\) on \(E\) given by \[ \mu_{X \mid B}(A) = \PP(X^{-1}(A) \mid B) \quad \textup{ for } A \in \mathcal{E} . \] Given a sub-\(\sigma\)-algebra \(\mathcal{B} \subset \mathcal{A}\), a conditional probability of an event \(A \in \mathcal{A}\) given \(\mathcal{B}\) is a \(\mathcal{B}\)-measurable function \(\PP(A \mid \mathcal{B}) : \Omega \to \RR\) such that \[ \int_B \PP(A \mid \mathcal{B}) d \PP = \PP(A \cap B) \quad \textup{ for all } B \in \mathcal{B} . \] For any \(A \in \mathcal{E}\), define \(\mu_{X \mid \mathcal{B}}(A \mid \mathcal{B}) = \PP(X^{-1}(A) \mid \mathcal{B})\). For any outcome \(\omega \in \Omega\), the function \(\mu_{X \mid \mathcal{B}}(- \mid \mathcal{B})(\omega) : \mathcal{E} \to \RR\) is called the conditional probability distribution of \(X\) given \(\mathcal{B}\). If it is a probability measure on \(\mathcal{E}\), it is called regular.
