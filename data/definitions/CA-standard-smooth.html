Let \(A\) be commutative ring, and \(B = A[x_1, \ldots, x_n] / (f_1, \ldots, f_c)\) a finitely presented \(A\)-algebra. Then \(B\) is a standard smooth over \(A\) if the determinant \[ \det \begin{pmatrix} \frac{\partial f_1}{\partial x_1} & \frac{\partial f_1}{\partial x_2} & \cdots & \frac{\partial f_1}{\partial x_c} \\ \frac{\partial f_2}{\partial x_1} & \frac{\partial f_2}{\partial x_2} & \cdots & \frac{\partial f_2}{\partial x_c} \\ \vdots & \vdots & \ddots & \vdots \\ \frac{\partial f_c}{\partial x_1} & \frac{\partial f_c}{\partial x_2} & \cdots & \frac{\partial f_c}{\partial x_c} \end{pmatrix} \] maps to an invertible element in \(B\). Note that this definition is dependent on the presentation of \(B\).
