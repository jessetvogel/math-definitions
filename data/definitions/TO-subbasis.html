Given a topological space \(X\) with topology \(\mathcal{T}\), a subbasis for \(\mathcal{T}\) is a subfamily \(\mathcal{B} \subset \mathcal{T}\) such that \(\mathcal{B}\) generates \(\mathcal{T}\). That is, every open set in \(\mathcal{T}\) is a union of finite intersections of sets of \(\mathcal{B}\).
