Let \(f : X \to Z\) and \(g : Y \to Z\) be morphisms in a category \(\mathcal{C}\). An object \(W\) of \(\mathcal{C}\) together with morphisms \(\pi_X: W \to X\) and \(\pi_Y : W \to Y\) is said to be a fiber product of the pair \(f, g\) if \(f \circ \pi_X = g \circ \pi_Y\) and for any other such \(W'\) with maps \(\pi_X'\) and \(\pi_Y'\) there exists a unique morphism \(h : W' \to W\) such that \(\pi_X' = \pi_X \circ h\) and \(\pi_Y' = \pi_Y \circ h\).
