A diffeomorphism is a smooth map \(f : M \to N\) between smooth manifolds that has a smooth inverse \(f^{-1} : N \to M\).
A local diffeomorphism is a smooth map \(f : M \to N\) for which every point \(p \in M\) has a neighborhood \(U \subset M\) such that \(f(U)\) is open in \(N\) and \(f : U \to f(U)\) is a diffeomorphism.
The manifolds \(M\) and \(N\) are called diffeomorphic if there exists diffeomorphism between them.
