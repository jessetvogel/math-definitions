Let \(G\) be a locally compact Hausdorff topological group, considered with its Borel \(\sigma\)-algebra. A left Haar measure is a measure \(\mu\) satisfying
  • (left invariant) \(\mu(gS) = \mu(S)\) for all \(g \in G\) and Borel sets \(S \subset G\),
  • (compact sets) \(\mu(K) < \infty\) for all compact \(K \subset G\),
  • (outer regular) \(\mu\) is outer regular on Borel sets \(S \subset G\), that is, \[ \mu(S) = \inf \{ \mu(U) \;:\; S \subset U, \; U \subset G \textup{ open} \} . \]
  • (inner regular) \(\mu\) is inner regular on open sets \(U \subset G\), that is, \[ \mu(S) = \sup \{ \mu(K) \;:\; K \subset U, \; K \textup{ compact} \} . \]
