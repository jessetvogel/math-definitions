Let \(R\) be a ring. Given an endomorphism \(\sigma : R \to R\), a \(\sigma\)-derivation is a group morphism \(\delta : R \to R\) satisfying \[ \delta(rs) = \delta(r) s + \sigma(r) \delta(s) , \quad \textup { for all } r, s \in R . \] The Ore extension \(R[x; \sigma, \delta]\) is the (noncommutative) ring \(R[x]\), whose multiplication is determined by \[ xr = \sigma(r) x + \delta(r), \quad \textup{ for all } r \in R . \]
