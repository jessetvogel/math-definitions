Let \(k \supset \QQ\) be a commutative ring. Note that for any \(k\)-module \(M\), the symmetric group \(S_n\) acts naturally on the \(n\)-fold tensor product \(M^{\otimes n}\) by permuting the factors, turning it into a \(k[S_n]\)-module. Now, for any partition \(\lambda\) of an integer \(n \ge 0\), the Schur functor corresponding to \(\lambda\) is the functor \[ \mathbb{S}_\lambda : k\textup{-}\textbf{Mod} \to k\textup{-}\textbf{Mod}, \quad M \mapsto V_\lambda \otimes_{\QQ[S_n]} M^{\otimes n} , \] where \(V_\lambda\) is the irreducible representation of \(S_n\) corresponding to \(\lambda\).
