Let \(X_1, \ldots, X_n\) be a random sample from a population \(F_\theta\) depending on some parameter \(\theta\), and assume that the joint PDF \(f(x; \theta)\) of \(X = (X_1, \ldots, X_n)\) satisfies \[ \frac{d}{d \theta} \int h(x) f(x; \theta) d \mu = \int h(x) \frac{\partial}{\partial \theta} f(x; \theta) d \mu \] for any function \(h(x)\) with \(\EE_\theta |h(x)| < \infty\).
Then the Cramér–Rao bound states that any estimator \(W(X_1, \ldots, X_n)\) where \(\EE_\theta(W(X_1, \ldots, X_n))\) is a differentiable function of \(\theta\) satisfies \[ \operatorname{Var}_\theta(W(X)) \ge \frac{\left( \frac{d}{d \theta} \EE_\theta(W(X)) \right)^2}{\EE_\theta\left(\left(\frac{\partial}{\partial \theta} \log f(X; \theta)\right)^2\right)} . \]
