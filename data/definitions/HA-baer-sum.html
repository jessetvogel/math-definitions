Let \(\mathcal{A}\) be an abelian category, and \(0 \to A \xrightarrow{f} E \xrightarrow{g} B \to 0\) and \(0 \to A \xrightarrow{f'} E' \xrightarrow{g'} B \to 0\) be two short exact sequences. The Baer sum of these sequences is the sequence \[ 0 \to A \to \coker \left(A \xrightarrow{(f, -f')} E \oplus_B E'\right) \to B \to 0 , \] where the maps are given by \(a \mapsto (f(a), 0) = (0, f'(a))\) and \((e, e') \mapsto g(e) = g'(e')\).
Viewing the sequences as extensions, the Baer sum is given by addition in \(\textup{Ext}_\mathcal{A}^1(B, A)\).
