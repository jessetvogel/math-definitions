Let \(\Omega\) be a set. A Dynkin system on \(\Omega\) is a collection \(\mathcal{D}\) of subsets of \(\Omega\), satisfying
  • \(\Omega \in \mathcal{D}\),
  • \(\Omega \setminus A \in \mathcal{D}\) for all \(A \in \mathcal{D}\),
  • \(\bigcup_{n = 1}^\infty A_n \in \mathcal{D}\) for pairwise disjoint \(A_n \in \mathcal{A}\) with \(n \in \NN\).
