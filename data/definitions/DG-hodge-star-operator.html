Let \((M, g)\) be an \(n\)-dimensional Riemannian manifold. The Hodge star operators are the maps \[ \star : \Omega^k(M) \to \Omega^{n - k}(M) \] for \(0 \le k \le n\) defined by \[ \alpha \wedge \star \beta = g(\alpha, \beta) \textup{vol}_g \] for all \(\alpha, \beta \in \Omega^k(M)\), where \(\textup{vol}_g = \sqrt{|\det g|} \; dx_1 \wedge \cdots dx_n\) is the volume form induced by \(g\). Here \(g\) is defined on \(\Omega^k(M)\) by \[ g(\alpha, \beta) = \det\left(g(\alpha_i, \beta_j)\right)_{i, j = 1}^{k} \] for decomposable \(\alpha = \alpha_1 \wedge \cdots \wedge \alpha_k\) and \(\beta = \beta_1 \wedge \cdots \wedge \beta_k\) and extended linearly.
