Let \(G\) be a reductive algebraic group acting on an algebraic scheme \(X\) over a field \(k\), and let \(\mathcal{L}\) be a \(G\)-linearized invertible sheaf on \(X\). The projective GIT quotient of \(X\) by \(G\) with respect to \(\mathcal{L}\) is the morphism \[ \pi : X^\text{ss}(\mathcal{L}) \to X \sslash_\mathcal{L} G := \text{Proj}\left(\bigoplus_{n \ge 0} H^0(X, \mathcal{L}^n)^G \right) , \] where \[ X^\text{ss}(\mathcal{L}) = \left\{ x \in X : \exists s \in H^0(X, \mathcal{L}^n)^G \text{ for some $n \ge 0$ such that $s(x) \ne 0$ and $X_s$ is affine} \right\} \] denotes the semistable locus of \(X\). It is a uniform categorical quotient.
Furthermore, there exists an open subset \(U \subset X \sslash_\mathcal{L} G\) such that \(\pi^{-1}(U)\) equals the set of stable points \[ X^\text{s}(\mathcal{L}) = \left\{ x \in X : \begin{array}{c} \exists s \in H^0(X, \mathcal{L}^n)^G \text{ for some $n \ge 0$ such that $s(x) \ne 0$,} \\ \text{$X_s$ is affine and the action of $G$ on $X_s$ is closed} \end{array} \right\} \] and the restriction \(\pi|_{X^\text{s}(\mathcal{L})} : X^\text{s}(\mathcal{L}) \to U\) is a uniform geometric quotient.
