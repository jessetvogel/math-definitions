Let \(Q\) be a quiver. A path in \(Q\) is a sequence \(\alpha_n \alpha_{n - 1} \cdots \alpha_1\) of arrows such that the head of \(\alpha_{i + 1}\) equals the tail of \(\alpha_{i}\). The path algebra of \(Q\) is defined as a vector space with the set of paths of \(Q\) as a basis. Multiplication is given by concatenation of paths, and if two paths cannot be connected because their endpoints are different, their product is defined as zero.
