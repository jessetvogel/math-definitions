Let \(\mathfrak{g}\) be a complex semisimple Lie algebra, with Cartan subalgebra \(\mathfrak{h}\) and root system \(\Phi\). For a choice of positive roots \(\Phi^+\), let \(\mathfrak{b} \subset \mathfrak{g}\) be the (Borel) subalgebra generated by \(\mathfrak{h}\) and all \(\mathfrak{g}_\alpha\) with \(\alpha \in \Phi^+\). For any \(\lambda \in \mathfrak{h}^*\), the Verma module with highest weight \(\lambda\) is the \(U(\mathfrak{g})\)-module \[ V_\lambda = U(\mathfrak{g}) \otimes_{U(\mathfrak{b})} \CC_\lambda , \] where \(U(\mathfrak{g})\) denotes the universal enveloping algebra of \(\mathfrak{g}\), and \(\CC_\lambda\) is the one-dimensional \(U(\mathfrak{b})\)-module, where any \(h \in \mathfrak{h}\) acts by \(\lambda(h)\) and any \(x \in \mathfrak{g}_\alpha\) acts by \(0\).
The Verma module \(V_\lambda\) has the universal property that for any representation \(\pi : \mathfrak{g} \to \mathfrak{gl}(V)\) generated by a highest weight vector of weight \(\lambda\), there is a surjective map \(V_\lambda \to V\) of representations.
