Let \(R\) and \(S\) be commutative rings. A ring morphism \(R \to S\) is said to be of finite presentation if \(S\) is isomorphic to \(R[x_1, \ldots, x_n] / (f_1, \ldots, f_m)\) for some integer \(n\) and some \(f_i \in R[x_1, \ldots, x_n]\).
In this case, \(S\) is also said to be a finitely presented \(R\)-algebra.
