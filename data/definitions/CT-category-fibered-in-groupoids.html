A category \(\mathfrak{X}\) over \(\mathfrak{S}\) is called a category fibered in groupoids over \(\mathfrak{S}\) if for any \(f : T \to S\) in \(\mathfrak{S}\) and object \(x\) over \(S\), there exists a lift \(\overline{f} : y \to x\) of \(f\), which is unique up to unique isomorphism. That is, for any other lift \(\overline{f}' : y' \to x\) of \(f\), there exists a unique isomorphism \(\alpha : y' \to y\) such that \(\overline{f}' = \overline{f} \circ \alpha\).
