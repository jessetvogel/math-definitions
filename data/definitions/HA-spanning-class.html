Let \(\mathcal{T}\) be a triangulated category. A collection \(\Omega\) of objects in \(\mathcal{T}\) is a spanning class (or spans \(\mathcal{T}\)) if for all \(B \in \mathcal{T}\) we have
  • \(\Hom(A, B[i]) = 0\) for all \(A \in \Omega\) and \(i \in \ZZ\) implies \(B \simeq 0\),
  • \(\Hom(B[i], A) = 0\) for all \(A \in \Omega\) and \(i \in \ZZ\) implies \(B \simeq 0\).
