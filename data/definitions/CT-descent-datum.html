Let \(\mathfrak{S}\) be a category and \(\pi : \mathfrak{X} \to \mathfrak{S}\) a fibered category over \(\mathfrak{S}\). Let \(\mathcal{U} = \{ f_i : S_i \to S \}_{i \in I}\) be a family of morphisms in \(\mathfrak{S}\), and assume that all fiber products \(S_{ij} = S_i \times_S S_j\) and \(S_{ijk} = S_i \times_S S_j \times_S S_k\) exist. A descent datum \((x_i, \varphi_{ij})\) in \(\mathfrak{X}\) relative to \(\mathcal{U}\) consists of an object \(x_i\) in \(\mathfrak{X}\) over \(S_i\) for each \(i \in I\) and an isomorphism \(\varphi_{ij} : \pi_0^* x_i \to \pi_1^* x_j\) (where \(\pi_0 : S_{ij} \to S_i\) and \(\pi_1 : S_{ij} \to S_j\)) for each \(i, j \in I\), satisying the cocycle condition: for each \(i, j, k \in I\) the diagram in \(S_{ijk}\) commutes.
A morphism of descent data \(\psi : (x_i, \varphi_{ij}) \to (y_i, \phi_{ij})\) is a collection of morphisms \((\psi_i : x_i \to y_i)_{i \in I}\) in \(\mathfrak{X}_{S_i}\) (i.e. \(\pi(\psi_i) = \id_{S_i}\)) such that for all \(i, j \in I\) the diagram in \(S_{ij}\) commutes.
A descent datum \((x_i, \varphi_{ij})\) is effective if there exists an object \(x\) of \(\mathfrak{X}\) over \(S\) such that \((X_i, \varphi_{ij})\) is isomorphic to the canonical descent datum \((f_i^* x, \text{can})\).
