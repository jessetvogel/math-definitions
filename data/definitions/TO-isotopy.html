Let \(X\) and \(Y\) be topological spaces, and let \(f, g : X \to Y\) be two embeddings. An isotopy from \(f\) to \(g\) is a homotopy \(H : X \times [0, 1] \to Y\) from \(f\) to \(g\) such that \(H(-, t) : X \to Y\) is an embedding for all \(t \in [0, 1]\). If such an isotopy exists, \(f\) and \(g\) are called isotopic.
