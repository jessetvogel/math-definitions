A Frobenius algebra over a field \(k\) is a \(k\)-algebra \(A\) equipped with a bilinear form \(\beta : A \otimes_k A \to k\), called the Frobenius pairing, satisfying
  • (associativity) \(\beta(ab \otimes c) = \beta(a \otimes bc)\) for all \(a, b, c \in A\),
  • (non-degeneracy) there exists a \(k\)-linear map \(\gamma : k \to A \otimes_k A\) such that \[ (\beta \otimes \id_A)(a \otimes \gamma(1)) = a = (\id_A \otimes \beta)(\gamma(1) \otimes a) \] for all \(a \in A\).
