Let \(R\) be a commutative ring. A chain complex of \(R\)-modules is perfect if it is quasi-isomorphic to a bounded chain complex of finitely generated projective \(R\)-modules.
An \(R\)-module is perfect module if it is perfect when seen as a chain complex concentrated at degree zero.
A chain complex of quasi-coherent sheaves \(\mathcal{F}\) over a scheme \(X\) is perfect if the (derived) pullback \(L f^* \mathcal{F}\) is perfect for all commutative rings \(R\) and morphisms \(f : \Spec(R) \to X\).
