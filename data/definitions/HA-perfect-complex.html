Let \(R\) be a commutative ring and let \(\textbf{D}(\textbf{Mod}_R)\) be the derived category of \(R\)-modules. A complex in \(\textbf{D}(\textbf{Mod}_R)\) is perfect if it is quasi-isomorphic to a bounded complex of finitely generated projective \(R\)-modules.
An \(R\)-module is perfect module if it is perfect when seen as a complex concentrated at degree zero.
