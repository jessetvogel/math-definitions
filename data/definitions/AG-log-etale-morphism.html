Let \(f : (X, \mathcal{M}) \to (Y, \mathcal{N})\) be a morphism of fine log-schemes. Then \(f\) is formally log étale if for any commutative diagram with \(i\) an strict closed immersion (i.e. \(T' \to T\) a closed immersion and \(i^* \mathcal{L} \to \mathcal{L}'\) an isomorphism), and \(T' \subset T\) defined by an ideal \(I\) with \(I^2 = 0\), there exists a unique morphism \(g : (T, \mathcal{L}) \to (X, \mathcal{M})\) making the diagram commute.
The morphism \(f\) is log étale if it is formally log étale and the underlying morphism of schemes is locally of finite presentation.
