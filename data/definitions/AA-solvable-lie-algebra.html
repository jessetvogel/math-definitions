A Lie algebra \(\mathfrak{g}\) is solvable if the derived series of subalgebras \[ \mathfrak{g} = \mathfrak{g}_0 \supset \mathfrak{g}_1 \supset \mathfrak{g}_2 \supset \cdots \] given by \(\mathfrak{g}_{i + 1} = [\mathfrak{g}_i, \mathfrak{g}_i]\), results in the zero algebra. That is, \(\mathfrak{g}_i = \{ 0 \}\) for some \(i > 0\).
