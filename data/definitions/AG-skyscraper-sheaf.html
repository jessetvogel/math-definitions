Let \(X\) be a topological space, \(A\) an abelian group, and take a point \(x \in X\). The skyscraper sheaf \(i_x(A)\) at \(x\) with value \(A\) is defined as \[ i_x(A) (U) = \left\{ \begin{array}{cl} A & \text{if } x \in U, \\ 0 & \text{otherwise.} \end{array} \right. \] The stalks of this sheaf are \(A\) at any point in the closure of \(x\), and zero elsewhere.
Equivalently, it is the direct image sheaf \(i_*(\underline{A})\) for \(\underline{A}\) the constant sheaf determined by \(A\) on the closure \(\overline{\{ x \}}\) and \(i : \overline{\{ x \}} \to X\) the inclusion.
