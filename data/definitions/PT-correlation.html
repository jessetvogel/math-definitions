Let \((\Omega, \mathcal{A}, \PP)\) be a probability space and \(X, Y : \Omega \to \RR\) two random variables. The correlation of \(X\) and \(Y\) is \[ \operatorname{Cor}(X, Y) = \frac{\operatorname{Cov}(X, Y)}{\sqrt{\operatorname{Var}(X) \operatorname{Var}(Y)}} , \] where \(\operatorname{Cov}(X, Y)\) is the covariance of \(X\) and \(Y\), and \(\operatorname{Var}\) denotes the variance.
