Let \(G\) be a group scheme acting on a scheme \(X\) via \(\sigma : G \times X \to X\). A geometric quotient for the action is a morphism \(\pi : X \to Y\) satisfying
  1. \(\pi \circ \sigma = \pi \circ \pi_X\),
  2. \(\pi\) is surjective, and the image of \(\Psi = (\sigma, \pi_X) : G \times X \to X \times X\) is \(X \times_Y X\) (or equivalently, the geometric fibers of \(\pi\) are precisely the orbits of the geometric points of \(X\)),
  3. \(\pi\) is submersive, i.e. a subset \(U \subset Y\) is open iff \(\pi^{-1}(U) \subset X\) is open (i.e. \(Y\) has a quotient topology),
  4. the structure sheaf \(\mathcal{O}_Y\) is the subsheaf of \(\pi_* \mathcal{O}_X\) consisting of invariant functions, i.e. if \(\alpha \in \pi_* \mathcal{O}_X(U) = \mathcal{O}_X(\pi^{-1}(U))\) then \(\alpha \in \mathcal{O}_Y(U)\) if and only if commutes.
