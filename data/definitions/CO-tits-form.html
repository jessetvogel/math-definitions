Let \(Q\) be a quiver. The Tits form of \(Q\) is the quadratic form on \(\ZZ^{Q_0}\) given by \[ q_Q(d) = \langle d, d \rangle_Q = \sum_{i \in Q_0} d_i^2 - \sum_{(\alpha : i \to j) \in Q_1} d_i d_j \] for any \(d = (d_i)_{i \in Q_0}\), where \(\langle \cdot, \cdot \rangle_Q\) denotes the Euler form of \(Q\).
