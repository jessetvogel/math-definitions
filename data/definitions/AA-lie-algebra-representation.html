Let \(\mathfrak{g}\) be a Lie algebra over a field \(k\). A Lie algebra representation of \(\mathfrak{g}\) on a vector space \(V\) over \(k\) is a Lie algebra morphism \[ \pi : \mathfrak{g} \to \mathfrak{gl}(V) , \] where \(\mathfrak{gl}(V)\) denotes the Lie algebra of endomorphisms of \(V\). Concretely, \(\pi\) is a \(k\)-linear map such that \[ \pi([x, y]) = [\pi(x), \pi(y)] \] for all \(x, y \in \mathfrak{g}\).
