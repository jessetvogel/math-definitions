Let \(\pi : \mathfrak{X} \to \mathfrak{S}\) be a category fibered in groupoids over \(\mathfrak{S}\). The inertia of \(\mathfrak{X}\) is the fiber product \[ \mathcal{I}_\mathfrak{X} = \mathfrak{X} \times_{\mathfrak{X} \times \mathfrak{X}} \mathfrak{X} . \] More generally, for a morphism \(f : \mathfrak{X} \to \mathfrak{Y}\) of fibered categories over \(\mathfrak{S}\), the relative inertia of \(\mathfrak{X}\) over \(\mathfrak{Y}\) is the fiber product \[ \mathcal{I}_{\mathfrak{X}/\mathfrak{Y}} = \mathfrak{X} \times_{\mathfrak{X} \times_\mathfrak{Y} \mathfrak{X}} \mathfrak{X} . \] Explicitly, it can be described as the category fibered in groupoids over \(\mathfrak{S}\) whose
  • objects over \(S \in \mathfrak{S}\) are pairs \((x, \alpha)\) with \(x \in \mathfrak{X}_S\) and \(\alpha : x \to x\) a morphism with \(f(\alpha) = \id_{f(x)}\),
  • morphisms \((x, \alpha) \to (y, \beta)\) over \(g : T \to S\) are given by morphisms \(\phi : x \to y\) such that \(\pi(\phi) = g\) and \(\phi \circ \alpha = \beta \circ \phi\).
If \(\mathfrak{X}\) and \(\mathfrak{Y}\) are stacks over \(\mathfrak{S}\), then so are \(\mathcal{I}_\mathfrak{X}\) and \(\mathcal{I}_{\mathfrak{X}/\mathfrak{Y}}\), called the inertia stack of \(\mathfrak{X}\) and the relative inertia stack of \(\mathfrak{X}\) over \(\mathfrak{Y}\), respectively.
