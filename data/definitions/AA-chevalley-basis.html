Let \(\mathfrak{g}\) be a semisimple complex Lie algebra with Cartan subalgebra \(\mathfrak{h}\) and root system \((\mathfrak{h}^*, \Phi)\). For a choice of positive roots, let \(\Delta\) be the set of simple roots, and for every simple root \(\alpha \in \Delta\), let \(H_\alpha \in \mathfrak{h}\) be determined by \(\beta(H_\alpha) = 2 \frac{\langle \alpha, \beta \rangle}{\langle \alpha, \alpha \rangle}\) for all \(\beta \in \Phi\). A Chevalley basis for \(\mathfrak{g}\) is a basis of the form \(\{ H_\alpha \;:\; \alpha \in \Delta \} \cup \{ X_\alpha \;:\; \alpha \in \Phi \}\) such that
  • \([H_\alpha, H_\beta] = 0\) for all \(\alpha, \beta \in \Delta\),
  • \([H_\alpha, X_\beta] = 2 \frac{\langle \beta, \alpha \rangle}{\langle \alpha, \alpha \rangle} X_\beta\) for all \(\alpha \in \Delta, \beta \in \Phi\),
  • \([X_\alpha, X_{-\alpha}] = H_\alpha\) for all \(\alpha \in \Phi\),
  • \([X_\alpha, X_\beta] = \pm (p + 1) X_{\alpha + \beta}\) for all \(\alpha, \beta \in \Phi\) such that \(\alpha + \beta \in \Phi\), where \(p\) is the greatest positive integer such that \(\beta - p \alpha\).
A Chevalley basis always exists.
