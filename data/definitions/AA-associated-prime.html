Let \(R\) be a commutative ring, and \(M\) an \(R\)-module. A prime ideal \(\mathfrak{p}\) of \(R\) is associated to \(M\) if there exists an element \(m \in M\) whose annihilator \(\{ r \in R \mid r \cdot m = 0 \}\) equals \(\mathfrak{p}\). The set of all such primes is denoted \(\operatorname{Ass}_R(M)\).
The prime ideals in \(\operatorname{Ass}_R(M)\) which are minimal with respect to inclusion are called isolated, and the other prime ideals are called embedded.
