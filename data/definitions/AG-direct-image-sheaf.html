Let \(f : X \to Y\) be a map of topological spaces, and let \(\mathcal{F}\) be a sheaf on \(X\). The direct image sheaf \(f_* \mathcal{F}\) on \(Y\) is defined by \[ (f_* \mathcal{F})(V) = \mathcal{F}(f^{-1}(V)) \] for any open set \(V \subset Y\) (indeed this presheaf is a sheaf).
This construction yields the direct image functor \[ f_* : \textup{Sh}(X) \to \textup{Sh}(Y) , \] which is right adjoint to the inverse image functor \(f^{-1}\).
When \(f : X \to Y\) is a morphism of schemes and \(\mathcal{F}\) an \(\mathcal{O}_X\)-module, the direct image \(f^* \mathcal{F}\) is a \(\mathcal{O}_Y\)-module, getting its structure via \(f^\# : \mathcal{O}_Y \to f_* \mathcal{O}_X\). Again we have a adjunction \(f^* \dashv f_*\).
