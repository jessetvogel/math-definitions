Let \(R\) be a ring. An \(R\)-module \(M\) is \(n\)-pseudo-coherent, for some integer \(n \ge 0\), if there exists an exact sequence of \(R\)-modules \[ \bigoplus_{i = 1}^{r_n} R \to \cdots \to \bigoplus_{i = 1}^{r_1} R \to \bigoplus_{i = 1}^{r_0} R \to M \to 0 . \] The module \(M\) pseudo-coherent if it is \(n\)-pseudo-coherent for all \(n \ge 0\).
