Given two groups \(N, H\) and a group homomorphism \(\varphi : H \to \text{Aut}(N)\), the semidirect product of \(N\) and \(H\) with respect to \(\varphi\), denoted \(N \rtimes_\varphi H\), is the group with underlying set \(N \times H\) where products and inverses given by \[ (n, h) \cdot (n', h') = (n \varphi(h)(n'), h h') \quad \text{ and } \quad (n, h)^{-1} = (\varphi(h)^{-1}(n^{-1}), h^{-1}) . \] In particular, \(N\) (viewed as \(N \times 1 \subset N \rtimes_\varphi H\)) is a normal subgroup and the quotient is isomorphic to \(H\) via \(\psi : (N \rtimes_\varphi H)/N \to H\) given by \((n, h) \mapsto h\).
Conversely, if \(G\) is a group with subgroup \(H\) and normal subgroup \(N\) such that \[ 1 \to N \hookrightarrow G \xrightarrow{\psi} H \to 1 \] is exact for some \(\psi : G \to H\), then \(G = N \rtimes H\), with \(H\) acting on \(N\) by conjugation.
