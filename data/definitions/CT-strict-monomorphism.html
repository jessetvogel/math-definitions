Let \(\mathcal{C}\) be a category. A morphism \(f : X \to Y\) in \(\mathcal{C}\) is a strict monomorphism if for all \(g : Z \to Y\) with the property that \(xg = yg\) implies \(xg = yg\) for all \(x, y : Y \to W\), there exists a unique \(h : Z \to X\) such that \(g = fh\). In other words, \(f\) is a split monomorphism if it is the limit of all parallel morphisms \(x, y : Y \to W\) that it equalizes.
