Let \(X\) be a scheme over \(S\). An invertible sheaf \(\mathcal{L}\) on \(X\) is called very ample relative to \(S\), if there is an immersion \(i : X \to \PP_S^n\) for some \(n\), such that \(\mathcal{L} \simeq i^*(\mathcal{O}_{\PP_S^n}(1))\).
An invertible sheaf \(\mathcal{L}\) on a noetherian scheme \(X\) is ample if for every coherent sheaf \(\mathcal{F}\) on \(X\), there is an integer \(n_0 > 0\) such that for every \(n \ge n_0\) the sheaf \(\mathcal{F} \otimes \mathcal{L}^{\otimes n}\) is generated by its global sections.
