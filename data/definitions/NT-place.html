Two valuations \(\phi\) and \(\psi\) on a field \(K\) are said to be equivalent if \(\phi = \psi^r\) for some constant \(r > 0\). A place of \(K\) is an equivalence class of non-trivial valuations on \(K\).
Places are also known as prime divisors, or simply primes, of \(K\). Places corresponding to archimedian (resp. non-archimedean) valuations are called infinite primes (resp. finite primes). This terminology comes from the fact that finite primes on a number field \(K\) precisely correspond to the non-zero prime ideals \(\mathfrak{p}\) of the ring of integers \(\mathcal{O}_K\) of \(K\), and infinite primes on \(K\) correspond to embeddings \(\sigma : K \to \CC\), up to complex conjugation.
