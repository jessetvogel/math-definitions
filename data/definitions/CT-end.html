The end of a functor \(S : \mathcal{C}^\textup{op} \times \mathcal{C} \to \mathcal{E}\) is an object \(e\) of \(\mathcal{E}\) with a family of morphisms \(\pi_c : e \to S(c, c)\) for \(c\) in \(\mathcal{C}\) such that the diagram commutes for all \(f : a \to b\) in \(\mathcal{C}\) (the wedge condition), which is universal in the sense that for any other such \((e', \pi')\) there exists a unique morphism \(h : e' \to e\) such that \(\pi'_c = \pi_c \circ h\) for all \(c\) in \(\mathcal{C}\). The end is denoted \[ e = \int_c S(c, c) . \]
Dually, one can define the coend of a functor \(S : \mathcal{C} \times \mathcal{C}^\textup{op} \to \mathcal{E}\), denoted \(\int^c S(c, c)\).
