Let \(\mathcal{C}\) be a category. The Karoubi envelope (or idempotent completion) of \(\mathcal{C}\) is the category defined as follows.
  • Objects are pairs \((X, e)\), with \(X\) an object of \(\mathcal{C}\) and \(e : X \to X\) an idempotent.
  • A morphism from \((X, e)\) to \((X', e')\) is a morphism \(f : X \to X'\) such that \(e' \circ f = f = f \circ e\).
  • Composition of morphisms is the same as composition in \(\mathcal{C}\).
  • The identity morphism of \((A, e)\) is \(e\).
The Karoubi envelope has the property that every idempotent morphisms splits, and is universal with this property.
