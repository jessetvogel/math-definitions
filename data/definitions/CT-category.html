A category \(\mathcal{C}\) is given by a collection of objects and morphisms.
  • Each morphism has a domain and codomain, which are objects. We write \(f : X \to Y\) or \(X \overset{f}{\to} Y\) if \(X\) is the domain of \(f\) and \(Y\) the codomain. We also write \(X = \textup{dom}(X)\) and \(Y = \textup{cod}(Y)\).
  • Given two morphisms \(f\) and \(g\) such that \(\textup{cod}(f) = \textup{dom}(g)\), the composition of \(f\) and \(g\), written \(g \circ f\), is defined and has domain \(\textup{dom}(f)\) and codomain \(\textup{cod}(g)\).
  • Composition is associative, that is, \((f \circ g) \circ h = f \circ (g \circ h)\).
  • For every object \(X\) there is an identity morphism \(\id_X : X \to X\) satisfying \(f \circ \id_X = f\) and \(\id_X \circ g = g\) for all \(f, g\).
