Let \(R\) be a ring and \(I \subset R\) an ideal of \(R\). Then \(I\) is called a
  • left principal ideal if \(I = Ra = \{ ra : r \in R \}\) for some \(a \in R\),
  • right principal ideal if \(I = aR = \{ ar : r \in R \}\) for some \(a \in R\),
  • two-sided principal ideal if \(I\) is both a left and right principal ideal.
When \(R\) is commutative, these notions coincide, and \(I\) is simply called a principal ideal, and denoted as \(I = (a)\).
