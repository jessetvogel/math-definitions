Let \(X\) be a topological space, \(X' \subset X\) a subpace, and \(Y \subset X'\) a subspace such that the closure \(\overline{Y}\) is contained in interior \(\textup{int}(X')\). The excision theorem states that the inclusion \(X \setminus Y \hookrightarrow X\) induces isomorphisms of relative homology groups \[ H_n(X \setminus Y, X' \setminus Y; A) \xrightarrow{\sim} H_n(X, X'; A) \] for all \(n \in \ZZ\) and coefficient groups \(A\).
