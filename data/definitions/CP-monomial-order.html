Let \(k\) be a field. A monomial in \(S = k[x_1, \ldots, x_n]\) is an element of the form \(X^\alpha = x_1^{\alpha_1} \cdots x_n^{\alpha_n}\) with \(\alpha_i \in \NN\). A monomial order on \(S\) is a total order on the set of monomials of \(S\) such that
  • \(1 < X\) for all monomials \(X \ne 1\) of \(S\),
  • if \(X < Y\) then \(XZ < YZ\) for all monomials \(X, Y\) and \(Z\) of \(S\).

For a given monomial order on \(S\) and any non-zero polynomial \(f \in k[x_1, \ldots, x_n]\), write \[ f = a_1 X^{\alpha_1} + \cdots a_m X^{\alpha_m} \] with \(a_i \ne 0\) and such that \(X^{\alpha_1} > \cdots > X^{\alpha_r}\), one defines
  • \(\operatorname{lm}(f) = X^{\alpha_1}\), the leading monomial of \(f\),
  • \(\operatorname{lc}(f) = a_1\), the leading coefficient of \(f\),
  • \(\operatorname{lt}(f) = a_1 X^{\alpha_1}\), the leading term of \(f\).
