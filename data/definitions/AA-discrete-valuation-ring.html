A domain domain \(R\) is a discrete valuation ring if there is a discrete valuation \(\nu\) of its field of fractions \(K\), such that \(R\) is the valuation ring of \(v\). This means there is a homomorphism \[ \nu : K^\times \to \ZZ \] such that \[ R = \{ x \in K \mid \nu(x) \ge 0 \} . \]
In particular, \(R\) is local with maximal ideal \(\mathfrak{m} = \{ x \in K \mid \nu(x) > 0 \}\). Indeed, any element \(x \in R\) with \(\nu(x) = 0\) is a unit in \(R\).
