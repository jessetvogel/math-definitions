Let \((M, g)\) be a Riemannian manifold. The Christoffel symbols \({\Gamma^k}_{ij}\) are the connection coefficients (in a given coordinate basis) of the Levi-Civita connection. That is, they are the unique coefficients such that \[ \nabla_{\partial_i} \partial_j = {\Gamma^k}_{ij} \partial_k , \] where the \(\partial_i\) denote a local coordinate basis, and \(\nabla\) the Levi-Civita connection.
The Christoffel symbols can be computed from the metric \(g\) via \[ {\Gamma^k}_{ij} = \frac{1}{2} g^{k \ell} \left(\frac{\partial g_{\ell i}}{\partial x^j} + \frac{\partial g_{\ell j}}{\partial x^i} - \frac{\partial g_{ij}}{\partial x^\ell} \right) . \]
