Let \(\mathfrak{g}\) be a semisimple Lie algebra. For any basis \(e_1, \ldots, e_n\) of \(\mathfrak{g}\) with dual basis \(e^1, \ldots, e^n\) with respect to some non-degenerate bilinear form \(B : \mathfrak{g} \times \mathfrak{g} \to k\) (commonly the Killing form), the Casimir element of \(\mathfrak{g}\) is the element \[ \Omega = \sum_{i = 1}^{n} e_i e^i \in U(\mathfrak{g}) , \] of the universal enveloping algebra of \(\mathfrak{g}\). In particular, it lies in the center of \(U(\mathfrak{g})\).
