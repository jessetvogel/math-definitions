Let \(X\) be a scheme and \(Z \subset X\) a closed subscheme given by a sheaf of ideals \(\mathcal{I} \subset \mathcal{O}_X\). A blowup of \(X\) along \(Z\) is a morphism of schemes \[ \pi : \textup{Bl}_Z(X) \to X \] with the universal property that for every morphism \(f : Y \to X\) such that \(f^* \mathcal{I}\) is invertible, there exists a unique morphism \(g : Y \to \textup{Bl}_Z(X)\) such that \(\pi \circ g = f\).
When the closed immersion \(Z \to X\) is of finite presentation, a blowup of \(X\) along \(Z\) exists and is given by \[ \textup{Bl}_Z(X) = \underline{\Proj}_X \left( \bigoplus_{n \ge 0} \mathcal{I}^n \right) . \]
