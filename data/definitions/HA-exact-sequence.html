Let \(\mathcal{A}\) be an abelian category. An exact sequence is a sequence of objects and morphisms \[ \cdots \rightarrow A_{i - 1} \xrightarrow{f_{i - 1}} A_i \xrightarrow{f_i} A_{i + 1} \rightarrow \cdots \] such that \(\ker f_i = \im f_{i - 1}\) for all \(i\). A short exact sequence is an exact sequence of the form \[ 0 \to A \xrightarrow{f} B \xrightarrow{g} C \to 0 . \] Concretely, this means that \(f\) is injective, \(g\) is surjective, and \(\ker g = \im f\).
