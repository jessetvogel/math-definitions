A log scheme \((X, \mathcal{M})\) consists of a scheme \(X\) together with a sheaf of commutative monoids \(\mathcal{M}\) on \(X\), and a monoid morphism \(\alpha : \mathcal{M} \to \mathcal{O}_X\) such that \(\alpha : \alpha^{-1} \mathcal{O}_X^* \to \mathcal{O}_X^*\) is an isomorphism.
A morphism of log schemes \((X, \mathcal{M}) \to (Y, \mathcal{N})\) is a morphism of schemes \(f : X \to Y\) together with a map \(h : f^{-1} \mathcal{N} \to \mathcal{M}\) such that commutes. Such a morphism is called strict if the induced map \(f^* \mathcal{N} \to \mathcal{M}\) is an isomorphism.
