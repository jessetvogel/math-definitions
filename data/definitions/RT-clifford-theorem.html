Let \(G\) be a group with normal subgroup \(N \subset G\) of finite index, and let \(V\) be a irreducible representation of \(G\) over a field \(k\). Then Cliffordâ€™s theorem states the restriction of \(V\) to \(N\) decomposes as a direct sum \[ \textup{Res}^G_N(V) \isom W_1^r \oplus \cdots \oplus W_n^r \] for some \(n, r \ge 0\) and distinct irreducible representations \(W_i\) of \(N\), such that
  1. the action of \(G\) acts permutes the summands \(W_i^r\) transitively,
  2. all \(W_i\) have the same dimension,
  3. \(V\) is isomorphic to the induced representation \(\operatorname{Ind}_{H_i}^G(W_i^r)\) with \(H_i = \{ g \in G \mid g W_i = W_i \}\), for all \(i\).
