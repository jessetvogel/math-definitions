Let \(\mathcal{A}\) be a monoidal category. An \(\mathcal{A}\)-enriched category \(\mathcal{C}\) consists of
  • (objects) a collection \(\textup{Ob}(\mathcal{C})\), whose elements are called objects of \(\mathcal{C}\),
  • (hom-objects) for every pair of objects \(X, Y\) an object \(\underline{\Hom}_\mathcal{C}(X, Y)\) of \(\mathcal{A}\),
  • (composition law) for every triple of objects \(X, Y, Z\) a morphism \[ c_{Z, Y, X} : \underline{\Hom}_\mathcal{C}(Y, Z) \otimes \underline{\Hom}_\mathcal{C}(X, Y) \to \underline{\Hom}_\mathcal{C}(X, Z) \] in \(\mathcal{A}\),
  • (identities) for every object \(X\) a morphism \(e_X : \textbf{1} \to \underline{\Hom}_\mathcal{C}(X, X)\) in \(\mathcal{A}\),
such that for every quadruple of objects \(W, X, Y, Z\) the diagram commutes, and for every pair of objects \(X, Y\), the diagrams commute.
