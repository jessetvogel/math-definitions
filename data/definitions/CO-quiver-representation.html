Let \(Q\) be a quiver. A representation of \(Q\) is a collection of vector spaces \(V_i\) for each vertex \(i \in Q_0\) and linear maps \(V(\alpha) : V_i \to V_j\) for each arrow \((\alpha : i \to j) \in Q_1\).
A morphism of quiver representations \(V \to W\) is a collection of maps \(\varphi_i : V_i \to W_i\) for each vertex \(i \in Q_0\) such that \(\varphi_j V(\alpha) = W(\alpha) \varphi_i\).
The sequence of dimensions \((\dim V_i)_{i \in Q_0} \in \NN^{Q_0}\) is called the dimension vector.
