The homotopy category of a simplicial set \(S_\bdot\) is the category \(\text{h}S_\bdot\) defined as follows:
  • The objects are the vertices \(x \in S_0\).
  • Every edge \(e \in S_1\) determines a morphism \([e] : d_1(e) \to d_0(e)\). The collection of morphisms in \(\text{h}S_\bdot\) is generated under composition by morphisms of the form \([e]\), subject to the relations \[ [s_0(x)] = \id_x \text{ for } x \in S_0, \qquad [d_1(\sigma)] = [d_0(\sigma)] \circ [d_2(\sigma)] \text{ for } \sigma \in S_2 . \]

The functor \(S_\bdot \mapsto \text{h}S_\bdot\) is left adjoint to the nerve functor.
