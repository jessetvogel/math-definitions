Let \(\mathcal{C}\) be a model category. The homotopy category \(\textup{Ho}(\mathcal{C})\) of \(\mathcal{C}\) is the category whose
  • objects are the objects of \(\mathcal{C}\) which are both fibrant and cofibrant,
  • morphisms are homotopy classes of maps.
There is a functor \(\mathcal{C} \to \textup{Ho}(\mathcal{C})\), sending an object \(X\) to its fibrant-cofibrant replacement.
Equivalently, the homotopy category is given as the localization of \(\mathcal{C}\) by its class of weak equivalences.
