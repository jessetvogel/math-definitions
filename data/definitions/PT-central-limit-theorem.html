Let \((\Omega, \mathcal{A}, \PP)\) be a probability space. Let \(X_1, X_2, \ldots : \Omega \to \RR\) be a sequence of independent identically distributed random variables with expected value \(\EE(X_i) = \mu < \infty\) and variance \(\operatorname{Var}(X_i) = \sigma^2 < \infty\). The central limit theorem states that the sequence \[ Z_n = \frac{(X_1 + \cdots + X_n) - n \mu}{\sigma \sqrt{n}} \] convergence in distribution to the standard normal distribution \(\mathcal{N}(0, 1)\).
