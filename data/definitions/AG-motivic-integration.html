Let \(X\) be a smooth complex variety of dimension \(n\). Define \[ \begin{array}{cc} \Delta_m = \Spec \CC[t] / (t^{m + 1}), & \Delta_\infty = \Spec \CC \llbracket t \rrbracket \\[10pt] X_m = \Hom_\CC(\Delta_m, X), & X_\infty = \Hom_\CC(\Delta_\infty, X) . \end{array} \] and truncation maps \(\pi^k_m : X_k \to X_m\) for \(k \ge m\) and \(\psi_m : X_\infty \to X_m\), induced by the natural maps \(\Delta_m \to \Delta_k\) and \(\Delta_\infty \to \Delta_m\), respectively.
For any \(C \subset X_\infty\), which can be written as \(C = \psi_m^{-1}(C_m)\) for some constructible \(C_m \subset X_m\), the motivic measure of \(C\) is \[ \mu(C) = \frac{[C_m]}{\mathbb{L}^{n(m + 1)}} \in \textup{K}(\textbf{Var}_\CC)[\mathbb{L}^{-1}] \] as an element in the Grothendieck ring of varieties localized by the Lefschetz motive \(\mathbb{L} = [\AA^1_\CC]\). This is well-defined, because if \(C = \psi_m^{-1}(C_m) = \psi_k^{-1}(C_k)\) for some \(k > m\), then \(C_k = (\pi_m^k)^{-1}(C_m)\) is an \(\AA_\CC^{n(k - m)}\)-bundle over \(C_m\), so \([C_k] = [C_m] \cdot \mathbb{L}^{n(k - m)}\). The motivic measure of \(X\) is defined as \[ \mu(X) = \mu(X_\infty) = \frac{[X]}{\mathbb{L}^n} . \]
