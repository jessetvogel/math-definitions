Let \(k\) be a field, and choose a monomial order on \(S = k[x_1, \ldots, x_n]\). A Gröbner basis for an ideal \(I \subset S\) is a generating set \(G = \{ g_1, g_2, \ldots, g_n \}\) such that for all non-zero \(f \in I\), the leading monomial \(\operatorname{lm}(g_i)\) divides \(\operatorname{lm}(f)\) for some \(i\).
A Gröbner basis \(G\) is called minimal if the leading coefficients \(\operatorname{lc}(g_i) = 1\) for all \(i\), and the leading monomial \(\operatorname{lm}(g_i)\) does not divide \(\operatorname{lm}(g_j)\) for \(i \ne j\).
A Gröbner basis \(G\) is called reduced if the leading coefficients \(\operatorname{lc}(g_i) = 1\) for all \(i\), and \(g_i\) is reduced with respect to \(G \setminus \{ g_i \}\). A reduced Gröbner basis for \(I\) always exists and is unique.
