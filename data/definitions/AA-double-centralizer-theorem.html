Let \(k\) be a field and \(V\) a finite-dimensional vector space over \(k\). The double centralizer theorem states that for any semisimple \(k\)-subalgebra \(A\) of \(\operatorname{End}_k(V)\), \[ A = \operatorname{End}_B V \quad \textup{ for } B = \operatorname{End}_A V , \] that is, \(A\) is equal to the centralizer of its centralizer in \(\operatorname{End}_k(V)\).
