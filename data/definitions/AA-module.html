Let \(R\) be a ring. A left \(R\)-module is an abelian group \(M\) with a left action of \(R\), that is, a map \[ R \times M \to M, \qquad (r, m) \mapsto r \cdot m, \] satisfying
  • \(r \cdot (m + m') = r \cdot m + r \cdot m'\),
  • \((r + s) \cdot m = r \cdot m + s \cdot m\),
  • \(r \cdot (s \cdot m) = (rs) \cdot m\),
  • \(1 \cdot m = m\),
for all \(r, s \in R\) and \(m, m' \in M\).
Similarly, a right \(R\)-module is an abelian group \(M\) with a right action of \(R\), \[ M \times R \to M, \qquad (m, r) \mapsto m \cdot r , \] satisfying the above conditions in reverse.
When \(R\) is commutative, left and right \(R\)-modules coincide, and are simply called \(R\)-modules.
