Lazardâ€™s universal ring is commutative ring \(\mathbb{L}\) with a formal group law \[ F(x, y) = x + y + \sum_{i + j \ge 2} a_{i,j} x^i y^j \in \mathbb{L}\llbracket x, y \rrbracket , \] with the universal property that for any formal group law \(G(x, y) \in R \llbracket x, y \rrbracket\) over a commutative ring \(R\), there exists a unique ring morphism \(f : \mathbb{L} \to R\) such that \(G(f(x), f(y)) = f(F(x, y))\). The ring \(\mathbb{L}\) is constructed as the quotient of the polynomial ring \(\ZZ[a_{i, j} : i + j \ge 2]\) by the relations on the \(a_{i, j}\) in order to make \(F\) associative.
It can be shown that \(\mathbb{L}\) is isomorphic to the graded ring \(\ZZ[x_1, x_2, \ldots]\), where \(x_i\) has degree \(i\), such that \(a_{i, j}\) has degree \((i + j - 1)\).
