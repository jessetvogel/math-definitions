Given a set \(S\), the free group on \(S\), denoted \(F_S\), is the group consisting of all words that can be built from elements of \(S\) and \(\{ s^{-1} : s \in S \}\), where two words are different unless their equality follows from the group axioms. Composition is given by concatenation of words, and the unit element is the empty word. The members of \(S\) are called generators of \(F_S\), and the number of generators (i.e. the size of \(S\)) is the rank of \(F_S\).
The free abelian group on \(S\) is the abelian group \(\bigoplus_{s \in S} \ZZ\).
