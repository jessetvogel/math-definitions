Let \(p\) be a prime number, and \(R\) a commutative ring. For every \(n \ge 0\), the \(n\)-th Witt polynomial is given by \[ W_n = \sum_{i = 0}^{n} p^i X_i^{p^{n - i}} \in \ZZ[X_0, X_1, \ldots] . \] The ring of Witt vectors over \(R\) is the set \(W(R)\) of sequences \(x = (x_0, x_1, \ldots)\) of elements in \(R\), with the unique commutative ring structure such that
  • for any \(x, y \in W(R)\), \[ \begin{aligned} x + y &= (\Sigma_0(x_0, y_0), \Sigma_1(x_0, y_0, x_1, y_1), \ldots) \\ x \cdot y &= (\Pi_0(x_0, y_0), \Pi_1(x_0, y_0, x_1, y_1), \ldots) \end{aligned} \] with \(\Sigma_i\) and \(\Pi_i\) polynomials with coefficients in \(\ZZ\), independent of \(R\).
  • for every \(n \ge 0\), the Witt polynomial \(W_n\) defines a ring morphism \(W(R) \to R\).
