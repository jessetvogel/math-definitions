  1. If \(G\) and \(H\) are locally isomorphic Lie groups, then their Lie algebras \(\mathfrak{g}\) and \(\mathfrak{h}\) are isomorphic.
  2. If \(G\) and \(H\) are Lie groups with Lie algebras \(\mathfrak{g}\) and \(\mathfrak{h}\), and \(G\) is simply connected, then any morphism of Lie algebras \(f : \mathfrak{g} \to \mathfrak{h}\) lifts uniquely to a morphism of Lie groups \(F : G \to H\), that is, \(dF_e = f\).
  3. Every Lie algebra is isomorphic to the Lie algebra of a (simply connected) Lie group.
