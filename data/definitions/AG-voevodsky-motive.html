Let \(k\) be a field. The category of geometric motives \(\textup{DM}_\textup{gm}(k)\) is constructed as follows.
  1. Let \(\textup{FinCorr}(k)\) be the category of finite correspondences over \(k\).
  2. Let \(\widehat{\textup{DM}}_\textup{gm}^\textup{eff}(k)\) be the category obtained from the bounded homotopy category \(\textbf{K}^\textup{b}(\textup{FinCorr}(k))\) by localizing with respect to
    • (\(\AA^1\)-invariance) \(\pi_X : X \times_k \AA^1_k \to X\) for all smooth schemes \(X\) over \(k\),
    • (Mayerâ€“Vietoris) for all smooth schemes \(X\) over \(k\) and Zariski opens \(j_U : U \hookrightarrow X\) and \(j_V : V \hookrightarrow X\) with \(X = U \cup V\), the map \[ \operatorname{Cone}(U \cap V \xrightarrow{j} U \oplus V) \xrightarrow{j_{U*} - j_{V*}} X , \] where \(j\) is induced by the inclusions of \(U \cap V\) into \(U\) and \(V\).
  3. Define the category of effective geometric motives \(\textup{DM}_\textup{gm}^\textup{eff}(k)\) as the idempotent completion of \(\widehat{\textup{DM}}_\textup{gm}^\textup{eff}(k)\).
  4. Consider the projection \(\pi : \PP^1_k \to \Spec k\) and any section \(\sigma : \Spec k \to \PP^1_k\). Writing \(\ZZ = \Spec k\), note that the exact triangle splits, so that \(\PP^1_k \cong \ZZ \oplus \operatorname{Cone}(\pi)[-1]\). Define \(\ZZ(1) = \operatorname{Cone}(\pi)[-3]\) so that \(\PP^1_k \cong \ZZ \oplus \ZZ(1)[2]\). Furthermore, define \(\ZZ(n) = \ZZ(1)^{\otimes n}\) for \(n \ge 0\).
  5. The category of geometric motives \(\textup{DM}_\textup{gm}(k)\) is defined by inverting the functor \((-) \otimes \ZZ(1)\) on \(\textup{DM}_\textup{gm}^\textup{eff}(k)\). That is, its objects are of the form \(X(n)\), with \(X \in \textup{DM}_\textup{gm}^\textup{eff}(k)\) and \(n \in \ZZ\), and the morphisms are given by \[ \Hom_{\textup{DM}_\textup{gm}(k)}(X(n), Y(m)) = \varinjlim_{N} \Hom_{\textup{DM}_\textup{gm}^\textup{eff}(k)}(X \otimes \ZZ(n + N), Y \otimes \ZZ(m + N)) . \]
The natural functor \(i : \textup{DM}_\textup{gm}^\textup{eff}(k) \to \textup{DM}_\textup{gm}(k)\) that sends \(X\) to \(X(0)\) is fully faithful, and the natural map \(i(X \otimes \ZZ(n)) \to X(n)\) is an isomorphism.
