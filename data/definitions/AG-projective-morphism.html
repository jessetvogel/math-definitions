A morphism \(f : X \to Y\) of schemes is
  • projective (in the sense of EGA) if it factors as \[ X \xrightarrow{i} \PP(\mathcal{E}) \xrightarrow{\pi} Y \] for some quasi-coherent finite type \(\mathcal{O}_Y\)-module \(\mathcal{E}\), with \(i\) a closed immersion and \(\pi\) the projection,
  • bundle-projective if it factors as \[ X \xrightarrow{i} \PP(\mathcal{E}) \xrightarrow{\pi} Y \] for some finite locally free \(\mathcal{O}_Y\)-module \(\mathcal{E}\), with \(i\) a closed immersion and \(\pi\) the projection,
  • H-projective (‘H’ standing for Hartshorne) if it factors as \[ X \xrightarrow{i} \PP^n_Y \xrightarrow{\pi} Y , \] for some integer \(n \ge 0\), with \(i\) a closed immersion and \(\pi\) the projection.
  • locally projective if there exists an open covering \(Y = \bigcup_i U_i\) such that \(f^{-1}(U_i) \to U_i\) is projective for each \(i\).
The morphism \(f\) is quasi-projective if it factors as \[ X \xrightarrow{j} X' \xrightarrow{g} Y , \] with \(j\) an open immersion and \(g\) a projective morphism.
