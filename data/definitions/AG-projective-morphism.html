A morphism \(f : X \to Y\) of schemes is projective if it factors as \[ X \xrightarrow{i} \PP^n_Y \xrightarrow{p} Y , \] with \(i\) a closed immersion and \(p\) the projection.
It is quasi-projective if it factors as \[ X \xrightarrow{j} X' \xrightarrow{g} Y , \] with \(j\) an open immersion and \(g\) a projective morphism.
