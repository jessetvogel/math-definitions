Let \(S\) be a scheme. A group scheme over \(S\) is a scheme \(G\) over \(S\) together with morphisms \(\mu : G \times_S G \to G\) (multiplication), \(\beta : G \to G\) (inversion), \(e : S \to G\) (unit) such that
  • (associativity) commutes,
  • (inverses) both equal \(e \circ (G \to S)\), where \(\Delta\) is the diagonal.
  • (unit) The compositions both equal \(\id_G\).
