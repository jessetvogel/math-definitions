Let \((\Omega, \mathcal{A}, \PP)\) be a probability space and \(X_1, X_2, \ldots : \Omega \to \RR\) be an independent sequence of random variables having a common (finite) expectation \(\EE(X_i) = a\) and a common (finite) variance \(\operatorname{Var}(X_i) = b\). The law of large numbers states that the sequence \(\overline{X}_n = \frac{1}{n} (X_1 + \cdots + X_n)\) converges almost surely \[ \overline{X}_n \xrightarrow{\textup{a.s.}} a \] to the constant random variable \(a\).
