An \(L_\infty\)-algebra is graded vector space \(\mathfrak{g} = \bigoplus_{i \in \ZZ} \mathfrak{g}_i\) together with, for each \(n \ge 1\), a linear map \[ l_n = [-, -, \cdots, -]_n : \mathfrak{g}^{\otimes n} \to \mathfrak{g} \] of degree \(n - 2\), called the \(n\)-ary bracket, satisfying
  • (graded skew-symmetry) for all homogeneous \(v_1, \ldots, v_n \in \mathfrak{g}\) and permutations \(\sigma \in S_n\), \[ l_n(v_{\sigma(1)}, \ldots, v_{\sigma(n)}) = \chi(\sigma, v_1, \ldots, v_n) l_n(v_1, \ldots, v_n) , \] where \(\chi(\sigma, v_1, \ldots, v_n) \in \{ \pm 1 \}\) is the product of the sign of \(\sigma\) with a factor of \((-1)^{\deg(v_i) \deg(v_j)}\) for each interchange of neighbors \((\ldots, v_i, v_j, \ldots)\) to \((\ldots, v_j, v_i, \ldots)\) in the decomposition of \(\sigma\) as a sequence of swapping neighbor pairs.
  • (generalized Jacobi identity) for all homogeneous \(v_1, \ldots, v_n \in \mathfrak{g}\), \[ \sum_{i + j = n + 1} \sum_{\sigma} \chi(\sigma, v_1, \ldots, v_n) (-1)^{i(j - 1)} l_j(l_i(v_{\sigma(1)}, \ldots, v_{\sigma(i)}), v_{\sigma(i + 1)}, \ldots, v_{\sigma(n)}) = 0 , \] where \(\sigma\) runs over the set of \((i, j - 1)\)-unshuffles.
