A morphism \(f : X \to Y\) in a category \(\mathcal{C}\) is an isomorphism if there exists a morphism \(g : Y \to X\) such that \(fg = \id_Y\) and \(gf = \id_X\). The morphism \(g\) is called the inverse of \(f\) and denoted \(g = f^{-1}\). If it exists, it is unique.
If an isomorphism between objects \(X\) and \(Y\) exists, the objects are called isomorphic.
