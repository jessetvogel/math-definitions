Let \(\mathfrak{g}\) be a complex semisimple Lie algebra, with Cartan subalgebra \(\mathfrak{h}\), root system \(\Phi\), and a choice of positive roots \(\Phi^+\). An element \(\lambda \in \mathfrak{h}^*\) is integral if \(2 \frac{(\lambda, \alpha)}{(\alpha, \alpha)}\) is an integer for all \(\alpha \in \Phi\), and called dominant if \((\lambda, \alpha) \ge 0\) for all \(\alpha \in \Phi^+\). Furthermore, an element \(\mu \in \mathfrak{h}^*\) is higher than an element \(\lambda \in \mathfrak{h}^*\) if \(\mu - \lambda\) can be expressed as a linear combination of positive roots with non-negative real coefficients. Finally, a weight of a representation of \(\mathfrak{g}\) is called a highest weight if it is higher than every other weight of of that representation.
The theorem of the highest weight states that
  1. Every irreducible, finite-dimensional representation of \(\mathfrak{g}\) has a highest weight, which is dominant and integral.
  2. Two irreducible, finite-dimensional representations of \(\mathfrak{g}\) with the same highest weight are isomorphic.
  3. For every dominant integral element \(\mu \in \mathfrak{h}^*\), there exists an irreducible, finite-dimensional representation of \(\mathfrak{g}\) with highest weight \(\mu\).
