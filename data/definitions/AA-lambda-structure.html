Let \(R\) be a commutative ring. A \(\lambda\)-structure on \(R\) is a sequence of maps \(\lambda^n : R \to R\) for \(n \ge 0\), satisfying
  • \(\lambda^0(r) = 1\) for all \(r \in R\),
  • \(\lambda^1(r) = r\) for all \(r \in R\),
  • \(\lambda^n(1) = 0\) for all \(n > 1\),
  • \(\lambda^{n}(rs) = \sum_{k = 0}^{n} \lambda^k(r) \lambda^{n - k}(s)\) for all \(r, s \in R\),
  • \(\lambda^n(rs) = P_n(\lambda^1(r), \ldots, \lambda^n(r), \lambda^1(s), \ldots, \lambda^n(s))\) for all \(r, s \in R\),
  • \(\lambda^m(\lambda^n(r)) = P_{m, n}(\lambda^1(r), \ldots, \lambda^{mn}(r))\) for all \(r \in R\),
where \(P_n\) and \(P_{m, n}\) are certain universal polynomials with integer coefficients.
