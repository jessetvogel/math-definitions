Let \(\mathcal{C}\) be a category. The pro-completion of \(\mathcal{C}\) is the category \(\textup{Pro}(\mathcal{C})\) whose
  • objects are functors \(F : \mathcal{I} \to \mathcal{C}\), with \(\mathcal{I}\) a small cofiltered category,
  • morphisms from \(F : \mathcal{I} \to \mathcal{C}\) to \(G : \mathcal{J} \to \mathcal{C}\) are given by \[ \Hom_{\textup{Pro}(\mathcal{C})}(F, G) = \lim_{J \in \mathcal{J}} \colim_{I \in \mathcal{I}} \Hom_\mathcal{C}(F(I), G(J)) . \]
The category \(\textup{Pro}(\mathcal{C})\) is thought of as adjoining to \(\mathcal{C}\) all formal cofiltered limits. In particular,
  • the embedding \(\mathcal{C} \to \textup{Pro}(\mathcal{C})\) that sends an object \(C\) to the corresponding diagram \(\textbf{1} \to \mathcal{C}\) is fully faithful,
  • every diagram \(F : \mathcal{I} \to \mathcal{C}\) is the limit of itself, under the above embedding,
  • the objects of \(\mathcal{C}\) are compact in \(\textup{Pro}(\mathcal{C})\).
