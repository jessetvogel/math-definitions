Let \(\mathcal{C}\) be a category with finite products. A group object in \(\mathcal{C}\) is an object \(G\) of \(\mathcal{C}\) together with morphisms \(m : G \times G \to G\) (multiplication), \(e : 1 \to G\) (group unit) and \(i : G \to G\) (inversion), such that
  • (associativity) \(m \circ (m \times \id_G) = m \circ (\id \times m)\),
  • (unit) \(m \circ (\id_G \times e) = \id_G\) and \(m \circ (e \times \id_G) = \id_G\), where we identified \(G \times 1 \simeq G\),
  • (inversion) \(m \circ (\id_G \times i) \circ \Delta = m \circ (i \times \id_G) \circ \Delta = e_G\), where \(\Delta : G \to G \times G\) is the diagonal map, and \(e_G : G \to G\) is the composition of \(e\) with the unique morphism \(G \to 1\).
