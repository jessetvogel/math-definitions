Let \(p\) be a prime number. The ring of \(p\)-adic integers is defined as the inverse limit \[ \ZZ_p = \varinjlim_{n \ge 1} \ZZ / p^n \ZZ . \] That is, a \(p\)-adic integer is a sequence \((a_n)_{n \ge 1}\) such that \(a_n \in \ZZ / p^n \ZZ\) and \(a_m \equiv a_n \mod p^m\) for \(m \le n\).
The ring of \(p\)-adic rationals \(\QQ_p\) is defined as the field of fractions of \(\ZZ_p\).
