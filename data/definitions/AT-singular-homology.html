Let \(X\) be a topological space and \(A\) an abelian group. A singular \(n\)-simplex in \(X\) is a continuous map \(\sigma : \Delta^n \to X\), where \[ \Delta^n = \left\{ (x_0, \ldots, x_n) \in \RR^{n + 1} \;\middle|\; \text{all } x_i \ge 0 \text{ and } \sum_{i = 0}^n x_i = 1 \right\} \] denotes the standard \(n\)-simplex. The set of all singular \(n\)-simplices in \(X\) is denoted \(\mathcal{S}(X)_n\).
The singular chain complex of \(X\) with coefficients in \(A\) is the chain complex \(C_\bdot(X; A)\) given by \[ C_n(X; A) = A[\mathcal{S}(X)_n] \] and the boundary maps \(\partial_n : C_n(X; A) \to C_{n - 1}(X; A)\) are induced from \[ \partial_n : \mathcal{S}(X)_n \to \mathcal{S}(X)_{n - 1}, \quad \sigma \mapsto \sum_{i = 1}^{n} (-1)^i \, \sigma \circ \delta_i , \] where \(\delta_i : \Delta^{n - 1} \to \Delta^n\) maps to the \(i\)-th face.
The singular homology groups of \(X\) with coefficients in \(A\) are the homology groups of \(C_\bullet(X; A)\), and are denoted \[ H_n(X; A) = H_n(C_\bdot(X; A)) . \] Dually, there is the singular cochain complex of \(X\) with coefficients in \(A\) is the cochain complex \(C^\bdot(X; A)\) given by \[ C^n(X; A) = \Hom_\ZZ(C_n(X; \ZZ), A) . \] Its cohomology groups are called the singular cohomology groups of \(X\) with coefficients in \(A\), and are denoted \[ H^n(X; A) = H^n(C^\bdot(X; A)) . \]
