Let \(\mathcal{C}\) be a category. A set of morphisms \(S\) in \(\mathcal{C}\) is a left multiplicative system if
  • \(\id_X \in S\) for all \(X\), and \(g \circ f \in S\) for all composable \(f, g \in S\),
  • for all \(f : X \to Y\) and \(t : X \to Z\) in \(\mathcal{C}\) with \(t \in S\), there exists an object \(W\) and morphisms \(g : Z \to W\) and \(s : Y \to W\) in \(\mathcal{C}\), such that \(s \in S\) and \(s \circ f = g \circ t\).
  • for all \(f, g : X \to Y\) in \(\mathcal{C}\) and \(t : W \to X\) in \(S\) with \(f \circ t = g \circ t\), there exists an \(s : Y \to Z\) with \(s \circ f = s \circ g\).
A set of morphisms \(S\) in \(\mathcal{C}\) is a right multiplicative system if
  • \(\id_X \in S\) for all \(X\), and \(g \circ f \in S\) for all composable \(f, g \in S\),
  • for all \(g : Z \to W\) and \(s : Y \to W\) in \(\mathcal{C}\) with \(s \in S\), there exists an object \(X\) and morphisms \(f : X \to Y\) and \(t : X \to Z\) in \(\mathcal{C}\), such that \(t \in S\) and \(s \circ f = g \circ t\).
  • for all \(f, g : X \to Y\) in \(\mathcal{C}\) and \(s : Y \to Z\) in \(S\) with \(s \circ f = s \circ g\), there exists an \(t : W \to X\) with \(f \circ t = g \circ t\).
A set of morphisms \(S\) in \(\mathcal{C}\) is a multiplicative system if it is both a left and right multiplicative system.
A multiplicative system \(S\) in \(\mathcal{C}\) is saturated if for all composable morphisms \(f, g, h\) in \(\mathcal{C}\) with \(g \circ f \in S\) and \(h \circ g \in S\), also \(g \in S\).
