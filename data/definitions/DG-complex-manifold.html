Let \(M\) be a smooth manifold. An almost complex structure on \(M\) is a smooth tensor field \(J \in \Gamma(TM \otimes T*M)\) satisfying \(J^2 = -1\) when viewed as a bundle isomorphism \(J : TM \to TM\). The pair \((M, J)\) is called an almost complex manifold.
A smooth manifold \(M\) is complex if it has an atlas of charts to open subsets of \(\CC^n\), such that the transition maps are holomorphic. Any complex smooth manifold has an almost complex structure given by multiplication by \(i\). If an almost complex structure arises in this way, it is called a complex structure or integrable.
