Let \(\mathcal{A}\) be an abelian category and let be a commutative diagram in \(\mathcal{A}\), such that all columns are exact. Then the nine lemma states that
  • if the two bottom rows are exact, so is the top row,
  • if the top two rows are exact, so is the bottom row,
  • if the top and bottom row are exact and the composition \(A \to B \to C\) is zero, then the middle row is exact.
