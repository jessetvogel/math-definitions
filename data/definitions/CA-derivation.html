Let \(A\) be a commutative ring, \(B\) an \(A\)-algebra, and \(M\) a \(B\)-module. An \(A\)-derivation is a map \(d : B \to M\) such that
  • \(d(b + b') = db + db'\) for all \(b, b' \in B\),
  • \(d(bb') = bdb' + b'db\) for all \(b, b' \in B\),
  • \(da = 0\) for all \(a \in A\).
The set of \(A\)-derivations \(d : B \to M\) is often denoted \(\text{Der}_A(B, M)\).
An \(A\)-derivation \(d : B \to M\) is universal if for any other \(A\)-derivation \(d' : B \to N\) there exists a unique \(B\)-module morphism \(\phi : M \to N\) such that \(d' = \phi \circ d\). In this case one often writes \(M = \Omega_{B/A}\).
