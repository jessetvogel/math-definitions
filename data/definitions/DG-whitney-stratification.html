Let \(X\) be a closed subspace \(\RR^n\). A Whitney stratification of \(X\) is a decomposition of \(X\) into finitely many connected locally closed submanifolds \(S_\alpha \subset X\), satisfying Whitneyâ€™s conditions:
  1. Given two strata \(S_\alpha\) and \(S_\beta\) and a sequence \(x_i \in S_\alpha\) converging to some \(y \in S_\beta\) such that the sequence of tangent planes \(T_{x_i} S_\alpha\) converges to a plane \(T\), then \(T\) should contain \(T_y S_\beta\).
  2. Given two strata \(S_\alpha\) and \(S_\beta\) and sequences \(x_i \in S_\alpha\) and \(y_j \in S_\beta\), both converging to \(y \in S_\beta\), such that the sequence of secant lines \(L_i\) from \(x_i\) to \(y_i\) converges to to a line \(L\), and the sequence of tangent planes \(T_{x_i} S_\alpha\) converges to a plane \(T\), then \(L\) should be contained in \(T\).
In fact, condition (B) implies condition (A).
