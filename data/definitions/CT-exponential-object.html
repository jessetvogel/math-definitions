Let \(\mathcal{C}\) be a category in which finite products exist, and let \(X\) and \(Y\) be objects of \(\mathcal{C}\). An exponential object of \(\mathcal{C}\) is an object \(X^Y\) of \(\mathcal{C}\) together with a morphism \(\textup{ev} : X^Y \times Y \to X\), called evaluation, such that for any object \(Z\) of \(\mathcal{C}\) and morphism \(e : Z \times Y \to X\), there exists a unique morphism \(u : Z \to X^Y\) such that \(e = \textup{ev} \circ (u \times \id_Y)\).
