Let \((\mathcal{C}, \otimes, \textbf{1})\) be a rigid \(k\)-linear monoidal category, such that \(\textup{End}(\textbf{1}) = k\), and let \(\omega : \mathcal{C} \to \textbf{Vect}_k\) be an exact faithful \(k\)-linear monoidal functor. The pair \((\mathcal{C}, \omega)\) is called a Tannakian category. Then Tannaka duality states that
  1. The group \(\textup{Aut}^\otimes(\omega)\) of natural automorphisms of \(\omega\) that preserve the monoidal structure, is naturally represented by an affine group scheme \(G\).
  2. The functor \(\mathcal{C} \to \textbf{Rep}_k(G)\), induced by \(\omega\), from \(\mathcal{C}\) to the category of finite-dimensional representations of \(G\) over \(k\), is an equivalence of categories.
In particular, an affine group scheme is uniquely determined up to isomorphism by its category of finite-dimensional representations \(\textbf{Rep}_k(G)\) together with its forgetful functor \(\textbf{Rep}_k(G) \to \textbf{Vect}_k\).
