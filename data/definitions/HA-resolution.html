Let \(\mathcal{A}\) be an abelian category and \(M\) an object in \(\mathcal{A}\). A resolution of \(M\) is a quasi-isomorphism \(M[0] \to A^\bdot\) in \(\text{Comp}(\mathcal{A})\). Equivalently, this is given by an exact complex \[ 0 \to M \to A^0 \to A^1 \to \cdots \] in \(\mathcal{A}\).
When all objects \(A^i\) are injective, we speak of an injective resolution, and similarly when they are all projective, we speak of an projective resolution.
