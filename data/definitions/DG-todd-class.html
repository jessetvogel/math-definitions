Let \(\pi : E \to M\) be a vector bundle, and \(\nabla\) a connection on \(E\) with curvature \(F_\nabla \in \Omega^2(M; \textup{End}(E))\). The form \[ \det\left(\frac{F_\nabla / 2 \pi i}{\exp(F_\nabla / 2 \pi i) - 1}\right) \in \Omega^\bdot(M) \] is closed, and its cohomology class \(\textup{td}(E) \in H^\bdot_\textup{dR}(M)\), called the Todd class of \(E\), is independent of the choice of connection.
The Todd class is multiplicative in the sense that \(\textup{td}(E \oplus F) = \textup{td}(E) \cdot \textup{td}(F)\).
