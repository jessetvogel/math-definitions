A module \(M\) over a ring \(R\) is noetherian if it satisfies the ascending chain condition: for any increasing sequence of submodules \[ M_1 \subset M_2 \subset M_3 \subset \cdots \] there exists some \(N \in \NN\) such that \(M_n = M_N\) for any \(n \ge N\).
Equivalently, \(M\) is noetherian if all its submodules are finitely generated.
