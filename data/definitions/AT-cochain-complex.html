A cochain complex \(C^\bullet\) is a sequence of abelian groups \(C^n\), for \(n \in \ZZ\), together with group morphisms \(d^n : C^n \to C^{n + 1}\) such that \(d^{n + 1} \circ d^n = 0\) for all \(n\). \[ \cdots \xrightarrow{d^{n - 2}} C_{n - 1} \xrightarrow{d_{n - 1}} C_n \xrightarrow{d_n} C_{n + 1} \xrightarrow{d_{n + 1}} \cdots \] Elements of \(C_n\) are called \(n\)-cochains. Elements of \(\ker d_n\) are called \(n\)-cocycles. Elements of \(\im d_{n - 1}\) are called \(n\)-coboundaries. Note that any \(n\)-coboundary is an \(n\)-cocycle.
