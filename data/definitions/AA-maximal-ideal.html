Let \(R\) be a commutative ring. An ideal \(I \subset R\) is called maximal if \(I \ne R\) and if \(I \subset J \subsetneq R\) implies \(I = J\) for any other ideal \(J \subset R\).
Equivalently, \(I \subset R\) is a maximal ideal iff the quotient ring \(R / I\) is a field.
Every maximal ideal is prime.
