A commutative ring \(R\) is catenary if for any prime ideals \(\mathfrak{p}\) and \(\mathfrak{q}\), any two strictly increasing chains of prime ideals \[ \mathfrak{p} = \mathfrak{p}_0 \subsetneq \mathfrak{p}_1 \subsetneq \cdots \subsetneq \mathfrak{p}_n = \mathfrak{q} \] are contained in maximally strictly increasing chains from \(\mathfrak{p}\) to \(\mathfrak{q}\) of the same (finite) length.
The ring \(R\) is universally catenary if all finitely generated algebras over \(R\) are catenary.
