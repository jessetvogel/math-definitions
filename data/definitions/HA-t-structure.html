Let \(\mathcal{D}\) be a triangulated category. A \(t\)-structure on \(\mathcal{D}\) is a pair \((\mathcal{D}_{\ge 0}, \mathcal{D}_{\le 0})\) of full subcategories, stable under isomorphisms, such that
  • \(\mathcal{D}_{\ge 0}[1] \subset \mathcal{D}_{\ge 0}\) and \(\mathcal{D}_{\le 0}[-1] \subset \mathcal{D}_{\le 0}\),
  • \(\Hom_\mathcal{D}(X, Y[-1]) = 0\) for all \(X \in \mathcal{D}_{\ge 0}\) and \(Y \in \mathcal{D}_{\le 0}\),
  • for all \(Y \in \mathcal{D}\), there exists a distinguished triangle \[ X \to Y \to Z \to X[1] \] with \(X \in \mathcal{D}_{\ge 0}\) and \(Z \in \mathcal{D}_{\le 0}[-1]\).
One writes \(\mathcal{D}_{\ge n} = \mathcal{D}_{\ge 0}[n]\) and \(\mathcal{D}_{\le n} = \mathcal{D}_{\le 0}[n]\) for all \(n \in \ZZ\).
The heart of the \(t\)-structure is the full subcategory \(\mathcal{D}^\heartsuit = \mathcal{D}_{\ge 0} \cap \mathcal{D}_{\le 0}\).
A \(t\)-structure is non-degenerate if the intersections \(\cap_{n \in \ZZ} \mathcal{D}_{\ge n}\) and \(\cap_{n \in \ZZ} \mathcal{D}_{\le n}\) consist only of zero objects.
