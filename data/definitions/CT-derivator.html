A prederivator is a strict \(2\)-functor \(\mathbb{D} : \textbf{Cat}^\textup{op} \to \textbf{CAT}\) from the \(2\)-category of small categories to the \(2\)-category of all categories (ignoring set-theoretic issues). For any \(u : I \to J\) in \(\textbf{Cat}\), the induced functor \(\mathbb{D}(u) : \mathbb{D}(J) \to \mathbb{D}(I)\) is usually denoted by \(u^*\).
A derivator is a prederivator \(\mathbb{D}\) such that
  • \(\mathbb{D}(\varnothing)\) is not the empty category,
  • the natural functor \(\mathbb{D}(I \sqcup J) \to \mathbb{D}(I) \times \mathbb{D}(J)\) is an equivalence of categories for all \(I, J \in \textbf{Cat}\),
  • for all \(I \in \textbf{Cat}\), any morphism \(f : X \to Y\) in \(\mathbb{D}(I)\) is an isomorphism if and only if \(f_i : X_i \to Y_i\) is an isomorphism in \(\mathbb{D}(\{ i \})\) for all \(i \in I\).
  • for every \(u : I \to J\) in \(\textbf{Cat}\), the functor \(u^*\) has a left adjoint \(u_!\) and right adjoint \(u^*\),
  • for every \(u : I \to J\) in \(\textbf{Cat}\) and \(j \in J\), the morphisms \[ \operatorname*{hocolim}_{u \downarrow j} \textup{pr}_1^*(X) \xrightarrow{\alpha_!} u_!(X)_j \quad \textup{ and } \quad u_*(X)_j \xrightarrow{\alpha_!} \operatorname*{hocolim}_{j \downarrow u} \textup{pr}_2^*(X) \] are isomorphisms for all \(X \in \mathbb{D}(I)\), where \(\textup{pr}_1 : u \downarrow j \to I\) and \(\textup{pr}_2 : j \downarrow u \to I\) denote the natural projections.
