Let \(\Phi\) be a root system in \(V\). The Weyl group \(W\) of \(\Phi\) is the subgroup of the orthogonal group \(\textup{O}(V)\) generated by the reflections \[ s_\alpha : V \to V, \quad v \mapsto v - 2 \frac{(v, \alpha)}{(\alpha, \alpha)} \alpha , \] for all \(\alpha \in \Phi\). By definition of the root system, each \(s_\alpha\) preserves \(\Phi\), so \(W\) is a finite group.
