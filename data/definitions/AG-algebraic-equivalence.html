Two algebraic cycles \(Z, Z'\) on a variety \(X\) are algebraically equivalent if there is an curve \(C\) and an algebraic cycle \(W\) on \(X \times C1\) flat over \(C\), such that \[ [Z] - [Z'] = [W \cap X \times \{ c_1 \}] - [W \cap X \times \{ c_2 \}] \] for two points \(c_1, c_2\) on \(C\).
The Néron–Severi group.
