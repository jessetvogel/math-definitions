Let \(\mathfrak{S}\) be a category. A fibered category over \(\mathfrak{S}\) is a category \(\pi : \mathfrak{X} \to \mathfrak{S}\) over \(\mathfrak{S}\) such that for every object \(S\) in \(\mathfrak{S}\) and \(x\) in \(\mathfrak{X}\) lying over \(S\), and morphism \(f : T \to S\), there exists a strongly cartesian morphism \(\alpha : y \to x\) with \(\pi(y) = T\).
