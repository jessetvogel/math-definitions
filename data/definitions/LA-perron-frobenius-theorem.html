Let \(A\) be an \(n \times n\) matrix whose entries are positive real numbers. The Perron–Frobenius theorem states that
  1. \(A\) has a positive real eigenvalue \(r\), called the Perron–Frobenius eigenvalue, such that \(r > |\lambda|\) for any other (possibly complex) eigenvalue \(\lambda\) of \(A\),
  2. the algebraic multiplicity of \(r\) is \(1\),
  3. there exists an eigenvector \(v = (v_1, \ldots, v_n)\) of \(A\) with eigenvalue \(r\) such that all components \(v_i\) are positive.
