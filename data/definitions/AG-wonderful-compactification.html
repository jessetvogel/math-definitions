Let \(G\) be a complex connected semisimple algebraic group with trivial center with Lie algebra \(\mathfrak{g}\) and write \(n = \dim G\). Consider the action of \(G \times G\) on the Grassmanian \(\textup{Gr}(n, \mathfrak{g} \oplus \mathfrak{g})\) via the adjoint representation \(\textup{Ad}_{G \times G} : G \times G \to \textup{GL}(\mathfrak{g} \oplus \mathfrak{g})\). The stabilizer of \(\mathfrak{g}_\Delta = \{ (x, x) : x \in \mathfrak{g} \}\) in \(G \times G\) is given by \(G_\Delta = \{ (g, g) : g \in G \}\), so that \((G \times G) \cdot \mathfrak{g}_\Delta \cong (G \times G) / G_\Delta \cong G\). The wonderful compactification of \(G\) is \[ \overline{G} = \overline{(G \times G) \cdot \mathfrak{g}_\Delta} \] where the closure is taken in \(\textup{Gr}(n, \mathfrak{g} \oplus \mathfrak{g})\). As \(\textup{Gr}(n, \mathfrak{g} \oplus \mathfrak{g})\) is projective, also \(\overline{G}\) is projective.
