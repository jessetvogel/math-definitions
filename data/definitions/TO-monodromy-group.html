Let \(X\) be a connected and locally connected topological space with basepoint \(x \in X\), and \(p : Y \to X\) a covering space of \(X\) with fiber \(F = p^{-1}(x)\) over \(x\). For any loop \(\gamma : [0, 1] \to X\) based at \(x\) and lift \(x' \in F\) of \(x\), there is a unique lift \(\gamma' : [0, 1] \to Y\) of \(\gamma\) starting at \(x'\), and we denote the endpoint by \(\gamma \cdot x' = \gamma'(1)\). This defines a homomorphism \[ \pi_1(X, x) \to \textup{Aut}(F), \quad \gamma \mapsto (x' \mapsto \gamma \cdot x') \] from the fundamental group of \(X\) to the automorphism group of \(F\), called the monodromy action of \(p\). The image of this homomorphism is the monodromy group of \(p\).
