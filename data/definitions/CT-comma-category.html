The comma category of two functors \(F : \mathcal{C} \to \mathcal{E}\) and \(G : \mathcal{D} \to \mathcal{E}\) is the category \(F \downarrow G\) whose
  • objects are triples \((C, D, f)\) where \(C, D\) are objects of \(\mathcal{C}\) and \(\mathcal{D}\), respectively, and \(f : F(C) \to G(D)\) a morphism in \(\mathcal{E}\),
  • morphisms from \((C, D, f)\) to \((C', D', f')\) are pairs \((g, h)\), where \(g : C \to C'\) and \(h : D \to D'\) are morphisms in \(\mathcal{C}\) and \(\mathcal{D}\), respectively, such that \(f' \circ F(g) = G(h) \circ f\).
