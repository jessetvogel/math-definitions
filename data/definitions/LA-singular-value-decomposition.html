Let \(A\) be a real or complex \(m \times n\) matrix. A singular value decomposition of \(A\) is a factorization \(A = U \sigma V^*\), where \(U\) is an \(m \times m\) unitary matrix, \(V\) an \(n \times n\) unitary matrix, and \(\Sigma\) a diagonal \(m \times n\) matrix. The diagonal entries \(\sigma_i = \Sigma_{ii}\) are uniquely determined, and called the singular values of \(A\).
If \(A\) is a real matrix, \(U\) and \(V\) can be chosen to be real orthogonal matrices.
