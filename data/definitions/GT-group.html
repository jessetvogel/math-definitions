A group is a set \(G\) together with an operation \(G \times G \to G\) (the group law) written as \((x, y) \mapsto xy\), and an element \(1 \in G\) (the unit), satisfying
  • (associativity) \((xy)z = x(yz)\) for all \(x, y, z \in G\),
  • (unit element) \(1 \cdot x = x \cdot 1 = x\) for all \(x \in G\),
  • (inverses) for all \(x \in G\) there exists an \(x^{-1} \in G\) such that \(x x^{-1} = x^{-1} x = 1\), called the inverse of \(x\).
