Let \((\Omega, \mathcal{A}, \PP)\) be a probability space and \(X : \Omega \to \RR\) a random variable. The \(n\)-th moment of \(X\) is the expected value of its \(n\)-th power, that is, \(\EE(X^n)\), if it exists. The \(n\)-th central moment of \(X\) is the expected value \(\EE((X - \EE(X))^n)\), if it exists.
