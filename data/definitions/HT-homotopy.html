Let \(\mathcal{C}\) be a model category, and let \(f, g : X \to Y\) be two morphisms in \(\mathcal{C}\).
  • A left homotopy from \(f\) to \(g\) is a morphism \(H : \textup{Cyl}(X) \to Y\), where \(\textup{Cyl}(X)\) is a cylinder object of \(X\), such that commutes. If such a left homotopy exists, then \(f\) and \(g\) are called left homotopic.
  • A right homotopy from \(f\) to \(g\) is a morphism \(H : X \to \textup{Path}(Y)\), where \(\textup{Path}(Y)\) is a path object of \(Y\), such that commutes. If such a right homotopy exists, then \(f\) and \(g\) are called right homotopic.
If \(X\) is cofibrant and \(Y\) is fibrant, then being left homotopic is equivalent to being right homotopic.
