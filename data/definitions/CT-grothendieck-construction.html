Let \(F : \mathcal{C} \to \textbf{Cat}\) be a functor from a category \(\mathcal{C}\) to the category of small categories \(\textbf{Cat}\). The Grothendieck construction for \(F\) is the category \(\int_\mathcal{C} F\) whose
  • objects are pairs \((C, X)\) with \(C\) an object of \(\mathcal{C}\) and \(X\) an object of \(F(C)\),
  • morphisms from \((C, X)\) to \((C', X')\) are pairs \((f, g)\) where \(f : C \to C'\) and \(g : F(f)(X) \to X'\).
Composition in \(\int_\mathcal{C} F\) is given by \((f, g) \circ (f', g') = (f \circ f', g \circ F(f)(g'))\).
