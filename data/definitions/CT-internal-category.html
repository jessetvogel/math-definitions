Let \(\mathcal{C}\) be a category with pullbacks. A category internal to \(\mathcal{C}\) consists of
  • objects \(C_0, C_1\) in \(\mathcal{C}\),
  • morphisms \(s, t : C_1 \to C_0\),
  • a morphism \(e : C_0 \to C_1\),
  • a morphism \(c : C_1 \times_{C_0} C_1 \to C_1\), where the pullback of \(s\) and \(t\) is taken,
such that
  • (source/target identity) \(s \circ e = t \circ e = \id_{C_0}\),
  • (source/target composition) \(s \circ c = s \circ \pi_1\) and \(t \circ c = t \circ \pi_2\),
  • (associativity) \(c \circ (\id_{C_1} \times_{C_0} c) = c \circ (c \times_{C_0} \id_{C_1})\),
  • (composition identity) \(e \times_{C_0} \id_{C_1} \circ c = \pi_2\) and \(\id_{C_1} \times_{C_0} e \circ c = \pi_1\).
