Let \(X\) be a finite CW-complex, and \(f : X \to X\) be a continuous map. The Lefschetz number of \(f\) is defined as \[ \Lambda_f = \sum_{k \ge 0} (-1)^k \tr \left(f_* : H_k(X; \QQ) \to H_k(X; \QQ) \right) , \] the alternating (finite) sum of traces of the maps induced by \(f\) on the singular homology of \(X\). Lefschetz fixed point theorem states that if \(\Lambda_f \ne 0\), then there exists some \(x \in X\) with \(f(x) = x\).
