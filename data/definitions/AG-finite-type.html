A morphism \(f : X \to Y\) of schemes is of finite type at \(x \in X\) if there exist affine opens \(U = \Spec A \subset X\) containing \(x\) and \(V = \Spec B \subset Y\) with \(f(U) \subset V\) such that \(A\) is a finitely generated \(B\)-algebra (via the induced map \(B \to A\)).
The morphism \(f\) is locally of finite type if it is of finite type at each \(x \in X\), and it is of finite type if it is locally of finite type and quasi-compact.
