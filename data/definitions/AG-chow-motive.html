Let \(k\) be a field. The category of effective pure Chow motives over \(k\), denoted \(\textup{Chow}^\textup{eff}(k)\), is the idempotent completion of the category of Chow correspondences \(\textup{Corr}(k)\), that is,
  • the objects are pairs \((X, p)\) with \(X\) a smooth projective scheme over \(k\), and \(p \in \textup{CH}_{\dim X}(X, X)\) with \(p \circ p = p\),
  • the morphisms \(f : (X, p) \to (Y, q)\) are correspondences \(f \in \textup{CH}_{\dim X}(X \times_k Y)\) such that \(f \circ p = f = q \circ f\).
This category has the structure of a monoidal category given by \[ (X, p) \otimes (Y, q) = (X \times_k Y, p \times q) , \] with unit \(\textbf{1} = (\Spec k, \Gamma_{\id})\).
Consider the morphism \(f : \PP^1_k \to \Spec k\) and a point \(x : \Spec k \to \PP^1_k\). Since \(x \circ f\) is idempotent, and every idempotent splits, \((\PP^1_k, \Gamma_\id) = \textbf{1} \oplus \mathbb{L}\), for some object \(\mathbb{L}\) called the Lefschetz motive.
The category of pure Chow motives \(\textup{Chow}(k)\) is obtained by adjoining a formal inverse \(\mathbb{L}^{-1}\) to \(\textup{Chow}^\textup{eff}(k)\).
