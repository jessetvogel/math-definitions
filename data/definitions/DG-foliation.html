Let \(M\) be an \(n\)-dimensional smooth manifold. A \(p\)-dimensional foliation of \(M\) is a decomposition of \(M\) into a union of disjoint connected submanifolds \[ M = \bigsqcup_{\alpha \in A} L_\alpha \] such that each point \(x \in M\) has a neighborhood \(U\) with local coordinates \(x^1, \ldots, x^n\) such that \(x^{p + 1}, \ldots, x^n\) are constant on \(U \cap L_\alpha\) for all \(\alpha \in A\). The submanifolds \(L_\alpha\) are called the leaves of the foliation, and the foliation is denoted as \(\mathcal{F} = \{ L_\alpha \}_{\alpha \in A}\).
