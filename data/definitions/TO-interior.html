Let \(X\) be a topological space, and \(A \subset X\) a subset. A point \(x \in X\) is an interior point of \(A\) if there exists an open set \(U \subset A\) with \(x \in U\). The interior \(\overset{\circ}{A}\) of \(A\) is the set of interior points of \(A\).
Equivalently, \(\overset{\circ}{A}\) is the largest open subset of \(X\) contained in \(A\).
