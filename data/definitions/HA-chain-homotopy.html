Let \(\mathcal{A}\) be an abelian category, and let \(f, g : A^\bdot \to B^\bdot\) be morphisms of chain complexes in \(\mathcal{A}\). A chain homotopy from \(f\) to \(g\) is a collection of morphisms \(k^i : A^i \to B^{i - 1}\) such that \(f - g = dk + kd\).
If a homotopy exists from \(f\) to \(g\), we say that \(f\) and \(g\) are homotopic. This gives an equivalence relation on the set of morphisms from \(A^\bdot\) to \(B^\bdot\).
