Let \((M, g)\) be a Riemannian manifold, and let \(E = \bigwedge T^*M \otimes \CC \to M\) be the complex vector bundle given by the exterior algebra of the cotangent bundle. The exterior derivative \(d\) defines a first order linear differential operator \(d : \Gamma(E) \to \Gamma(E)\). Under the Hodge inner product, it has a formal adjoint \(d^*\) given by \(d^* \omega = (-1)^{nk + 1} \star d \star \omega\) for \(\omega \in \Omega^k(M; \CC)\), where \(\star\) is the Hodge star operator. The Hodge–Dirac operator is the Dirac operator on \(E\) given by \[ D = d + d^* . \] Its square \(\Delta = D^2\) is the Hodge–Laplace operator.
