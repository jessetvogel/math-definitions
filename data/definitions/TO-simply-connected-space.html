A topological space \(X\) is simply connected if it is path-connected and every loop \(f : S^1 \to X\) can be contracted to a point, i.e. there exists map \(F : D^2 \to X\) which restricts to \(f\) on \(S^1\).
A topological space \(X\) is locally simply connected if it admits a basis of simply connected sets.
