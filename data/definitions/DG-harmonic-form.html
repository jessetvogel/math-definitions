Let \((M, g)\) be an \(n\)-dimensional Riemannian manifold. The exterior derivative \(d : \Omega^k(M) \to \Omega^{k + 1}(M)\) has an adjoint \(\delta = (-1)^{nk + 1} \star d \star : \Omega^k(M) \to \Omega^{k - 1}(M)\) with respect to the Hodge inner product. That is, \[ (d \alpha, \beta) = (\alpha, \delta \beta) \quad \text{for all } \alpha \in \Omega^k(M) \text{ and } \beta \in \Omega^{k + 1}(M) . \] A differential form \(\alpha \in \Omega^k(M)\) is harmonic if \(\Delta \alpha = 0\), where \(\Delta = d \delta + \delta d\) is the Laplacian operator.
