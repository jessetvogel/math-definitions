Let \(X\) be a set. An equivalence relation on \(X\) is a binary relation \(\sim\) on \(X\) such that
  • (reflexivity) \(x \sim x\) for all \(x \in X\),
  • (symmetry) \(x \sim y\) if and only if \(y \sim x\) for all \(x, y \in X\),
  • (transitivity) if \(x \sim y\) and \(y \sim z\), then \(x \sim z\) for all \(x, y, z \in X\).
