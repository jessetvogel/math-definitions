Let \((\mathcal{C}, \otimes, \textbf{1})\) be a monoidal category. A monoid object in \(\mathcal{C}\) is an object \(M\) together with morphisms \(\mu : M \otimes M \to M\) (the multiplication map) and \(\eta : \textbf{1} \to M\) (the unit), such that
  • (associativity) the diagram commutes, where \(\alpha\) is the associator.
  • (unit) the diagram commutes, where \(\lambda\) and \(\rho\) are the left and right unitor, respectively.
