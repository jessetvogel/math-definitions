A permutation \(\sigma \in S_n\) is a cyclic permutation, or cycle, of length \(k\) if there exist \(k\) distinct integers \(1 \le a_1, \ldots, a_k \le n\) with \(\sigma(a_i) = a_{i + 1}\) for \(1 \le i < k\) and \(\sigma(a_k) = a_1\), and \(\sigma(x) = x\) for \(x \not\in \{ a_1, \ldots, a_k \}\). This is denoted by \[ \sigma = (a_1 \;\; a_2 \;\; \ldots \;\; a_k) . \] A cycle of length \(2\) is also called a transposition.
