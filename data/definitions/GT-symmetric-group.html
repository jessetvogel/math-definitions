Let \(\Sigma\) be a set. The symmetric group on \(\Sigma\) is the group \(S_\Sigma\) of all bijections \(\Sigma \to \Sigma\), where multiplication is given by composition, and the unit is given by the identity map \(\id_\Sigma\).
Given an integer \(n \ge 1\), one writes \(S_n\) for the symmetric group on the set \(\Sigma = \{ 1, 2, \ldots, n \}\). Elements of \(S_n\) are called permutations.
