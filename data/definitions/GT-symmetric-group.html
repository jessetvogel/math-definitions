Let \(\Sigma\) be a set. The symmetric group on \(\Sigma\) is the group \(S_\Sigma\) of all bijections \(\Sigma \to \Sigma\). The group law is given by composition, and the unit is the identity map.
When \(\Sigma = \{ 1, 2, \ldots, n \}\) for some integer \(n \ge 1\), one writes \(S_n\) for the symmetry group. Its elements are called permutations.
