Given a presheaf \(\mathcal{F}\), there is a sheaf \(\mathcal{F}^+\) and a morphism \(\theta : \mathcal{F} \to \mathcal{F}^+\), with the property that for any sheaf \(\mathcal{G}\) and morphism \(f : \mathcal{F} \to \mathcal{G}\) there is a unique morphism \(g : \mathcal{F}^+ \to \mathcal{G}\) such that \(f = g \circ \theta\). The sheaf \(\mathcal{F}^+\) is called the sheaf associated to the presheaf \(\mathcal{F}\).
