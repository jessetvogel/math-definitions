A differential graded algebra (dga) over a field \(k\) is a chain complex \((A_\bdot, d)\) of \(k\)-modules with a multiplication map \(A_i \times A_j \to A_{i + j}\), satisfying the Leibniz rule \[ d(ab) = b d(a) + (-1)^{\deg(a)} a d(b) \] for all \(a, b \in A_\bdot\).
A dga is commutative if \(ab = (-1)^{\deg(a) \deg(b)} ba\) for all \(a, b \in A_\bdot\).
