An \(m \times n\) matrix \(A\) is said to be in row echelon form if
  • the zero rows of \(A\) are below the nonzero rows of \(A\), and
  • the pivot of each nonzero row, i.e. its first nonzero entry, is farther to the right than the pivots of the rows above.
The matrix \(A\) is said to be in reduced row echelon form if in addition
  • the pivot of each nonzero row is equal to \(1\),
  • the pivot of each nonzero row is the only nonzero entry in its column.
