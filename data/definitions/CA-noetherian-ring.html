A commutative ring \(R\) is called noetherian if it satisfies the ascending chain condition: for any increasing sequence of ideals \[ I_1 \subset I_2 \subset I_3 \subset \cdots \] there exists some \(N \in \NN\) such that \(I_n = I_N\) for any \(n \ge N\).
Equivalently, \(R\) is noetherian if every ideal is finitely generated.
