Let \(\mathcal{A}\) be an abelian category, and \(A\) an object in \(\mathcal{A}\). The homological dimension of \(A\) is the least number \(n\) such that there exists a projective resolution \[ 0 \to P^n \to \cdots \to P^1 \to P^0 \to A . \] If such a resolution does not exist, the homological dimension of \(A\) is \(\infty\).
The global homological dimension of the category \(\mathcal{A}\) is the supremum of the homological dimension of all objects \(A\) of \(\mathcal{A}\).
