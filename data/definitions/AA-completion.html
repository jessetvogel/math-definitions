Let \(R\) be a commutative ring and \(M\) an \(R\)-module. Each ideal \(\mathfrak{a} \subset R\) determines a topology on \(M\) called the \(\mathfrak{a}\)-adic topology: a subset \(U \subset M\) is open if and only if for each \(x \in U\) there exists a positive integer \(n\) such that \(x + \mathfrak{a}^n M \subset U\).
The completion of \(M\) with respect to \(\mathfrak{a}\) is the inverse limit \[ \widehat{M} = \varprojlim_{n \ge 1} M / \mathfrak{a}^n M = \left\{ (x_n)_{n \ge 1} \in \prod_{n \ge 1} M / \mathfrak{a}^n M : x_m = x_n \textup{ mod } \mathfrak{a}^n M \textup{ for } m \le n \right\} \] with the subspace product topology.
When \(R = M\), the completion \(\widehat{R}\) has a ring structure and is called the completion of \(R\), with repsect to \(\mathfrak{a}\).
