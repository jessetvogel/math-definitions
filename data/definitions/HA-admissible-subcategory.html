Let \(\mathcal{T}\) be a triangulated category. A full triangulated subcategory \(\mathcal{S} \subset \mathcal{T}\) is right admissible (resp. left admissible) if the inclusion \(i : \mathcal{S} \to \mathcal{T}\) has a right (resp. left) adjoint \(\pi : \mathcal{T} \to \mathcal{S}\). If it is both left and right admissible, it is called admissible.
