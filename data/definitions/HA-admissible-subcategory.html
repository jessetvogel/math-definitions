Let \(\mathcal{D}\) be a triangulated category. A full triangulated subcategory \(\mathcal{D}' \subset \mathcal{D}'\) is admissible if the inclusion has a right adjoint \(\pi : \mathcal{D} \to \mathcal{D}'\).
Equivalently, \(\mathcal{D}'\) is admissible if for all \(A \in \mathcal{D}\) there exists a distinguished triangle \[ B \to A \to C \to B[1] \] with \(B \in \mathcal{D}'\) and \(C \in {\mathcal{D}'}^\perp\) (the orthogonal complement). Indeed, one may use \(B = \pi(A)\).
