Let \(R\) be a commutative ring and \(I \subset R\) an ideal. A divided power structure on \(I\) is a sequence of maps \(\delta_n : I \to A\) for \(n \ge 0\), satisfying
  • \(\delta_0(x) = 1\) and \(\delta_1(x) = x\) and \(\delta_n(x) \in I\) for all \(x \in I\) and \(n > 0\),
  • \(\delta_n(x + y) = \sum_{i = 0}^{n} \delta_{n - i}(x) \delta_i(y)\) for all \(x, y \in I\),
  • \(\delta_n(a x) = a^n \delta_n(x)\) for all \(a \in R\) and \(x \in I\),
  • \(\delta_m(x) \delta_n(x) = \frac{(m + n)!}{m! n!} \delta_{m + n}(x)\) for all \(x \in I\) and \(m, n \ge 0\),
  • \(\delta_n(\delta_m(x)) = \frac{(mn)!}{(m!)^n n!} \delta_{mn}(x)\) for all \(x \in I\) and \(m > 0\).
