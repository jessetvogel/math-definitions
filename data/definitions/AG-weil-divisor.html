Let \(X\) be a noetherian integral separated scheme, where every local ring \(\mathcal{O}_{X,x}\) of dimension one is regular.
A prime divisor on \(X\) is a closed integral subscheme \(Y\) of codimension one. A Weil divisor on \(X\) is an element of the free abelian group \(\text{Div } X\) generated by the prime divisors.
A Weil divisor \(D = \sum_i n_i Y_i\) is effective if \(n_i \ge 0\) for all \(i\).
The Weil divisor associated to a rational function \(f\) on \(X\) is \[ \text{div}(f) = \sum v_Y(f) \cdot Y , \] where the sum is taken over all prime divisors \(Y\), and \(v_Y : K(X) \to \ZZ\) denotes the discrete valuation of \(Y\). Any Weil divisor of this form is called a principal Weil divisor.
Two Weil divisors \(D\) and \(D'\) are linearly equivalent, denoted \(D \sim{} D'\), if the difference \(D - D'\) is principal. The quotient \(\text{Cl } X = \text{Div } X / \sim{}\) is called the divisor class group of \(X\).
