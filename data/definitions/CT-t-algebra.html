Let \((T, \mu, \eta)\) be a monad on a category \(\mathcal{C}\). A \(T\)-algebra in \(\mathcal{C}\) is a pair \((X, h)\) of an object \(X\) in \(\mathcal{C}\) and a morphism \(h : TX \to X\), such that commute. Morphisms between \(T\)-algebras \((X, h) \to (Y, k)\) are morphisms \(f : X \to Y\) in \(\mathcal{C}\) for which commutes.
