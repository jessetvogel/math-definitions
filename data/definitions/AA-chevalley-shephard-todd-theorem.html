Let \(V\) be a finite-dimensional vector space over a field \(k\), and \(G \subset \textup{GL}(V)\) a finite group whose order \(|G|\) is invertible in \(k\). An element \(s \in \textup{GL}(V)\) is a pseudoreflection if \(s \ne 1\) and \(s\) fixes a codimension \(1\) subspace of \(V\). The Chevalley–Shephard–Todd theorem states that the following are equivalent:
  1. \(G\) is generated by pseudoreflections,
  2. the ring of invariants \(k[V]^G\) is a free polynomial algebra over \(k\),
  3. \(k[V]\) is a free module over \(k[V]^G\).
