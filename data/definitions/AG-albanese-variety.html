Let \(X\) be a connected complete variety over an algebraically closed field \(k\), with a basepoint. Then there exists an abelian variety \(\textup{Alb}(X)\), the Albanese variety of \(X\), and a morphism of pointed varieties \(i : X \to \textup{Alb}(X)\), the Albanese map, with the universal property that for any other abelian variety \(A\) and morphism of pointed varieties \(f : X \to A\), there exists a unique morphism of abelian varieties \(f' : \textup{Alb}(X) \to A\) such that \(f = f' \circ i\).
