Let \(X\) be a path-connected pointed space. A Whitehead tower of \(X\) is a commutative diagram of pointed spaces such that for every \(n \ge 1\),
  • the map \(W_n X \to X\) induces isomorphisms \(\pi_k W_n X \to \pi_k X\) for all \(k > n\),
  • \(\pi_k W_n X = 0\) for all \(k \le n\).
It is a theorem that a Whitehead tower of \(X\) exists, and it is unique up to weak homotopy equivalence. In fact, one can take \(W_n X\) to be the homotopy fiber \(\textup{hofib}(X \to P_n X)\) for a Postnikov tower \(P_n X\).
