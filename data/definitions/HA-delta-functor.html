Let \(\mathcal{A}\) and \(\mathcal{B}\) be abelian categories. A homological \(\delta\)-functor from \(\mathcal{A}\) to \(\mathcal{B}\) is a collection of additive functors \(T_n : \mathcal{A} \to \mathcal{B}\) for \(n \ge 0\), together with morphisms \[ \delta_n : T_n(C) \to T_{n - 1}(A) \] for each short exact sequence \(0 \to A \to B \to C \to 0\) in \(\mathcal{A}\), satisfying the following conditions.
  • For each short exact sequence \(0 \to A \to B \to C \to 0\) in \(\mathcal{A}\), there is a long exact sequence \[ \cdots \to T_{n + 1}(C) \xrightarrow{\delta_{n + 1}} T_n(A) \to T_n(B) \to T_n(C) \xrightarrow{\delta} T_{n - 1}(A) \to \cdots \to T_0(C) \to 0 . \]
  • For each morphism of short exact sequences from \(0 \to A' \to B' \to C' \to 0\) to \(0 \to A \to B \to C \to 0\), the diagram commutes.
Similarly, a cohomological \(\delta\)-functor is a collection of additive functors \(T^n : \mathcal{A} \to \mathcal{B}\) for \(n \ge 0\), together with morphisms \(\delta^n : T^n(C) \to T^{n + 1}(A)\) for each short exact sequence \(0 \to A \to B \to C \to 0\), satisfying the dual conditions.
