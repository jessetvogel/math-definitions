Let \(X\) be a scheme. A sheaf of \(\mathcal{O}_X\)-modules \(\mathcal{F}\) is quasi-coherent if \(X\) can be covered by open affine subsets \(U_i = \Spec R_i\), such that for each \(i\), the restriction \(\mathcal{F}|_{U_i}\) is isomorphic to \(\tilde{M}_i\) for some \(R_i\)-module \(M_i\).
Furthermore, \(\mathcal{F}\) is coherent is each \(M_i\) can be taken to be a finitely presented \(R_i\)-module.
