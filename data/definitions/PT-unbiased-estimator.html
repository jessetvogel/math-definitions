Let \(X_1, \ldots, X_n\) be a random sample from a population \(F_\theta\) depending on some parameter \(\theta\). The bias of a point estimator \(\hat{\theta}(X_1, \ldots, X_n)\) for \(\theta\) is the difference \[ \EE(\hat{\theta}) - \theta . \] When the bias is zero (for all \(\theta\)), the estimator is called unbiased.
When the bias tends to zero as \(n \to 0\), the estimator is called asymptotically unbiased.
