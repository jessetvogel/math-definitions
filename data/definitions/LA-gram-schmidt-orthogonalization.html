Let \(V\) be a real or complex vector space with inner product \(\langle \cdot, \cdot \rangle\) and basis \(\{ v_1, \ldots, v_n \}\). Define \[ \begin{aligned} u_1 &= v_1, \\ u_2 &= v_2 - \frac{\langle u_2, v_1 \rangle}{\langle v_1, v_1 \rangle} v_1, \\ u_3 &= v_3 - \frac{\langle u_3, v_1 \rangle}{\langle v_1, v_1 \rangle} v_1 - \frac{\langle u_3, v_2 \rangle}{\langle v_2, v_2 \rangle} v_2, \\ & \vdots \\ u_n &= v_n - \sum_{i = 1}^{n - 1} \frac{\langle u_n, v_i \rangle}{\langle v_i, v_i \rangle} . \end{aligned} \] Then \(\{ u_1, \ldots, u_n \}\) forms an orthogonal basis of \(V\), and the calculation of the \(u_i\) is known as Gramâ€“Schmidt orthogonalization.
