Let \(\mathcal{E}\) be a vector bundle over a curve \(C\). There exists a unique filtration by subbundles \[ 0 = \mathcal{E}_0 \subset \mathcal{E}_1 \subset \cdots \subset \mathcal{E}_{r + 1} = \mathcal{E} \] such that \(\mathcal{E}_{i + 1}/\mathcal{E}_i\) are semistable vector bundles with slope \(\mu_i\), where \(\mu_1 > \mu_2 > \cdots > \mu_r\). It is constructed as follows. If \(\mathcal{E}\) is not semistable, take \(0 \ne \mathcal{F} \subsetneq \mathcal{E}\) with maximal slope and among those with maximal rank. Then \(\mathcal{E}_1 := \mathcal{F}\) is semistable by construction, and repeat the process with \(\mathcal{E}/\mathcal{F}\).
