Let \((M, \omega, G, \varphi, \mu)\) be a Hamiltonian manifold, and assume \(G\) acts freely and properly on the zero-level set of the moment map \(\mu^{-1}(0) \subset M\). The Marsden–Weinstein theorem states that the orbit space \[ M \sslash G := \mu^{-1}(0) / G \] is a symplectic manifold in a canonical way, called the Marsden–Weinstein quotient of \(M\) by \(G\). That is, there exists a unique symplectic form \(\overline{\omega}\) on \(M \sslash G\) which pulls back to the restriction of \(\omega\) on \(\mu^{-1}(0) / G\).
Furthermore, \[ \dim(M \sslash G) = \dim M - 2 \dim G . \]
