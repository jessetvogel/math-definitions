Let \(X\) be a topological space. The Stone–Čech compactification of \(X\) is a compact Hausdorff space \(\beta(X)\) together with a continuous map \(i : X \to \beta(X)\), such that for any continuous map \(f : X \to K\), with \(K\) a compact Hausdorff space, there exists a unique \(g : \beta(X) \to K\) such that \(f = g \circ i\).
