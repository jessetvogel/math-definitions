Let \(x \in \RR\) be a real number. The continued fraction of \(x\) is a (possibly finite) sequence \[ [a_0; a_1, a_2, \ldots] \] given by \(a_i = \lfloor x_i \rfloor\) with \(x_0 = x\) and \(x_{i + 1} = \frac{1}{x_i - a_i}\). The sequence stops if \(x_i = a_i\), which happens precisely if \(x\) is rational.
The continued fraction of \(x\) is also commonly denoted as \[ x = a_0 + \frac{1}{a_1 + \frac{1}{a_2 + \frac{1}{a_3 + \cdots }}} . \]
