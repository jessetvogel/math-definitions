A group \(G\) is solvable if there exist subgroups \(H_0, H_1, \ldots, H_r\) of \(G\) such that \[ G = H_0 \supset H_1 \supset \cdots \supset H_r = \{ 1 \} \] where \(H_{i + 1}\) is normal in \(H_i\) and \(H_i/H_{i + 1}\) is abelian.
Equivalently, \(G\) is solvable if its derived series terminates in the trivial subgroup. This is because the quotient \(H_i/H_{i + 1}\) is abelian if and only if \(H_{i + 1}\) contains the commutator subgroup \([H_i, H_i]\).
