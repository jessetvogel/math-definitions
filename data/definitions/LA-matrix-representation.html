Let \(V\) and \(W\) be finite-dimensional vector spaces, and \(T : V \to W\) a linear transformation. If \((v_1, v_2, \ldots, v_n)\) and \((w_1, w_2, \ldots, w_m)\) are (ordered) bases of \(V\) and \(W\), respectively, then the matrix representation of \(T\) is the \(m \times n\) matrix \[ A = \begin{pmatrix} a_{11} & a_{21} & \cdots & a_{n1} \\ a_{21} & a_{22} & \cdots & a_{2n} \\ \vdots & \vdots & \ddots & \vdots \\ a_{m1} & a_{m2} & \cdots & a_{mn} \end{pmatrix} \] such that \(i\)-th component of \(T(v_j)\) (w.r.t. the basis for \(W\)) is \(a_{ij}\).
