Let \(X\) be a pointed \(n\)-connected CW-complex. The natural map \(X \to \Omega (\Sigma X)\), coming from the unit of the adjunction \(\Sigma \dashv \Omega\) between the loop space \(\Omega\) and suspension \(\Sigma\), induces a morphism \[ \pi_k(X) \to \pi_k(\Omega(\Sigma X)) \isom \pi_{k + 1}(\Sigma X) . \] The Freudenthal suspension theorem states that this map is an isomorphism for \(k \le 2n\) and surjective for \(k = 2n + 1\).
