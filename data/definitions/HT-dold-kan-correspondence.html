The Dold–Kan correspondence is an equivalence between the category of simplicial abelian groups and (nonnegatively graded) chain complexes of abelian groups, \[ \textbf{Ab}_\Delta \simeq \text{Ch}(\ZZ)_{\ge 0} . \] To any simplicial abelian group \(A_\bdot\), one assigns the (normalized) Moore complex \[ N(A_\bdot)_n := \bigcap_{i = 0}^{n - 1} \ker d_{n, i} \quad \text{ with differential } \quad \partial_n = d_{n, n}, \] where \(d_{n, i} : A_n \to A_{n - 1}\) are the face maps of \(A_\bdot\).
Inversely, to any chain complex \(C_\bdot\) one assigns the simplicial abelian group \[ \sigma(C_\bdot)_n := \bigoplus_{[n] \twoheadrightarrow [k]} C_k \] and the face and degeneracy maps are given by (todo).
The statement can be generalized to any abelian category \(\mathcal{A}\): there is an equivalence \[ \mathcal{A}_\Delta \simeq \text{Ch}(\mathcal{A})_{\ge 0} . \]
