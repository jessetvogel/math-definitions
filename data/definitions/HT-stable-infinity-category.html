An \(\infty\)-category \(\mathcal{C}\) is stable if
  • there exists a zero object \(0\) in \(\mathcal{C}\),
  • every morphism \(f : X \to Y\) in \(\mathcal{C}\) has a fiber and cofiber, that is, a pullback and pushout diagram
  • Every diagram in \(\mathcal{C}\) of the form is a pullback diagram if and only if it is a pushout diagram.
