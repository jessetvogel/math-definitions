Let \(M\) be a smooth manifold. A Finsler metric on \(M\) is a smooth function \(F : TM \to \RR_{\ge 0}\) on the tangent bundle of \(M\) such that for all \(x \in M\),
  • (triangle inequality) \(F(v + w) \le F(v) + F(w)\) for all and \(v, w \in T_x M\),
  • (positive homogeneity) \(F(\lambda v) = \lambda F(v)\) for all \(v \in T_x M\) and \(\lambda \ge 0\),
  • (positive definite) \(F(v) > 0\) for all non-zero \(v \in T_x M\).
The pair \((M, F)\) is called a Finsler manifold.
A Finsler metric is reversible if in addition \(F(v) = F(-v)\) for all \(x \in M\) and \(v \in T_x M\).
