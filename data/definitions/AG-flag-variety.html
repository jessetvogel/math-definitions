A flag in a finite-dimensional vector space \(V\) over a field \(k\) is a strictly increasing sequence of subspaces \[ 0 = V_0 \subsetneq V_1 \subsetneq \cdots \subsetneq V_m = V . \] A flag is complete if \(m = \dim V\) and \(\dim_k V_i = i\) for all \(i\), otherwise it is partial. The signature of the flag is the sequence \((d_1, \ldots, d_m)\) where \(d_i = \dim_k V_i\).
Given a signature, the set of all flags with such signature is called the flag variety of that signature. It naturally has the structure of a projective variety.
