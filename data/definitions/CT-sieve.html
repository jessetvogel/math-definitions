Let \(\mathcal{C}\) be a category, and \(C\) an object of \(\mathcal{C}\). A sieve on \(C\) is a set of morphisms \(S = \{ f : C' \to C \}\) such that if \(f : C' \to C\) is in \(S\) and \(g : C'' \to C'\) is arbitrary, then \(f \circ g\) is in \(S\).
Equivalently, a sieve on \(C\) is a subpresheaf of \(y_C\), the Yoneda embedding.
