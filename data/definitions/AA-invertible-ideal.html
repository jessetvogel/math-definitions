Let \(R\) be a domain and \(K\) its field of fractions. An \(R\)-submodule \(M\) of \(K\) is an invertible ideal if there exists a submodule \(N\) of \(K\) such that \(MN = R\). This module \(N\) is then unique and equal to \[ N = (R : M) := \{ x \in K : xM \subset R \} . \]
The invertible ideals form a group with respect to multiplication, whose unit element is \(R = (1)\).
