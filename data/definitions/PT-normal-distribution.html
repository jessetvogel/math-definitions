The normal distribution with parameters \(\mu\) and \(\sigma^2 > 0\) is the probability distribution \(\mathcal{N}(\mu, \sigma^2)\) on \(\RR\) with PDF \[ f(x) = \frac{1}{\sqrt{2 \pi \sigma^2}} \exp( -(x - \mu)^2 / 2 \sigma^2) \] for \(x \in \RR\).
The standard normal distribution is the normal distribution with \(\mu = 0\) and \(\sigma^2 = 1\).
