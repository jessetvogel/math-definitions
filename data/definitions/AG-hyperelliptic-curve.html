A hyperelliptic curve is an algebraic curve over a field \(k\) of genus \(g > 1\), given by an equation of the form \[ y^2 + h y = f \] where \(f \in k[x]\) is a separable polynomial of degree \(n = 2g + 1\) or \(n = 2g + 2\), and \(h \in k[x]\) is a polynomial of degree less than \(g + 2\). When \(\operatorname{char}(k) \ne 2\), one can take \(h = 0\) by a substitution of variables.
