Let \(G\) be a finite group, and \(\rho_i, \rho_j\) two irreducible representations of \(G\). The tensor product \(\rho_i \otimes \rho_j\) is generally reducible, and can be written as \[ \rho_i \otimes \rho_j = A \left( \bigoplus_k a^k_{ij} \rho_k \right) A^{-1} , \] for some invertible \(A\). The coefficients \(a^k_{ij}\) are called the Clebsch–Gordan series. The matrix entries of \(A\) in some standard basis are called the Clebsch–Gordan coefficients.
