If \(G\) is a finite group, and \(\rho_i, \rho_j\) two irreducible representations, the tensor product \(\rho_i \otimes \rho_j\) is generally reducible, and can be written as \[ \rho_i \otimes \rho_j = A \left( \bigoplus_k a^k_{ij} \rho_k \right) A^{-1} , \] for some invertible \(A\) and coefficients \(a^k_{ij}\), called the Clebsch–Gordan series. The matrix elements of \(A\) in some standard basis are called the Clebsch–Gordan coefficients.
