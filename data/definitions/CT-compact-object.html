Let \(\mathcal{C}\) be a locally small category in which filtered colimits exist. An object \(X\) of \(\mathcal{C}\) is compact if \[ \Hom_\mathcal{C}(X, -) : \mathcal{C} \to \textbf{Set} \] preserves filtered colimits, that is, for every filtered category \(\mathcal{I}\) and functor \(F : \mathcal{I} \to \mathcal{C}\), the natural morphism \[ \varinjlim_{I \in \mathcal{I}} \Hom_\mathcal{C}(X, F(I)) \xrightarrow{\sim} \Hom_\mathcal{C}(X, \varinjlim_{I \in \mathcal{I}} F(I)) \] is an isomorphism.
