Let \(G\) be a reductive algebraic group acting on a normal projective variety. In the context of GIT quotients, there is no canonical choice of \(G\)-linearized invertible sheaf \(\mathcal{L}\) on \(X\), and different choices yield different quotients. The GIT fan describes these variations.
Let \(\textup{NS}^G(X)\) be the \(G\)-Néron–Severi group, \(\textup{NS}^G(X)_\RR = \textup{NS}^G(X) \otimes \RR\), and \(\textup{NS}^G(X)_\RR^+\) the convex cone generated by ample \(G\)-linearized invertible sheaves, and \[ C^G(X) = \{ \ell \in \textup{NS}^G(X)_\RR^+ \mid X^\textup{ss}(\ell) \ne \varnothing \} \subset \textup{NS}^G(X)_\RR^+ \] the cone of effective points.
Then, for all \(\ell \in C^G(X)\), the subsets \[ C(\ell) = \{ \ell' \in C^G(X) \mid X^\textup{ss}(\ell') = X^\textup{ss}(\ell) \} \] are closed convex rational polyhedral cones in \(C^G(X)\), and together they form a fan covering \(C^G(X)\). This fan is called the GIT fan for the action of \(G\) on \(X\).
