A representation \(\rho : G \to \text{GL}(V)\) is reducible if there exists a non-zero proper invariant subspace \(W \subset V\), that is, \(\rho(g) w \in W\) for all \(w \in W\) and \(g \in G\). If no such subspace exists, the representation is irreducible. If \(\rho\) can be written as a direct sum \(\rho_1 \oplus \rho_2\), then it is called completely reducible, or semisimple.
