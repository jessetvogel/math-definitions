Let \(C_\bdot\) and \(D_\bdot\) be chain complexes of \(k\)-modules, for some commutative ring \(k\). If for all \(n \in \ZZ\), the \(k\)-modules \(C_n\), \(D_n\), \(\ker(d_n^C)\) and \(\ker(d_n^D)\) are all flat, there is a short exact sequence \[ 0 \to \bigoplus_{p + q = n} H_p(C_\bdot) \otimes H_q(D_\bdot) \to H_n(C_\bdot \otimes D_\bdot) \to \bigoplus_{p + q = n - 1} \textup{Tor}_1^k(H_p(C_\bdot), H_q(D_\bdot)) \to 0 , \] known as the KÃ¼nneth formula. In particular, if \(H_n(C_\bdot)\) or \(H_n(D_\bdot)\) are projective \(k\)-modules for all \(n\), there is an isomorphism \[ \bigoplus_{p + q = n} H_p(C_\bdot) \otimes H_q(D_\bdot) \isom H_n(C_\bdot \otimes D_\bdot) . \]
