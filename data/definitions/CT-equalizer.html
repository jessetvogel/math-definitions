Let \(f, g : X \to Y\) be morphisms in a category \(\mathcal{C}\). An equalizer of the pair \(f, g\) is an object \(E\) of \(\mathcal{C}\) together with a morphism \(e : E \to X\) such that \(fe = ge\), and such that for any other \(e' : E' \to X\) with \(f \circ e' = g \circ e'\) there exists a unique morphism \(h : E' \to E\) such that \(e' = e \circ h\). Dually, a coequalizer of the pair \(f, g\) is an object \(Q\) of \(\mathcal{C}\) together with a morphism \(q : Y \to Q\) such that \(q \circ f = q \circ g\), and such that for any other \(q' : Y \to Q'\) with \(q' \circ f = q' \circ g\) there exists a unique morphism \(h : Q \to Q'\) such that \(q' = h \circ q\).
