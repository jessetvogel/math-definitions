Let \(X\) be a topological space, \(i : Z \to X\) the inclusion of a closed subset, and \(j : U \to X\) the inclusion of the complement \(U = X \setminus Z\). For any sheaf of abelian groups \(\mathcal{F}\) on \(U\), the extension by zero of \(\mathcal{F}\) is the sheaf \(j_! \mathcal{F}\) on \(X\) associated to the presheaf \[ V \mapsto \left\{ \begin{array}{cl} \mathcal{F}(V) & \textup{ if } V \subset U , \\ 0 & \textup{ otherwise} . \end{array} \right. \] For any sheaf of abelian groups \(\mathcal{F}\) on \(Z\), the direct image \(i_* \mathcal{F}\) is called the extension by zero of \(\mathcal{F}\).
