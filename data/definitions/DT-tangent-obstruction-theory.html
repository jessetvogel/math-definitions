Let \(D\) be a deformation functor. A small extension of local Artin \(k\)-algebras is an extension \(0 \to M \to B \to A \to 0\) such that \(\mathfrak{m}_B M = 0\). A tangent-obstruction theory for \(D\) consists of finite-dimensional \(k\)-vector spaces \(T_1\) (called the tangent space) and \(T_2\) (called the obstruction space) and sequences \[ T_1 \otimes_k M \to D(B) \to D(A) \xrightarrow{\textup{ob}} T_2 \otimes_k M \] functorial in small extensions \(0 \to M \to B \to A \to 0\), such that
  • \(\textup{ob}(a) = 0\) if and only if \(a\) lifts to \(D(A)\), for all \(a \in D(A)\),
  • if \(a \in D(A)\) lifts to \(D(B)\), then \(T_1 \otimes M\) acts transitively on the set of lifts,
  • if \(A = k\), then the action is free and transitive.
Removing the condition of finite-dimensionality, one obtains a generalized tangent-obstruction theory.
