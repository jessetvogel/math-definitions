A tangent-obstruction theory for a deformation functor \(D\) consists of finite-dimensional \(k\)-vector spaces \(T_1\) (called the tangent space) and \(T_2\) (called the obstruction space) such that
  • for all small extensions \(0 \to M \to B \to A \to 0\), there exists an exact sequence of sets \[ T_1 \otimes_k M \to D(B) \to D(A) \xrightarrow{\textup{ob}} T_2 \otimes_k M , \]
  • when \(A = k\) the sequence becomes \[ 0 \to T_1 \otimes_k M \to D(B) \to D(k) \xrightarrow{\textup{ob}} T_2 \otimes_k M , \]
  • and these sequences are functorial in small extensions.

One obtains a generalized tangent-obstruction theory by removing the condition of finite-dimensionality.
