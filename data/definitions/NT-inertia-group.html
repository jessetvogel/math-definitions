Let \(K\) be a field with valuation \(\phi\), and let \(L/K\) be a finite Galois extension with valuation \(\psi\) extending \(\phi\). The inertia group of \(\psi\) in \(L/K\) is \[ \begin{aligned} I_\psi &= \{ \sigma \in \operatorname{Gal}(L/K) \mid \psi(\sigma(x) - x) < 1 \textup{ for all } x \in L \} \\ &= \ker (\operatorname{Gal}(L/K) \to \operatorname{Aut}(A_L / \mathfrak{p}_L)) , \end{aligned} \] where \(A_L = \{ x \in L \mid \psi(x) \le 0 \}\) denotes the valuation ring of \(L\), and \(\mathfrak{p}_L = \{ x \in L \mid \psi(x) < 0 \}\) its maximal ideal, with uniformizer \(\pi_L \in \mathfrak{p}_L\).
The inertia field of \(\psi\) in \(L/K\) is the corresponding invariant field \[ T_\psi = \{ x \in L \mid \sigma(x) = x \textup{ for all } \sigma \in I_\psi \} . \]
