Let \(M, N\) be model categories. A Quillen adjunction is an adjunction \[ F : M \to N, \qquad G : N \to M, \qquad F \dashv G , \] such that \(F\) preserves cofibrations and \(G\) preserves fibrations.
In particular, a Quillen adjunction descends to an adjunction on the homotopy categories \[ \mathbb{L} F : \textup{Ho}(M) \to \textup{Ho}(N), \qquad \mathbb{R} G : \textup{Ho}(N) \to \textup{Ho}(M), \qquad \mathbb{L} F \dashv \mathbb{R} G . \]
