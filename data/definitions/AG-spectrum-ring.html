Let \(R\) be a commutative ring. The spectrum of \(R\) is the locally ringed space \((X, \mathcal{O}_X)\) defined as follows.
  • The topological space \(X\) is the set of prime ideals of \(R\), whose closed sets are given precisely by the sets \(V(I) = \{ \textup{prime ideals } \mathfrak{p} \textup{ with } \mathfrak{p} \supset I \}\) for all ideals \(I\) of \(R\).
  • The sheaf of rings \(\mathcal{O}_X\) is given as follows. For each open set \(U \subset X\), \(\mathcal{O}_X(U)\) is the set of functions \(s : U \to \sqcup_{\mathfrak{p} \in U} R_\mathfrak{p}\) with \(s(\mathfrak{p}) \in R_\mathfrak{p}\) for each \(\mathfrak{p} \in U\), such that \(s\) is locally a quotient of elements of \(R\). This means that for each \(\mathfrak{p} \in U\), there is a neighborhood \(V \subset U\) of \(\mathfrak{p}\), and elements \(a, f \in R\) such that for each \(\mathfrak{q} \in V\), \(f \not\in \mathfrak{q}\) and \(s(\mathfrak{q}) = a/f\) in \(A_\mathfrak{q}\). The sets \(\mathcal{O}_X(U)\) are indeed rings.
The spectrum of \(R\) is denoted \(\Spec R\).
