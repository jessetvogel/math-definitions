Let \(A\) be Huber ring. A subset \(S \subset A\) is bounded if for every neighborhood \(U\) of \(0\) in \(A\) there exists a neighborhood \(V\) of \(0\) with \(\{ vs : v \in V, s \in S \} \subset U\). Denote by \(A^\circ\) the subset of elements \(a \in A\) for which \(\{ a \in A \mid \{ a^n : n \ge 1 \}\) is bounded.
A Huber pair is a pair \((A, A^+)\) where \(A\) is a Huber ring and \(A^+ \subset A^\circ\) is an open subring of \(A\) that is integrally closed in \(A\).
A morphism of Huber pairs \((A, A^+) \to (B, B^+)\) is a continuous ring morphism \(f : A \to B\) such that \(f(A^+) \subset B^+\).
