Let \(X_1, \ldots, X_n\) be a random sample from a population \(F_\theta\) depending on some parameter \(\theta\). A statistic \(T(X_1, \ldots, X_n) : \Omega \to (E, \mathcal{E})\) is sufficient for \(\theta\) if, for any \(B \in \mathcal{E}\), the conditional probability distribution of \((X_1, \ldots, X_n)\) given \(B\) is independent of \(\theta\).
A sufficient statistic \(T(X_1, \ldots, X_n)\) for \(\theta\) is minimal sufficient if, for any other sufficient statistic \(T'(X_1, \ldots, X_n)\) for \(\theta\), the statistic \(T(X_1, \ldots, X_n)\) can be expressed as a function of \(T'(X_1, \ldots, X_n)\).
