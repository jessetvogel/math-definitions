The nerve of a category \(\mathcal{C}\) is the simplicial set \(\text{N}_\bdot(\mathcal{C})\), where \(\text{N}_n(\mathcal{C})\) is the set of functors from \([n] = \{ 0, 1, \ldots, n \}\) (viewed as category: a unique morphism from \(i\) to \(j\) iff \(i \le j\)) to \(\mathcal{C}\). Indeed, for any non-decreasing map \(\alpha : [m] \to [n]\), precomposition with \(\alpha\) gives a map \(\text{N}_n(\mathcal{C}) \to \text{N}_m(\mathcal{C})\).
It can be shown that the nerve functor \[ \text{N}_\bdot : \textbf{Cat} \to \textbf{Set}_\Delta \] is fully faithful.
