Let \(\mathcal{A}\) be an abelian category with enough injectives. The Yoneda product is the pairing between Ext groups \[ \text{Ext}_\mathcal{A}^m(B, C) \otimes \text{Ext}_\mathcal{A}^n(A, B) \to \text{Ext}_\mathcal{A}^{m + n}(A, C) \] induced by \(\Hom_\mathcal{A}(B, C) \otimes \Hom_\mathcal{A}(A, B) \to \Hom_\mathcal{A}(A, C)\), \(f \otimes g \mapsto f \circ g\).
In terms of extensions \[ \xi : 0 \to B \to E_0 \to \cdots \to E_{n - 1} \to A \to 0 \] \[ \eta : 0 \to C \to F_0 \to \cdots \to F_{m - 1} \to B \to 0 \] the Yoneda cup product is given by \[ \xi \smile \eta : 0 \to C \to F_0 \to \cdots \to F_{m - 1} \to E_0 \to \cdots \to E_{n - 1} \to A \to 0 \] in \(\text{Ext}_\mathcal{A}^{m + n}(A, C)\).
