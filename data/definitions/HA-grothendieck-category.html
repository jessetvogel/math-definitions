A Grothendieck category is an abelian category \(\mathcal{A}\) such that
  • arbitrary direct sums exist in \(\mathcal{A}\),
  • direct limits of short exact sequences in \(\mathcal{A}\) are again short exact,
  • \(\mathcal{A}\) has a generator, that is, there exists an object \(G\) in \(\mathcal{A}\) such that the functor \(\Hom_\mathcal{A}(G, -) : \mathcal{A} \to \textbf{Set}\) is faithful.
