Let \(F : \mathcal{A} \to \mathcal{B}\) be an additive functor between abelian categories. An object \(A\) of \(\mathcal{A}\) is called acyclic with respect to \(F\), or \(F\)-acyclic, if \[ \text{R}^iF(A) = 0 \quad \text{for all } i > 0, \] where \(\text{R}^i F\) are the right derived functors of \(F\).
