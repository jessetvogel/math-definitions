Let \(S\) be a scheme in \(\textbf{Sch}_\textup{fppf}\). An algebraic space over \(S\) is a presheaf \[ F : (\textbf{Sch}/S)_\textup{fppf}^\textup{op} \to \textbf{Set} \] such that
  • \(F\) is a sheaf,
  • the diagonal \(\Delta : F \to F \times F\) is representable, that is, for every \(T \in (\textbf{Sch}/S)_\textup{fppf}\) and \(\xi \in (F \times F)(T) = F(T) \times F(T)\) the fiber product \(T \times_{(F \times F)} F\) is representable by a scheme,
  • there exists a scheme \(X \in (\textbf{Sch}/S)_\textup{fppf}\) and a representable map \(X \to F\) which is surjective and étale. That is, for every \(T \in (\textbf{Sch}/S)_\textup{fppf}\) and \(\xi \in F(T)\) the map \(X \times_F T \to T\) (of schemes) is surjective and étale.
