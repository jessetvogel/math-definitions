Given a partition \(\lambda = (\lambda_1, \lambda_2, \ldots, \lambda_k)\) of a non-negative integer \(n\), a Young diagram of shape \(\lambda\) is a collection of \(n\) boxes, arranged in left-aligned rows, such that row \(i\) has \(\lambda_i\) boxes. For example, a Young diagram of shape \((5, 3, 2)\) is given by: The arm length of a box \(s\), denoted \(a_\lambda(s)\), is the number of boxes to the right of \(s\). The leg length of \(s\), denoted \(l_\lambda(s)\), is the number of boxes below \(s\). The hook length \(s\) is \(a_\lambda(s) + l_\lambda(s) + 1\).
A Young tableau on a Young diagram is a numbering of the boxes by the integers \(1, 2, \ldots, n\).
