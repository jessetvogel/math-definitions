A topological space is a set \(X\) together with family \(\mathcal{T}\) of subsets of \(X\) satisfying
  • \(X, \varnothing \in \mathcal{T}\),
  • the intersection of any two sets in \(\mathcal{T}\) is in \(\mathcal{T}\),
  • the union of any collection of sets in \(\mathcal{T}\) is in \(\mathcal{T}\).

The family \(\mathcal{T}\) is called a topology for \(X\), and the members of \(\mathcal{T}\) are referred to as open sets. A subset \(V \subset X\) is called closed if its complement \(X \backslash V\) is open.
