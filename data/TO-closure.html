Let \(X\) be a topological space, and \(A \subset X\) a subset. A point \(x \in X\) is a point of closure of \(A\) if \(U \cap A \ne \varnothing\) for any open subset \(U \subset X\) with \(x \in U\). The closure \(\overline{A}\) of \(A\) is the set of points of closure of \(A\).
Equivalently, \(\overline{A}\) is the smallest closed subset of \(X\) containing \(A\).
