A group is a set \(G\), together with an element \(e \in G\) and a multiplication map \(G \times G \to G\), which we write as \((x, y) \mapsto x \cdot y\), satisfying
  • (associativity) for all \(x, y, z \in G\) we have \((x \cdot y) \cdot z = x \cdot (y \cdot z)\),
  • (unit element) for all \(x \in G\) we have \(e \cdot x = x = x \cdot e\),
  • (inverses) for all \(x \in G\) there exists a \(y \in G\) such that \(x \cdot y = e = y \cdot x\). The element \(y\) is called the inverse of \(x\), and is also denoted as \(x^{-1}\).
We say that a group is commutative or abelian if \(x \cdot y = y \cdot x\) for all \(x, y \in G\).
