Let \(f : X \to Y\) be a map of topological spaces, and let \(\mathcal{G}\) be a sheaf on \(Y\). The inverse image sheaf \(f^ {-1}\mathcal{G}\) on \(X\) is defined as the sheaf associated to the presheaf given by \(U \mapsto \lim_{V \supset f(U)} \mathcal{G}(V)\) for any open set \(U \subset X\).
This construction yields the inverse image functor \[ f^{-1} : \textup{Sh}(Y) \to \textup{Sh}(X) . \] It is the left adjoint of the direct image functor \(f_*\).
