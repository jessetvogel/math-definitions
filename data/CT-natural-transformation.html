A natural transformation between two functors \(F, G : \mathcal{C} \to \mathcal{D}\) consists of a collection of morphisms \(\mu_X : F(X) \to G(X)\) for each object \(C\) in \(\mathcal{C}\), such that for each \(f : X \to Y\) the diagram \[ \begin{tikzcd} F(X) \arrow{r}{\mu_X} \arrow[swap]{d}{F(f)} & G(X) \arrow{d}{G(f)} \\ F(Y) \arrow[swap]{r}{\mu_Y} & G(Y) \end{tikzcd} \] commutes. We denote this by \(\mu : F \Rightarrow G\).
