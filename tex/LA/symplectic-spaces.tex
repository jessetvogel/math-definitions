\begin{topic}{symplectic-form}{symplectic form}
    Let $V$ be a finite-dimensional, real \tref{vector-space}{vector space}. A \textbf{symplectic form} is a skew-symmetric non-degenerate bilinear map $\omega : V \times V \to \RR$.
    
    Such a vector space $V$ with a symplectic form $\omega$ is called a \textbf{symplectic vector space}.
\end{topic}
